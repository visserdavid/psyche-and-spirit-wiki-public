# Archetypal Wheel

**Summary**: Myss's practical tool for Sacred Contract self-discovery — a twelve-house wheel (adapted from astrology's horoscope) onto which twelve personal archetypes are cast, revealing the pattern of life purpose, challenges, and soul agreements.

**Sources**:
- [Myss, C. (2001). *Sacred contracts: Awakening your divine potential*. Harmony Books.](../sources/sacred-contracts.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

---

## Overview

The Archetypal Wheel, which Myss also calls the "Chart of Origin," is the central practical tool of *Sacred Contracts*. It is a twelve-part wheel modelled on the astrological horoscope, where twelve numbered "houses" each govern a domain of life. The process involves:

1. Selecting twelve personal archetypes (four fixed survival archetypes + eight companions chosen through self-inquiry).
2. Randomly casting them onto the twelve houses.
3. Interpreting the archetype-house pairings as a map of the Sacred Contract.

## The Twelve Houses

Each house corresponds to a domain of life, drawn from astrological tradition but reframed in Myss's spiritual terms:

| House | Domain |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Ego and personality; persona presented to the world |
| 2 | Life values; relationship to money and resources |
| 3 | Self-expression; communication; siblings |
| 4 | Home; the foundation; the unconscious |
| 5 | Creativity; good fortune; the erotic |
| 6 | Occupation and health |
| 7 | Marriage and intimate partnerships |
| 8 | Other people's resources; inheritance; death and transformation |
| 9 | Spirituality; travel; higher education |
| 10 | Highest potential; life's greatest challenges |
| 11 | Relationship to the world at large |
| 12 | The unconscious; hidden strengths and weaknesses |

## Choosing the Twelve Archetypes

The four [survival archetypes](four-survival-archetypes.md) (Child, Victim, Prostitute, Saboteur) are always present. The eight personal companions are chosen through a structured self-inquiry process:

- Reviewing a "Gallery of Archetypes" (over 70 patterns in the book's appendix) for resonance.
- Asking historical questions (where has this pattern appeared in my life?), personal questions (what relationships connect to this?), and spiritual questions (what is this archetype teaching me?).
- Interviewing each candidate archetype through journalling.
- Eliminating archetypes by resonance until eight remain.

Myss emphasises that archetypes should be chosen by felt recognition — the sense of "yes, this is me" — rather than by aspiration or wish.

## Casting the Wheel

Once twelve archetypes are chosen, they are distributed randomly across the twelve houses — either by writing each on a card and drawing them, or by any other method that removes conscious control from the placement. The randomness is considered intentional: it reflects the soul's guidance rather than the ego's preference.

## Interpreting the Wheel

Each archetype-house pairing is read as a statement about the Sacred Contract in that life domain. For example:

- The Saboteur in House 6 (occupation and health) suggests that self-undermining patterns are most active in work and the body, and that the guardian function of the Saboteur (discernment) is especially needed there.
- The Prostitute in House 2 (life values/money) signals a recurring challenge around compromising integrity for financial security — and an opportunity to develop deep faith.

Interpretation draws on all three dimensions of the [Three Column Model](symbolic-sight.md): the material, the psychological, and the sacred.

## Case Studies (from the book)

**Maeve** (astronomer): The Wizard archetype in her house of people and resources confirmed her sense that astronomy and astrology together formed her path; the Saboteur in her house of self-expression warned against derailing her professional reputation by revealing unconventional beliefs too early.

**Byron** (actor): The Child archetype in house of home revealed how childhood molestation had formed his identity; the Warrior in his house of occupation confirmed that acting was a form of justice work for his community.

**Mickey Magic**: Former drug smuggler turned children's magician — his Magician archetype's path from shadow (drug trafficking trickery) to light (healing through wonder) was mapped across multiple house pairings.

## The Healing Chart

Myss describes a variant application — the "healing chart" — in which archetypes are cast specifically to gain insight into a health challenge. The archetype-house pairings are then interpreted in relation to the illness or body system involved, asking what soul-level pattern the health challenge may be expressing. This approach extends the core argument of [biography is biology](biography-is-biology.md) into archetypal language.

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

- The wheel extends and structures the seven-chakra model of [Anatomy of the Spirit](../sources/anatomy-of-the-spirit.md), adding a twelfth-house container for the same energy anatomy insights.
- It is explicitly adapted from astrology's horoscope structure, though Myss does not claim astrological causation — the twelve-house framework is used as a map of life domains, not as a claim about planetary influence.
- The concept of placing a self-portrait in a structured wheel to understand life purpose has antecedents in Jungian individuation (the mandala as psychic map) and in Indigenous medicine wheel traditions.

## Critique

**Internal consistency**: The random casting is intended to remove ego preferences, but the prior step of selecting archetypes is highly subjective. Critics note that most people will choose archetypes they find flattering or that confirm existing self-narratives.

**Astrological structure**: Adapting the twelve-house framework borrows the structure of a system Myss does not otherwise endorse, which some find philosophically inconsistent. The houses' meanings shift somewhat from traditional astrology without clear justification.

**Interpretation is open-ended**: The pairing of any archetype with any house can be interpreted in multiple, sometimes contradictory ways. The method lacks the structure to make interpretations falsifiable or to distinguish insight from projection.

**Note**: Mainstream psychology does not validate the archetypal wheel as a diagnostic or therapeutic instrument. Its value, where it is found, lies in its function as a structured reflective framework — a way of seeing life patterns that can prompt genuine self-inquiry and narrative re-authoring. These are recognised as valuable in narrative therapy and depth psychology, even without accepting the metaphysical premises.
