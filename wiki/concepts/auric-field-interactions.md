# Auric Field Interactions in Relationships

**Summary**: Brennan's mapping of how Human Energy Fields interact in relationships — through three distinct mechanisms (harmonic induction, bioplasmic streamers, and cords of light) that reveal the relational dynamics hidden beneath social surfaces; developed in *Light Emerging* (1993) as the energetic anatomy of human connection.

**Sources**:
- [Brennan, B. A. (1993). *Light emerging: The journey of personal healing*. Bantam Books.](../sources/light-emerging.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-24

## Overview

Chapter 14 of *[Light Emerging](../sources/light-emerging.md)* describes what [Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) calls "one of the privileges of High Sense Perception" — the ability to observe the living, moving field interactions that occur whenever people are in close proximity. These interactions are not metaphors; Brennan describes them as directly observable phenomena at the fourth level (relational/astral) of the [Human Energy Field](seven-layer-auric-field.md).

The fourth level carries the quality of relationship — the I-Thou connection, love, resentment, envy, attraction, and all the nuances of human bonding. It is the bridge between the three lower (personal/self-referenced) and three higher (spiritual/transpersonal) levels of the auric field. When two people interact, their fourth-level fields meet, merge, and exchange energy in ways that directly affect both people's health and the quality of their relationship.

> "The interplay of plasma reveals a great deal about relationships that we never suspected when we could see the field interactions in detail. These displays reveal how connected we are in ways that no theories have yet concluded."
> — Brennan (1993), Chapter 14

## Three Types of Auric Field Interactions

### Type 1: Harmonic Induction

**What it is**: One person's field pulsation rate induces a change in another person's field pulsation rate — directly influencing the frequency at which the other's field vibrates.

**Mechanism**: The auric field pulsates at characteristic frequencies that shift depending on the person's state, the layer of the field, and their habitual energetic tone. When two people are in proximity, their fields interact like tuning forks: the stronger or more coherent field tends to pull the weaker or less coherent field toward its frequency. This is called **field coupling**.

**Examples**:
- *Sitting with a teacher or guru*: Disciples who sit in range of an experienced meditator's field report subjective experiences of uplifted consciousness, peace, or expanded awareness. Brennan explains this as induction — the teacher's highly charged, high-frequency field induces a shift in the student's field toward higher frequencies.
- *Intimate relationships*: Couples' fields entrain to each other over time. A highly charged partner can lift a depleted partner's field; conversely, a chronically depressed partner can pull down a more energised partner's field. People unconsciously select partners whose field range complements their own — either matching it (comfort) or offsetting it (stimulation/challenge).
- *Healing*: The healer's field, brought into the state of healing through alignment, grounding, and expanded consciousness, induces shifts in the patient's field through induction. This is the mechanism by which hands-on healing can alter field states before any specific technique is applied.
- *Robert Beck's research*: Brennan cites physicist Robert Beck's finding that healers' brain waves synchronise with the Schumann resonance of the earth (7.8–8 Hz) during healing, and that their patients' brain waves then synchronise with the healer's. This is field coupling at the measurable brain-wave level.

**Health implications**: Chronic exposure to a field in states of fear, anger, or depletion through induction can compromise one's own field health. This is the energetic basis for the observation that being in consistently negative relational environments is itself a health risk.

### Type 2: Bioplasmic Streamers

**What it is**: Visible streams of fourth-level bioplasma that flow between people's fields during interaction, carrying the emotional quality and content of the interaction.

**Mechanism**: The fourth-level field is composed of coloured bioplasma — a flowing, luminous substance visible through HSP. When people interact, streams of this plasma reach out from one person's field and touch the other's. The colours and qualities of these streams directly reflect the emotional content of the interaction:

| Emotional quality | Plasma appearance |
|---|---|
| Love | Rose-coloured, warm, flowing |
| Passion/eros | Orange-red, wave-like with peaks |
| Anger | Red, pointed, penetrating |
| Envy | Greenish, sticky |
| Grief | Grey-blue, heavy, slow |
| Joy | Gold-yellow, sparkling |
| Fear | Whitish, contracted |

**Burnout streamers**: A specific pattern Brennan identifies in overstretched caregivers (therapists, nurses, teachers): the plasma streamers from clients/students pull energy from the caregiver's field rather than exchanging energy. Over time, this produces energetic depletion — burnout — that is visible as a draining of the fourth-level field. The solution is not to stop caring but to engage from a charged, centred field that can give without depleting.

**Objects**: Bioplasma attaches to objects. Loved objects carry the plasma imprint of the relationship; some objects carry field distortions from their history. This is the energetic basis for the folk practice of "blessing" objects or concerns about the energetic history of second-hand items.

**In healing sessions**: When the healer works with the client's relational issues, bioplasmic streamers from past relationships may be visible in the client's field. A healing may involve clearing old plasma patterns from departed relationships that are still actively influencing the client's field.

### Type 3: Cords of Light

**What it is**: Threads of energy connecting specific chakras between people in close relationships — particularly between parents and children, and romantic partners. First described briefly in *Hands of Light* and developed in more detail in *Light Emerging*.

**Development**: Cords form and develop through specific stages of childhood development, connecting child to parent chakra-by-chakra as the child develops each chakra's functions. By adulthood, a person has a complex web of cords connecting them to their primary attachment figures. The cords carry the specific emotional and energetic qualities of those early relationships.

**In adult relationships**: When an adult forms a new close relationship, cords develop that echo the childhood patterns. The cord quality between adult partners reflects the quality of the original parent-child cords — until conscious healing work changes the template.

**Cord connections in the five character structures**: Chapter 15 of *Light Emerging* maps in detail how each of the five [character structures](character-structures-auric.md) creates distinctive cord and interaction patterns with other people. For example, the schizoid structure tends to form cords that avoid close contact, maintaining a protective distance even when the cord is present; the oral structure forms cords with strong pull energy at the lower chakras.

## Character Structure Defensive Patterns in Auric Interaction

Chapter 15 extends the five-character-structure framework from *Hands of Light* with a detailed mapping of each structure's defensive auric reactions when triggered in relationship. Each structure has five defensive responses:

| Response | Description |
|---|---|
| **Push** | Sending energy forcefully outward to ward off approach |
| **Pull** | Drawing the other's energy inward (demanding, clinging) |
| **Stop** | Blocking energy flow between self and other |
| **Allow/Deny** | Permitting some energy exchange while simultaneously blocking it |
| **Withdrawal** | Retreating energetically (field contracts, connections thin) |

These five defensive reactions are mapped for each of the five character structures, producing 25 distinct defensive patterns that are visible in the field and correspond to specific relationship behaviours.

The healing response for each structure and each defensive pattern is also described — showing how the field changes when a defense is dissolved and the person allows genuine contact.

**Couple dynamics**: Two people in relationship who both carry strong defenses will produce compound patterns (Figures 15-37 to 15-39 in the book): the fields of both people show their respective defenses interacting, creating escalating patterns of mutual activation. The most destructive pattern is two people in escalating defensive interaction; the most healing is synchronistic communion — where both fields align and merge in genuine mutual openness.

## The Fourth Level as Bridge

Brennan emphasises the unique significance of the fourth level (relational, astral) among the seven auric levels:

> "The fourth level of the auric field is the bridge between the physical and spiritual worlds... The bridge is made of relationships. Relationships are what we are becoming."

The lower three levels (1-3) carry personal experience — body, emotion, thought. The upper three levels (5-7) carry spiritual experience — will, ecstasy, mind. The fourth level connects these two worlds, and human relationships are the arena in which this bridge is built or broken.

Health at the fourth level requires: giving and receiving love in diverse forms (friendship, family, romantic partnership, community); the capacity for genuine I-Thou contact (not merger, not isolation, but authentic meeting); and the willingness to allow one's field to be affected by others' fields (which requires trusting the process of field coupling).

## Scientific Context

**Empirical support for field interactions**: Brennan cites Dr. Valerie Hunt's UCLA research correlating aura-reader colour observations with electrically measured frequency ranges on the body surface. The existence of some form of field surrounding the body with measurable properties is supported; the specific structures (streamers, cords) are HSP descriptions not yet measurable with instruments.

**Field coupling research**: Robert Beck's Schumann wave research provides partial support for the harmonic induction model at the brain-wave level — the entrainment of healer and patient brain waves is a measurable phenomenon.

**Note**: The specific entities described (bioplasmic streamers, cords of light as distinct from bioplasma) are HSP observations without independent verification. Mainstream relationship psychology does not use energy-field concepts, though the patterns described (induction/entrainment, projective identification, relational reenactment) have analogs in psychological literature.
