# Authentic Power

**Summary**: Gary Zukav's distinction between "external power" (control over circumstances, money, status, or other people) and "authentic power" (alignment of the personality with the soul) — a framework he argues is replacing external power as humanity evolves into a multisensory species, and a likely direct influence on Caroline Myss's later Three Ages of Power model.

**Sources**:
- [Zukav, G. (1989). *The seat of the soul*. Simon & Schuster.](../sources/seat-of-the-soul.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-07-09

---

## The Claim

In [*The Seat of the Soul*](../sources/seat-of-the-soul.md), [Gary Zukav](../thinkers/gary-zukav.md) argues that most human institutions — militaries, economies, family hierarchies, competitive business — are built on "external power": the capacity to control one's environment or other people. External power can be won, lost, bought, stolen, or inherited, and Zukav treats it as inherently fear-based, since it requires perceiving others as threats or resources rather than as fellow souls.

Against this he sets "authentic power": the state in which the personality's thoughts, emotions, and actions are aligned with the soul, rather than driven by fear, competition, or the need for external validation. Authentic power cannot be won or lost in the external-power sense — it is built incrementally through countless individual choices to respond to fear, temptation, and difficulty with compassion, honesty, and forgiveness rather than defensiveness or domination. Zukav frames the shift from pursuing external power to cultivating authentic power as the central task of humanity's ongoing transition from a [five-sensory to a multisensory species](multisensory-perception.md).

## Relationship to Myss's Three Ages of Power

Zukav's acknowledgments in *The Seat of the Soul* credit [Caroline Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md)'s contributions as woven into the book, seven years before her own *[Anatomy of the Spirit](../sources/anatomy-of-the-spirit.md)* and eight years before *[Why People Don't Heal](../sources/why-people-dont-heal.md)* — where she develops the [Three Ages of Power](three-ages-of-power.md) (Tribal, Individual, Symbolic). Zukav's external/authentic distinction maps loosely onto Myss's progression: Tribal and much of Individual power correspond to what Zukav calls external power (group survival, personal ego-driven ambition), while Symbolic power — the capacity to read events archetypally and act from spiritual insight — is close to what Zukav calls authentic power. Because Myss was in documented dialogue with this material before writing her own framework, the relationship between the two models is better described as a probable direct influence than a coincidental parallel, though no single passage can be traced as a direct source for a specific Myss claim.

Zukav's authentic power also parallels [personal power](personal-power.md) (Myss's third-chakra concept of inner authority independent of external approval), though Zukav's version operates at the level of the whole personality-soul relationship rather than a single chakra.

## Relationship to Jungian Individuation

Zukav's alignment of "personality" with "soul" is a populist, non-clinical echo of [Jung](../thinkers/carl-jung.md)'s [individuation](individuation.md) — the lifelong process of aligning the ego with the [Self](the-self.md). Both frameworks describe a developmental movement from an externally-oriented, defense-driven mode of being toward one governed by an inner, more encompassing center. The register differs sharply: Jung's individuation is derived from decades of clinical case material and alchemical/mythological comparison; Zukav's authentic power is asserted from personal conviction with no comparable evidentiary apparatus. See also [Core Star](core-star.md), Brennan's structurally similar (though independently developed) concept of an innermost divine essence underlying the energy field.

## Critique

**No operational definition**: "Alignment of personality with soul" is not defined in terms that could be measured, tested, or falsified — there is no criterion by which an outside observer (or the person themselves) could distinguish authentic power from a sincerely-felt but mistaken sense of spiritual alignment. This is a general limitation of the wiki's more devotional sources, but it is unusually pronounced here since the entire book is organized around this single, undefined construct.

**Political and social omissions**: by locating power primarily in individual intention and inner alignment, Zukav's framework has little to say about structural or systemic power (economic inequality, institutional racism, colonial history) except as an aggregate of individual fear-based choices — a reduction that mainstream sociology and political theory would reject as inadequate to explain durable, structural forms of domination.

**Contrast with clinical accounts of power and evil**: Zukav's claim that external power is essentially fear-driven and dissolves once authentic power is achieved sits in tension with [Fromm](../thinkers/erich-fromm.md)'s and [Peck](../thinkers/m-scott-peck.md)'s more clinically grounded accounts of [human evil](human-evil.md), which describe malignant narcissism and character pathology as durable structures that do not simply yield to increased self-awareness or "alignment."
