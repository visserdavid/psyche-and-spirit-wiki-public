# Cellular Energy Bank

**Summary**: Myss's metaphor for the finite daily supply of life force available to each person — approximately one hundred "units" — which can be invested in healing and growth or drained by holding onto wounds, grudges, and unresolved past events, with depletion contributing directly to illness.

**Sources**:
- [Myss, C. (1997). *Why people don't heal and how they can*. Harmony Books.](../sources/why-people-dont-heal.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

---

## Overview

In [*Why People Don't Heal*](../sources/why-people-dont-heal.md), [Caroline Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) introduces the Cellular Energy Bank as a practical model for understanding how biographical and emotional patterns translate into physical health outcomes. The metaphor makes concrete the abstract claim of [biography is biology](biography-is-biology.md): where energy goes, biology follows.

## The Model

Myss proposes that each person has a finite daily allowance of life force — she uses the figure of one hundred "dollars" as a currency metaphor. This energy is required for:

- **Maintenance**: keeping all bodily systems running — organs, immune function, hormonal regulation
- **Growth and creativity**: relationships, work, spiritual development
- **Healing**: repair and regeneration in response to illness or injury

If the full allowance is directed toward maintenance and growth, health is sustained and healing proceeds efficiently. But when significant portions are diverted into holding the past — reliving grievances, maintaining victim narratives, sustaining unresolved fear or anger — the remaining energy is insufficient for biological maintenance. The deficit accumulates in cell tissue.

> "Debt becomes cell tissue." — Myss's formulation

## How Energy Is Lost

Myss identifies several major categories of energy drain:

1. **Woundology** — Using wounds as social currency and identity requires constant energy to sustain the wound-narrative and defend the associated relationships. See [Woundology](woundology.md).
2. **Holding the past** — Investing psychic energy in replaying events, rehearsing resentments, or mourning what cannot return.
3. **Fear and anticipatory anxiety** — Projecting energy into possible futures rather than the present moment.
4. **Negative belief patterns** — The five [Five Myths of Healing](five-myths-of-healing.md) each represent a pattern of energy investment that does not return.
5. **Broken or dishonest relationships** — Energy invested in maintaining false roles, protecting lies, or avoiding necessary confrontation.

Myss notes that during acute trauma or crisis, the body borrows energy from future reserves — an evolutionary protection mechanism. But this loan has a time limit: if the trauma is not processed and the borrowed energy not returned, the deficit becomes chronic and illness-promoting.

## Spirit Retrieval as the Remedy

The direct remedy for the cellular energy debt is [Spirit Retrieval](spirit-retrieval.md): the practice of actively reclaiming energy from past investments. Myss teaches this as a deliberate act of will and intention — calling energy back from the events, relationships, and stories that hold it — and redirecting it to the present and to healing.

Forgiveness is identified as the primary mechanism of energy retrieval: not as moral virtue but as energetic pragmatism. Releasing resentment frees the energy previously required to sustain it.

## Relationship to the Chakra System

The cellular energy bank is implicitly mapped onto the [Chakra System](chakra-system.md). Each chakra manages a domain of life — tribe, relationship, self-esteem, love, will, mind, spirit — and energy drains characteristic of each domain affect the organs associated with that chakra:

- **Lower chakra drains** (tribal loyalty, money, security fears) affect immune function, lower back, colon, legs
- **Middle chakra drains** (self-esteem, forgiveness, will) affect digestion, heart, lungs
- **Upper chakra drains** (meaning, faith, purpose) affect depression, exhaustion, neurological function

The seventh chakra (crown) contains what Myss calls the **grace bank account**: energy generated by prayer, devotion, and acts of compassion. This is a positive reserve that can supplement the daily allowance in times of crisis.

## Relationship to Shealy's Work

[Norman Shealy](../thinkers/norman-shealy.md)'s research on DHEA as a marker of life energy (see [DHEA and Vitality](dhea-and-vitality.md)) provides a potential biochemical correlate for the cellular energy bank concept: DHEA levels decrease under chronic stress, correlating with lowered resilience and illness vulnerability. While Shealy does not use Myss's metaphor, the two frameworks converge on the claim that life force is finite and depleted by sustained stress.

## Critique

**From mainstream medicine**: The cellular energy bank is a metaphor, not a measurable biological quantity. While stress hormones, neuroimmunological markers, and DHEA levels do respond to chronic emotional states, there is no scientific evidence for a unified "life force" that flows according to emotional investment in the way Myss describes. The metaphor usefully organises the clinical observation that chronic stress impairs health; it should not be read as literal physiology.

**Internal critique**: The model can slide into victim-blaming: if illness results from energy drain caused by holding the past, then the patient is implicitly responsible for their own illness by failing to let go. Myss explicitly rejects this reading (addressing it in her treatment of the [Five Myths](five-myths-of-healing.md)), but the model's logic makes the charge difficult to entirely deflect.

**On forgiveness**: The claim that forgiveness is energetically necessary for healing implies that people who cannot forgive (due to ongoing abuse, unacknowledged injustice, or trauma) are blocking their own recovery. This risks placing undue moral and therapeutic burden on survivors.
