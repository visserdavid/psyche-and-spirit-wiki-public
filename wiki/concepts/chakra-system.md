# Chakra System

**Summary**: The Hindu framework describing seven energy centres running from the base of the spine to the crown of the head, each governing specific life themes, bodily systems, and stages of spiritual development — the structural backbone of Myss's integrated model.

## Overview

The chakra system (from Sanskrit *chakra*, meaning "wheel" or "circle") originates in Hindu spiritual philosophy and describes seven primary energy centres aligned along the spine. Each chakra is understood as a vortex of energy that governs a specific dimension of human experience — from the most physical and earth-bound (first chakra) to the most subtle and transcendent (seventh chakra).

[Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) adopts the chakra system as one of three convergent traditions she uses to map the human energy system, alongside the [Christian sacraments](christian-sacraments.md) and the [Kabbalistic sefirot](sefirot.md). In her synthesis, all three systems describe the same seven-level architecture of spiritual development, which she calls the [Seven Sacred Truths](seven-sacred-truths.md).

## The Seven Chakras

### Chakra 1: Muladhara (Root)
- **Location**: Base of spine / coccyx
- **Body connection**: Spine, rectum, legs, bones, feet, immune system
- **Life theme**: Tribal identity, survival, belonging, loyalty, honour
- **Sacred Truth**: "All is One" — we are interconnected; what we do to others we do to ourselves
- **Shadow fears**: Abandonment by the group, loss of physical security
- **Aligned with**: Sacrament of Baptism; sefirah Shekhinah
- See [Tribal Power](tribal-power.md)

### Chakra 2: Svadisthana (Sacral)
- **Location**: Lower abdomen, between navel and genitals
- **Body connection**: Sexual organs, large intestine, lower vertebrae, pelvis, appendix, bladder
- **Life theme**: Relationships, creativity, sexuality, money, power dynamics
- **Sacred Truth**: "Honour One Another"
- **Shadow fears**: Loss of control, betrayal, rape, financial ruin, abandonment
- **Aligned with**: Sacrament of Communion; sefirah Yesod

### Chakra 3: Manipura (Solar Plexus)
- **Location**: Solar plexus
- **Body connection**: Stomach, pancreas, adrenals, small intestine, gallbladder, liver, middle spine
- **Life theme**: Personal power, self-esteem, individuation, courage
- **Sacred Truth**: "Honour Oneself"
- **Shadow fears**: Rejection, criticism, failure, appearing inadequate
- **Aligned with**: Sacrament of Confirmation; sefirot Hod and Nezah
- See [Personal Power](personal-power.md)

### Chakra 4: Anahata (Heart)
- **Location**: Centre of chest
- **Body connection**: Heart, lungs, shoulders, arms, breasts, diaphragm
- **Life theme**: Love, forgiveness, compassion, the bridge between outer and inner power
- **Sacred Truth**: "Love Is Divine Power"
- **Shadow fears**: Loneliness, emotional loss, betrayal, inability to forgive
- **Aligned with**: Sacrament of Marriage; sefirah Tif'eret

### Chakra 5: Vishuddha (Throat)
- **Location**: Throat
- **Body connection**: Throat, thyroid, trachea, neck vertebrae, mouth, teeth, gums, oesophagus
- **Life theme**: Will, self-expression, truth, the alignment of personal will with Divine will
- **Sacred Truth**: "Surrender Personal Will to Divine Will"
- **Shadow fears**: Loss of control, inability to express oneself, being silenced
- **Aligned with**: Sacrament of Confession; sefirot Hesed and Gevurah

### Chakra 6: Ajna (Third Eye)
- **Location**: Centre of forehead
- **Body connection**: Brain, nervous system, eyes, ears, nose, pineal gland
- **Life theme**: Mind, intuition, insight, the perception of truth beyond appearances
- **Sacred Truth**: "Seek Only Truth"
- **Shadow fears**: Closed-mindedness, inability to learn from experience, refusal to see
- **Aligned with**: Sacrament of Ordination; sefirot Binah and Hokhmah

### Chakra 7: Sahasrara (Crown)
- **Location**: Top of head
- **Body connection**: Nervous system, muscular system, skin, energetic connection to the Divine
- **Life theme**: Spiritual consciousness, the present moment, connection to the sacred
- **Sacred Truth**: "Live in the Present Moment"
- **Shadow fears**: Spiritual crisis, loss of faith, dark night of the soul
- **Aligned with**: Sacrament of Extreme Unction; sefirah Keter

### Chakra 8: The Seat of the Sacred Contract
In *Sacred Contracts* (2001), Myss introduces an eighth chakra located approximately one arm's length above the crown of the head. This chakra is the seat of the soul's agreement with the Divine — the energetic location where the [Sacred Contract](sacred-contracts.md) is held. It is where individuality merges with universal purpose.

The eight [archetypes](archetypes.md) that comprise a person's personal companions reside in the eighth chakra. They descend through the system, taking on the specific expressions of each lower chakra as they do. The eighth chakra is thus the "organising principle" of the entire energy field — the reason the lower seven chakras hold the specific patterns they do.

- **Life theme**: Sacred Contract, soul mission, connection to the Divine plan
- **Key function**: Holds the twelve archetypal companions; connects the personal soul to universal energy
- **Associated concept**: [Divine Potential](divine-potential.md)

## Three Zones of Power

Myss organises the seven chakras into three developmental zones:

- **External power** (chakras 1–3): The ego's arena — tribal identity, relational dynamics, personal will. Energy here is largely directed by fear and by negotiation with the outer world.
- **The heart bridge** (chakra 4): The transition between outer and inner power; the centre of love, forgiveness, and emotional intelligence.
- **Internal power** (chakras 5–7): The domain of spirit — Divine will, truth, intuition, surrender, and transcendence. Power here is rooted in spiritual authority rather than external circumstance.

## Illness and the Chakras

Myss's clinical principle is that all illness, when analysed energetically, can be traced to one or more chakras. The location of an illness in the body reflects which energy centre's concerns — which life lessons and power issues — have been most depleted or violated. For example:
- Heart disease and breast cancer arise in the region of chakra 4 — issues of love, self-love, forgiveness, and nurturing.
- Pancreatic and stomach cancers arise in the region of chakra 3 — issues of self-esteem, responsibility, and entrapment.
- Reproductive problems arise in the region of chakra 2 — issues of sexuality, creativity, and relationships.

## Kundalini

Myss refers to **kundalini** — the dormant spiritual energy coiled at the base of the spine (chakra 1) which, when activated through spiritual practice or life crisis, rises through the chakras and can produce states of heightened consciousness, ecstasy, or crisis. She describes kundalini as particularly relevant to women approaching menopause, when energy that has been focused in the lower chakras begins to rise and illuminate unresolved business.

## Brennan's Extension: Psychological Functions and Auric Layers

[Barbara Ann Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) in [*Hands of Light*](../sources/hands-of-light.md) (1987) provides the most detailed published account of chakra psychological functions and their relationship to the [seven-layer auric field](seven-layer-auric-field.md). Each chakra corresponds to one layer of the HEF; each has both a front aspect (relating to feeling) and a back aspect (relating to will):

| Chakra | Front aspect (feeling) | Back aspect (will) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (Root) | Physical sensation, will to live | Connection to earth, grounding |
| 2 (Sacral) | Sexuality, creativity, feeling | Desire, will to create |
| 3 (Solar plexus) | Personal power, self-respect | Will to act in the world |
| 4 (Heart) | Love, compassion | Will to love; commitment |
| 5 (Throat) | Self-expression, communication | Will to speak truth; divine will |
| 6 (Third eye — front) | Conceptualising, forming ideas | — |
| 6 (Third eye — back) | — | Implementing ideas; executive function |
| 7 (Crown) | Spiritual connection | Integration of the whole being |

Brennan also maps each chakra to an endocrine gland and nerve plexus, providing a more anatomically specific version of the illness-chakra correspondences that Myss uses clinically.

Her pendulum diagnostic technique (Chapter 10 of *Hands of Light*) makes the chakra state visible through the pendulum's movement direction and diameter — clockwise = open/functioning; counterclockwise = closed/projecting; vertical/horizontal = diverted energy; axis-shifting = active growth work in progress.

## Campbell's Comparative Psychological Reading

[Joseph Campbell](../thinkers/joseph-campbell.md)'s [*The Mythic Image*](../sources/the-mythic-image.md) (1974) — Myss's cited scholarly reference for the chakra system — provides a different interpretive layer from both Johari (traditional Tantric) and Brennan (auric field mapping). Campbell reads the seven chakras as **seven modes of motivation** — levels of consciousness at which a person's life energy is primarily invested — and demonstrates that Western art, philosophy, and mysticism encode the same seven-stage map when read through the Kundalini lens.

| Chakra | Campbell's psychological reading | Western parallel |
|--------|----------------------------------|-----------------|
| 1 Muladhara | Pure materialism; the dragon's hoard; security and survival | —  |
| 2 Svadhishthana | The Freudian level: sexuality as primary motivation | Freud's libido theory |
| 3 Manipura | The Adlerian level: power as primary motivation | Adler's will to power |
| 4 Anahata | *La Vita Nuova* — the New Life; the Wish Fulfilling Tree; the sound of *om* | Dante's Beatrice; the troubadour tradition |
| 5 Vishuddha | The razor's edge; the Gate of Great Liberation; the threshold guardians | Homer's Sirens; the mystical desert |
| 6 Ajna | *Savikalpa samadhi*: vision of the divine as Form; the conditioned rapture | Dante's Trinity vision; Blake's Job; Eckhart's encounter with God as Person |
| 7 Sahasrara | *Nirvikalpa samadhi*: dissolution of subject/object duality; the Diamond Body mandala | Dante's final vision; Eckhart's "Godhead beyond God" |

Campbell's key comparative evidence: the **caduceus** (two serpents entwined around a central staff = ida and pingala around sushumna) appears identically on Sumerian vessels (2000 BC), Indus Valley seals, Mesoamerican monuments, and in Greek Hermes/Asklepios iconography — arguing for a shared Bronze Age matrix underlying all these traditions' use of the serpent-power symbol.

Sri Ramakrishna's first-person account of Kundalini rising — describing five movement types (ant, fish, monkey, bird, serpent) as the Current ascends through the five elemental chakras — is quoted by Campbell as phenomenological evidence that the chakra map describes a real psychological process operating independently of cultural transmission.

See [Chakra Psychology (Campbell)](chakra-psychology-campbell.md) for the full comparative analysis.

## Sources and Interpretations

Myss draws on [Joseph Campbell](../thinkers/joseph-campbell.md)'s [*The Mythic Image*](../sources/the-mythic-image.md) (Princeton University Press, 1974) as an accepted academic mapping of the chakra system. Key practitioner interpretations include [Barbara Ann Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md)'s *Hands of Light* (Pleiades Books, 1987) and Rosalyn Bruyere's *Wheels of Light* (Healing Light Center, 1987). The Theosophical tradition (Leadbeater, *The Chakras*, 1974) is the primary channel through which the Hindu chakra model entered Western energy medicine.

## Johari's Traditional Hindu Framework

[Harish Johari](../thinkers/harish-johari.md)'s [*Chakras: Energy Centers of Transformation*](../sources/chakras-energy-centers.md) (originally 1987; revised 2000) provides the primary-source Hindu-Tantric perspective on the chakra system that underlies the Western energy-medicine adaptations of Myss and Brennan.

Where Myss reads the chakras as maps of illness and Brennan maps them onto the Human Energy Field for healing, Johari presents them in their original context: as the landscape through which [Kundalini](kundalini.md) energy must travel on its way to liberation (*moksha*). Each chakra is a centre of desire and consciousness that binds the soul to that level's concerns; Yoga practice aims not merely to "balance" the chakras but to pierce through each one's attachment and allow Kundalini to ascend to Sahasrara.

The traditional attribute system that Johari restores — which is largely absent from Western energy-medicine presentations — includes:
- **Bija mantras** (seed sounds): LANG/Muladhara, VANG/Svadhishthana, RANG/Manipura, YANG/Anahata, HANG/Vishuddha, AUM/Ajna
- **Yantras** (geometric diagrams): square (earth), circle/crescent (water), inverted triangle (fire), hexagram (air), crescent-in-circle (akasha), circle (Ajna)
- **Presiding deities**: Brahma (1st), Vishnu (2nd), Braddha Rudra (3rd), Ishana Rudra/Sada Shiva (4th), Panchavaktra Shiva (5th), Paramashiva (6th)
- **Shakti doorkeepers**: Dakini, Rakini, Lakini, Kakini, Shakini, Hakini
- **Carrier animals**: Elephant Airavata (1st), Crocodile makara (2nd), Ram (3rd), Musk deer (4th), White elephant (5th), Nada/sound (6th)
- **Ruling planets**: Mars (1st), Mercury (2nd), Sun (3rd), Venus (4th), Jupiter (5th), Saturn (6th), Ketu (7th)
- **Cosmic planes (lokas)**: Bhu, Bhuvar, Svarga, Maha, Jana, Tapas, Satyam Loka

Johari also describes three granthis (energetic knots) that must be pierced for Kundalini to ascend: **Brahma Granthi** (Muladhara–Manipura area: attachment to the material world), **Vishnu Granthi** (Anahata: attachment to preservation and world-service), and **Rudra Granthi** (Ajna: attachment to I-consciousness). These knots parallel the three major transitions in Myss's three zones of power, though the Tantric framing is liberation-oriented rather than health-oriented.

The **Soma Chakra** (between Ajna and Sahasrara) and the **Kameshvara Chakra** (within the Soma region) are additional subtle centres not mentioned in Western energy-medicine sources; they relate to the nectar (*soma/amrita*) that flows from the crown and can be preserved through Khechari Mudra, halting aging.

For full details see [Tantric Chakra Attributes](tantric-chakra-attributes.md).

## Joy's Contribution: Independent Clinical Discovery and Extended Mapping

[W. Brugh Joy](../thinkers/w-brugh-joy.md) provides a remarkable instance of independent rediscovery in [*Joy's Way*](../sources/joys-way.md) (1979). Working as a Los Angeles internist in 1973, Joy felt the impulse during a physical examination to pass his hand above (rather than on) a patient's body and detected a field of energy radiating from the solar plexus. He then spent three months systematically mapping energy fields he could feel at distances of two to six feet from patients' body surfaces — and only discovered the chakra system when he encountered a Tantra Yoga book in a bookstore and recognised that his clinical map matched the traditional centres exactly.

This independent clinical rediscovery adds a specific evidentiary weight to the chakra model that is distinct from either tradition (Johari), intuitive perception (Brennan, Myss), or scholarship (Campbell): an evidence base from hands-on medical observation by a board-certified physician with no prior knowledge of the system.

Joy's extended mapping goes beyond the classical seven:
- **Transpersonal point**: a ball of energy 12–24 inches above the crown, not described in traditional texts
- **Upper midchest centre**: at the junction of the manubrium and sternum
- **Spleen area centre**: on the left side, not named in traditional texts
- **Joint centres**: hip joints, knees, elbows, shoulders, hands/palms, feet — all detectable but largely absent from classical descriptions

Joy also distinguishes chakra fields from disease fields: illness produces anomalous energy radiating from the affected organ location, distinct from the chakra centres. This allows him to locate disease processes by scanning — though without blinded clinical validation. See [Body Scanning](body-scanning.md) for the full technique and evidence discussion.

Joy's central interpretive contribution is the claim that the chakra system is a **transducing system** — it converts higher-dimensional energy into forms of matter and energy on the physical plane, analogous to the way a prism splays light into its frequencies. Chakras are not merely energy centres but transformers at the interface of spirit and matter.

## Northrup's Contribution: The Lower Female Centers

[Christiane Northrup](../thinkers/christiane-northrup.md) in [*Women's Bodies, Women's Wisdom*](../sources/womens-bodies-womens-wisdom.md) (1994) applies the chakra framework specifically to women's gynecological health, generating the most clinically detailed organ-level mapping in the wiki for chakras 1–4.

Northrup's key additions:
- **Earth energy (centripetal)**: Women's bodies draw energy upward from the Earth (centripetal force), concentrating it in the second-chakra organs (uterus, ovaries). This is the energetic basis for women's relational orientation and their particular vulnerability to second-chakra disorders when relationships or creative expression are chronically suppressed.
- **The "lower female centers"**: Chakras 1–4 govern the foundational territory of women's health — tribal inheritance, relationships/creativity, self-esteem, and love/giving. The reproductive organs, pelvis, and breasts carry the accumulated history of women's social position: the first and second chakras hold the cultural programming about women's worth; the third chakra carries the suppressed permission to individuate; the fourth chakra carries the energetic cost of giving without receiving.
- **Second-chakra specifics**: The uterus as creativity centre (not merely reproductive organ); fibroids as "solidified blocks of creative energy"; endometriosis as the body's attempt to draw attention to second-chakra imbalance; ovarian cysts as third-chakra conflict around autonomy.
- **Fourth-chakra specifics**: Breast cancer arising in the region where women are programmed to give love without receiving it — the breast as the organ that feeds others; fourth-chakra depletion as the energetic substrate of breast disease.

Northrup explicitly credits the chakra-organ mapping to Myss and Shealy's *Creation of Health* (reproduced as Table 4); her contribution is the clinical specificity of its application to women's reproductive health and the case studies documenting its use.

## Critique

**From within the tradition**: The Hindu chakra system is rich, varied, and context-dependent — different schools offer different chakra counts, locations, and associations. Myss's seven-chakra model (heavily influenced by the Theosophical tradition's systematisation in the 19th–20th century) is one interpretation, not the only or most orthodox one. Even within the Tantric tradition Johari draws on, different texts (Rudrayamala Tantra, Kankalmalini Tantra, Kularnava Tantra) disagree on petal colours and deity details.

**From mainstream science**: No peer-reviewed study has detected the chakra system as a physical structure or confirmed that energy depletion in chakras precedes illness. The illness-chakra correspondences Myss describes are not supported by epidemiological data.

**Intercultural concern**: Translating the chakra system into a Western therapeutic and self-help framework risks decontextualising it from the broader system of yoga philosophy, Ayurvedic medicine, and Hindu cosmology within which it was developed.
