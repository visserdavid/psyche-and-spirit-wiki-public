# Character Structures (Auric)

**Summary**: Brennan's extension of Reich's and Lowen's five character structures into auric terms — each structure has a distinctive Human Energy Field signature, defense system, life wound, and higher-self gift, making the psychological typology visible and healable at the energetic level.

**Sources**:

## Overview

[Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) inherits the five-character-structure framework from [Wilhelm Reich](../thinkers/wilhelm-reich.md), [Alexander Lowen](../thinkers/alexander-lowen.md)'s bioenergetics, and [John Pierrakos's](../thinkers/john-pierrakos.md) Core Energetics. Each character structure is a patterned way of managing overwhelming early experience: a childhood wounding produces a defensive reorganisation of the personality, body, and — Brennan adds — the Human Energy Field.

In Brennan's extension, each character structure has:
- A **core wound** (the childhood experience that produced the defense)
- A **core fear** (the unbearable feeling being avoided)
- A **core defense** (the behavioural and energetic strategy)
- A **distinctive HEF signature** (visible to [HSP](high-sense-perception.md))
- A **life task** (the growth challenge)
- A **higher-self gift** (what is released when the life task is engaged)

These structures are not diagnostic categories in the sense of distinct boxes — most people contain elements of more than one, and the structures shade into each other. They are descriptive maps of the territory of defended selfhood.

The framework is adapted from: Reich (1950), Lowen (1958), and Pierrakos (Core Energetics monographs, 1975–1977).

## The Five Character Structures

### 1. Schizoid Structure

**Core wound**: The child's right to exist was not affirmed; there was hostility, indifference, or invasiveness toward the child's very being — sometimes from before birth.

**Core fear**: Non-existence; annihilation; the terror of being.

**Core defense**: Withdrawal — pulling inward and out of the body; minimising contact with physical reality; living in the mind or the spiritual dimension.

**Body/HEF signature**:
- Energy scattered to the extremities rather than grounded in the centre
- Field thin, fragmented, barely present at the skin's surface
- Energy leaks visible throughout the field
- Aura appears pale, disconnected; the core energy barely reaches the surface
- Head energy may be stronger than body energy
- Brain activity shows energy in the back of the skull rather than the full cortex

**Defense system**: Withdrawal; porcupine-like energy spines that repel closeness; avoidance of contact.

**Life task**: Integration — learning to inhabit the body, tolerate the physical world, and allow the self to exist in relationship.

**Higher-self gift**: When integrated, the schizoid brings extraordinary spiritual sensitivity, visionary capacity, and ability to perceive other dimensions — a gift that serves others once the terror of existence is resolved.

---

### 2. Oral Structure

**Core wound**: The child's need for nourishment — physical and emotional — was not adequately met in early infancy; there was deprivation, abandonment, or unreliable care.

**Core fear**: Abandonment; the void of emptiness; not enough.

**Core defense**: Energetic sucking — drawing nourishment from others; playing victim to elicit giving; grabbing and holding to avoid the void.

**Body/HEF signature**:
- Field depleted throughout, especially in the heart and solar plexus regions
- "Sucking arrows" visible — arrow-like projections reaching outward to draw energy from others
- Body energy concentrated in the upper half; legs may be under-energised
- Brain activity shows energy in the lateral lobes (interpersonal sensitivity) rather than centre
- Aura has a quality of need, of reaching outward

**Defense system**: Sucking arrows; victim role; provocation of others' caregiving responses.

**Life task**: Trust in the abundance of the universe — learning to give rather than grab, and to find internal sources of nourishment.

**Higher-self gift**: When the grabbing is released, the oral structure's tremendous sensitivity and capacity for connection are available for genuine caregiving. The landscape of the higher self is richness — a Stradivarius waiting to be played.

---

### 3. Psychopathic Structure (Displaced)

**Core wound**: The child experienced betrayal from the parent of the opposite sex — love given conditionally or withdrawn as a power move; the child learned that vulnerability invites exploitation.

**Core fear**: Surrender; vulnerability; defeat; being controlled or used.

**Core defense**: Control — manipulating others before they can manipulate; dominating through will, seduction, or undermining; never letting the defences down.

**Body/HEF signature**:
- Energy strongly charged in the upper body; lower body depleted
- Aura's egg shape distorted — bulging upper half, diminished lower half
- Energy "arches" extend from the head to grasp and reach over others' fields
- Will centres (back of chakras) overactivated; feeling centres underactivated
- Brain activity concentrated in frontal lobes (intellect used to serve will)
- A quality of control and ownership in the energy pattern

**Defense system**: Denial; head grasp (arching energy over others); hysteria (explosive rage when control is threatened); seduction; undermining.

**Life task**: Surrender — trusting that vulnerability does not mean defeat; finding honour, truth, and integrity as the real source of strength.

**Higher-self gift**: When surrender is embraced, the psychopathic character brings extraordinary leadership capacity, vision, and the ability to bring order and direction to complex situations — leadership from integrity rather than fear.

---

### 4. Masochistic Structure

**Core wound**: The child's assertion, expression, and movement toward independence were systematically crushed — often by a controlling, sacrificing mother who smothered with love while dominating; the child was made to feel that assertion = humiliation.

**Core fear**: Humiliation; being crushed; being put down.

**Core defense**: Holding — internalising rather than expressing; withholding as a subtle form of control; provocation of others to deliver the rage the person cannot express.

**Body/HEF signature**:
- Energy compacted and held inward; the field appears inflated and heavy
- Body is compact and muscular; energy is blocked at the throat and pelvis
- Blue tones in the body and field; dark mauve/maroon in places
- Aura egg is inflated and weighted toward the bottom; edge is thick and tense
- Brain activity shows energy in the periventricular area
- A quality of boiling inward pressure

**Defense system**: Tentacles (energetic extensions that draw others in without fully connecting); brooding (dense dark energy that creates a pull); whining and complaining (indirect assertion); passive provocation.

**Life task**: Free self-expression — learning to assert directly, to take up space, to deliver rage cleanly rather than through suffering and provocation.

**Higher-self gift**: When the masochist releases the holding, extraordinary refinement of feeling, creative taste, and capacity for nuanced human understanding are available. The landscape of the higher self is filigreed silver and gold.

---

### 5. Rigid Structure

**Core wound**: The child experienced rejection from the parent of the opposite sex in the domain of love and pleasure — love was given and then withdrawn, teaching the child that vulnerability in love leads to betrayal and pain.

**Core fear**: Vulnerability in love; betrayal; the loss of pride.

**Core defense**: Holding at the periphery — being open to everything except the deepest vulnerability; keeping feelings controlled; using pride to avoid the risk of being seen as needing.

**Body/HEF signature**:
- Energy held at the periphery of the field; the core is intact but closed
- The most integrated and balanced of the five structures in terms of energy distribution
- Feeling centres (front of chakras) closed; will centres more developed
- Aura egg is resilient and shaped, with silvery-gold tones; back chakras more active than front
- Brain activity shows strong cortical activity, particularly in the temporal lobes in people who have developed artistic or intellectual pursuits; bilateral bridges begin to form with spiritual work
- A quality of contained power and pride in the energy pattern

**Defense system**: Power display (showing strength to avoid appearing vulnerable); boundary containment (a forceful energetic wall); hysteria (sudden emotional release when containment is overwhelmed).

**Life task**: Opening the feeling centres — allowing the risk of vulnerability in love; integrating the heart with the will.

**Higher-self gift**: When the rigid character opens the heart, tremendous passion, leadership, and capacity for commitment become available. The landscape of the higher self is Icarus trying to fly to the sun, Moses leading people to the promised land.

## Auric Healing Approach by Structure

Each character structure requires a different primary emphasis in the [spectrum of healing](spectrum-of-healing.md):

- **Schizoid**: Charge the field; seal energy leaks; ground the energy downward; the person needs to feel the body is safe
- **Oral**: Charge the field; open chakras; strengthen boundaries; the person needs containment and learning to hold their own charge
- **Psychopathic**: Balance upper and lower body; open feeling centres; ground; the person needs to feel that surrender is safe
- **Masochistic**: Release compaction; open throat and pelvis; encourage expression; the person needs permission to assert
- **Rigid**: Open the front feeling centres; connect heart to lower centres; support vulnerability; the person needs spiritual confirmation of inner reality

## Defensive Auric Interactions by Character Structure (Light Emerging, 1993)

In Chapter 15 of *[Light Emerging](../sources/light-emerging.md)*, Brennan extends the character structure model to describe the specific [auric field interactions](auric-field-interactions.md) each structure generates in relationship — the energetic defenses activated when another person's field is experienced as a threat. Each structure has five characteristic defensive reactions:

| Character Structure | Push | Pull | Stop | Allow/Deny | Withdrawal |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Schizoid** | Spines repel | Faint draw inward | Disappearing (vacancy) | Allows energy in then denies it | Pulls out of body entirely |
| **Oral** | Sucking pull appears as push when frustrated | Draws energy via need | Freezes; can't move forward | Allows love in then doubts it | Collapses inward; abandons self |
| **Psychopathic** | Controls/dominates | Head grasp; arches over | Blocks with will | Allows then seizes control | Withholds self; retreats to position |
| **Masochistic** | Tentacles that hold | Brooding density draws others in | Passive resistance | Allows then withholds; self-sabotage | Implodes; turns rage inward |
| **Rigid** | Pride display | Guarded charm | Boundary wall | Allows but holds back at the deepest level | Retreats into role/persona |

These patterns operate largely unconsciously at the fourth (relational) auric layer, and are often the source of recurring relational difficulties that character-structure work alone — without auric-level healing — does not fully resolve.

## Relationship to Somatic Psychology

[Keleman's somatic distress patterns](somatic-distress-patterns.md) — the four body shapes (rigid, dense/compact, swollen, collapsed) — map loosely onto the same territory as the character structures, though from a different direction (embryonic tissue and pulsatory pattern rather than defensive psychological organisation). Both frameworks describe how biographical injury becomes encoded in the body's structural organisation, making that organisation observable and therapeutically addressable.

| Brennan (HEF) | Keleman (somatic) |
|---|---|
| Schizoid: scattered, depleted | Collapsed: shrunken, withdrawn |
| Masochistic: compacted | Dense/compact: held inward |
| Rigid: held at periphery | Rigid: over-bounded |
| Oral: depleted | Collapsed (variant): needy |

The frameworks are not equivalent — they arise from different theoretical traditions and describe somewhat different aspects of character — but they describe the same underlying phenomenon: that personality defenses become embodied.

## Critique

**Typological limitations**: The five character structures are a map, not a territory. Most people are mixtures; the types are idealised poles. Brennan acknowledges that most clients show elements of more than one structure.

**Reich's original framework**: Wilhelm Reich's orgone theory (from which the character structures derive) has not been validated by mainstream science. The character armoring concept is influential in body psychotherapy but not accepted by academic psychology or psychiatry as a diagnostic framework.

**HSP observation**: The auric signatures Brennan describes for each character structure are visible through HSP but not through instrumentation. This limits the framework to practitioners with developed HSP — or to clients' subjective recognition of the patterns.
