# Christian Sacraments

**Summary**: The seven rites of the Christian tradition (Baptism, Communion, Confirmation, Marriage, Confession, Ordination, Extreme Unction), reinterpreted by Myss as energy maps describing seven stages of spiritual empowerment — the second of three traditions in her integrated model.

**Sources**:
- [Myss, C. (1996). *Anatomy of the spirit: The seven stages of power and healing*. Harmony Books.](../sources/anatomy-of-the-spirit.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

## Overview

The seven sacraments are recognised rites of the Christian church — ceremonies that, in Roman Catholic and other traditions, invoke Divine grace and mark significant transitions in a believer's life. [Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) re-reads these sacraments not primarily as ecclesiastical ceremonies but as descriptions of seven stages of personal spiritual empowerment. In her framework, each sacrament encodes a truth about how to receive *and give* grace at a specific stage of life.

Her key interpretive move: the sacraments are not acts performed *on* you by a priest but powers meant to be enacted *by* you — capacities you develop and bestow on others as well as receive. The word "sacrament," she notes, means "invoking the power of the soul."

## The Seven Sacraments and Their Energy Meanings

### 1. Baptism — Chakra 1 (Tribal Power)
**Traditional meaning**: Initiation into the Christian community; cleansing from original sin; acceptance of family responsibility for a new life.

**Myss's energy meaning**: The adult expression of Baptism is the act of accepting your family of origin as the teachers and tribe chosen for your soul's learning — and accepting your own responsibility as a member of the human community. Healing at this level means forgiving family wounds and honouring tribal bonds, however imperfect.

**Sacred Truth**: All is One — whatever we do to others returns to us.

### 2. Communion — Chakra 2 (Partnership/Relationships)
**Traditional meaning**: Sharing the consecrated host as holy union with God and the community.

**Myss's energy meaning**: All unions — friendships, partnerships, creative collaborations — are "communions" in which we partake of another's energy and they of ours. The truth embedded here is that every relationship is a Divine design; each person we meet is sent to teach or to learn. Communion means recognising the sacred nature of human bonds.

**Sacred Truth**: Honour One Another.

### 3. Confirmation — Chakra 3 (Personal Power / Self-Esteem)
**Traditional meaning**: Affirmation of faith and personal commitment to Christian life; a coming-of-age rite administered by a bishop.

**Myss's energy meaning**: The energy act of Confirmation is the emergence of self as an empowered individual — claiming one's own identity, values, and voice, rather than living entirely under the authority of the tribe or the need for others' approval. True confirmation happens any time a person consciously commits to their own becoming.

**Sacred Truth**: Honour Oneself.

### 4. Marriage — Chakra 4 (Emotional Power / Love)
**Traditional meaning**: The sacramental blessing of a union between two people in God's presence; commitment to care for another's well-being.

**Myss's energy meaning**: Marriage is the sacrament of the heart chakra — not limited to romantic partnership, but expressing the general call to enter into relationships that require us to learn love, forgiveness, and responsibility toward another. Every deep relationship is a kind of marriage: an agreement to show up for another person's growth.

**Sacred Truth**: Love Is Divine Power.

### 5. Confession — Chakra 5 (Will / Truth-telling)
**Traditional meaning**: Acknowledgment of sin to a priest; absolution and penance.

**Myss's energy meaning**: Confession is the act of retrieving spirit from the places where misuse of will has left it. It is not primarily about guilt or punishment, but about *cleansing* — releasing the distortions in the energy system created by dishonesty, violation of others, or betrayal of one's own truth. Twelve-step programmes and psychotherapy are forms of confession. The act of confessing (acknowledging wrongdoing) liberates the spirit from the energetic burden of what it has done or left undone.

**Sacred Truth**: Surrender Personal Will to Divine Will.

### 6. Ordination — Chakra 6 (Mind / Symbolic Sight)
**Traditional meaning**: Consecration as a priest or minister; entry into a life of service to God and community.

**Myss's energy meaning**: In Myss's reading, "ordination" is the recognition that one's life work — whatever it is — is a channel for Divine energy. Every person has an "ordained" capacity: a potential to be a vessel through which grace flows into others' lives. Recognising and pursuing this capacity is the energy meaning of Ordination. It corresponds to the concept of "Right Livelihood" in Buddhism.

**Sacred Truth**: Seek Only Truth.

### 7. Extreme Unction (Last Rites / Anointing of the Sick) — Chakra 7 (Spirit)
**Traditional meaning**: Administered to the dying or seriously ill; spiritual preparation for death.

**Myss's energy meaning**: The energy capacity of Extreme Unction is not limited to dying. It is the power to bring things to closure — to complete the business of any chapter of one's life so that one's spirit is not indefinitely attached to the past. It is the capacity to release what has been, to receive guidance only in the present, and to live in the moment rather than in the archive of yesterday's pain.

**Sacred Truth**: Live in the Present Moment.

## Receiving and Giving

A distinctive element of Myss's interpretation: each sacrament is not merely *received* but *bestowed*. We do not just receive Baptism — we baptise others by honouring them as family. We do not only receive Marriage — we "marry" others in the sense of committing to their growth. This bidirectional quality aligns with the energy model: sacramental grace flows in both directions.

## Critique

**From Christian theology**: Myss's reinterpretation strips the sacraments of their ecclesial, communal, and theological content. In Catholic and Orthodox theology, the sacraments work *ex opere operato* — by the act itself, performed by ordained ministers — not through the personal spiritual work of the recipient. Myss's privatised, therapeutic reading represents a significant departure from the tradition she draws on.

**Selectivity**: Myss notes that she does not include Islamic teachings because she has not lived that tradition and could not write about it with integrity. Her framework is therefore explicitly Judeo-Christian-Hindu in scope, a limitation she acknowledges.

**Ecumenical concern**: The seven-sacrament structure is specifically Roman Catholic; Protestant traditions recognise only two (Baptism and Communion). Myss's framework thus silently privileges one strand of Christian tradition.
