# Conscientious Psychology

**Summary**: Shealy's culminating framework (2014): conscientiousness — the personality trait of being organized, disciplined, responsible, and purpose-driven — is the single most powerful predictor of health, longevity, and bliss; and it is a trait that can be developed.

**Sources**:
- [Shealy, C. N. (2014). *Living bliss: Major discoveries along the path*. Hay House.](../sources/living-bliss.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

---

## Overview

[Shealy](../thinkers/norman-shealy.md) introduces Conscientious Psychology as the framework for his final decades of research. After fifty years treating 30,000 patients suffering chronic pain and depression, he concluded that the root problem was not biochemical, structural, or even energetic — it was a deficit of conscientiousness.

Conscientiousness, as defined in mainstream personality psychology, is one of the Big Five personality traits. It includes:
- Being organized, disciplined, and detail-oriented
- Planning, persistence, and follow-through
- Taking responsibility for one's actions rather than blaming others
- Prudence and sound judgment
- Reliability and self-control

Shealy argues this trait is both the foundation of health and the prerequisite for genuine healing. Patients who lacked conscientiousness — who did not follow basic health habits, did not take responsibility for their choices, who waited for others to fix them — consistently failed to recover, regardless of the quality of treatment.

## Empirical Foundation: The Longevity Project

The primary evidence Shealy cites is the *Longevity Project* (Friedman, H. S., & Martin, L. R., 2011), the culmination of an 80-year study initiated by Dr. Lewis Terman in 1921. Terman enrolled 1,500 children (born c. 1910) and followed them — or successive teams of researchers followed them — across their entire lives.

Key findings:

**Conscientiousness is the strongest personality predictor of longevity.** More than intelligence, sociability, or physical health at baseline, childhood conscientiousness predicted how long a person lived.

**The mechanisms Friedman and Martin propose**: Conscientious individuals tend to:
- Avoid health-damaging behaviours (smoking, excessive drinking, dangerous driving)
- Sustain beneficial behaviours (exercise, diet, sleep)
- Form stable marriages and friendships
- Engage persistently with meaningful work
- Show lower rates of violent death and accidents

**Divorce is a health hazard**: Death of a parent before the age of 21 did not significantly predict early death. But parental divorce did — increasing the risk of premature death in adult children. Women who divorced their husbands but remained unmarried lived as long as continuously married women; men who divorced did not.

**Exercise in childhood does not protect in adulthood**: Activity habits must be maintained. Adults who exercised had greater longevity regardless of their childhood activity levels.

**Sociability alone is not health-protective**: Extroverted children were more likely to drink and smoke. Scientists (lower sociability) outlived lawyers, businesspeople, and salespeople.

**Catastrophizing kills**: Men who described themselves as pessimistic or worried about their health died earlier — including from accidents and violence.

## Conscientiousness and the 97% Problem

Shealy reports that in forty years of practice, 97% of patients did not consistently follow four basic health habits:
1. Maintaining healthy body weight (BMI 18–24)
2. Not smoking
3. Eating five or more servings of fruit and vegetables daily
4. Exercising at least 30 minutes per day, five days per week

Research cited: following these habits increases life expectancy by 18–20 years. Yet barely one person in thirty does all four. Shealy argues this is not ignorance — the information is universally available — but a deficit of conscientiousness.

## Conscientiousness and Oxytocin

The neurochemical basis Shealy proposes for conscientiousness is **[oxytocin](oxytocin-and-health.md)** — the bonding and nurturing hormone. His argument:

1. Adequate nurturing in the first seven years of life establishes a functioning oxytocin system.
2. A well-functioning oxytocin system produces the emotional security, self-esteem, and social attunement that underlie conscientious behaviour.
3. Oxytocin deficiency — from birth trauma, early abuse, parental divorce, or lack of nurturing — produces the deficit states (depression, ADHD, addiction, borderline personality) that preclude conscientiousness.

This means conscientiousness is not simply a character failing or a matter of willpower. It is, in part, a neurochemical condition — one that can be addressed through the oxytocin-raising therapies Shealy develops (Ring Air, Air Bliss oil).

## Developing Conscientiousness

Shealy argues that while conscientiousness has heritable and early-developmental roots, it can be grown in adulthood through:

- **Autogenic Training / Biogenics**: The 20-minute daily self-regulation practice (based on J.H. Schultz's Autogenic Training, customized by Shealy as Biogenics) calms the stress response, reduces cortisol, and retunes the brain toward parasympathetic regulation — the physiological foundation of conscientious functioning.
- **Purpose**: Identifying and committing to a purpose beyond oneself is a key component of conscientiousness in the Longevity Project data.
- **Social engagement**: Participating in community groups, helping others, and maintaining quality friendships were protective factors independent of conscientiousness per se — but they reinforce it.
- **Boosting oxytocin**: Ring Air stimulation, Air Bliss oil, and sex with a loving partner all raise oxytocin levels, providing neurochemical support for the emotional security that enables conscientiousness.

## The Ten Steps to Bliss

Shealy's concrete lifestyle protocol:
1. BMI 18–24
2. 5–7 servings fruits/vegetables daily
3. 30 min exercise, 5 days/week
4. No smoking
5. 7+ hours sleep per night
6. 20 min Autogenic Training/Biogenics daily
7. Maintain positive attitude (see [Attitude and Health](attitude-and-health.md))
8. Develop and maintain social network
9. Sex with a loving partner
10. Quality supplements (B complex, magnesium lotion, astaxanthin, vitamin D3, vitamin C, lithium orotate)

## Caroline Myss's Foreword: Conscientiousness as Personal Responsibility

Myss writes in the foreword that she has taught for thirty years that people need to take responsibility for their lives. She had always found, however, that the word "responsibility" sounded threatening or accusatory. Shealy's use of "conscientiousness" reframes the same idea as a learnable skill and a positive trait rather than a moral demand. She identifies the *conscientious* person's key characteristics (organized, purposeful, committed) and argues that Shealy has done what she could not: provided a practical handbook for becoming that person.

## Relation to Other Frameworks in the Wiki

**Myss's [biography is biology](biography-is-biology.md)**: Conscientiousness is the trait that allows a person to act on the awareness that biographical patterns affect health. Without it, a person can know their story shapes their biology and still not change.

**Myss's [woundology](woundology.md)**: Woundology — using wounds as social currency and identity — is the anti-conscientious pattern. The conscientious person does not build their identity on what has harmed them; they take responsibility for moving forward regardless.

**Hillman's [calling](calling.md)**: Hillman argues that calling is character and fate — what one is compelled to become. Shealy's conscientiousness is what allows the calling to be fulfilled rather than dissipated. Without conscientiousness, even a strong daimon cannot organize a life around its image.

**Jung's [individuation](individuation.md)**: Individuation requires sustained engagement over a lifetime with the material of one's own psyche. Conscientiousness is the ego-structure that enables this engagement without collapse or flight.

## Critique

**Empirical support**: The Longevity Project is one of the strongest longitudinal datasets in personality research; its findings on conscientiousness are well-replicated in the broader personality-health literature. Conscientiousness is genuinely predictive of health outcomes in mainstream research.

**Oversimplification**: Shealy's framing can imply that illness is the result of insufficient conscientiousness — which risks pathologizing the sick (blaming patients for their conditions). The Longevity Project itself showed that genetics accounts for 25% of health outcomes; structural factors (poverty, environmental toxins, adverse childhood experiences) account for much more. Conscientiousness is a powerful factor, not the sole factor.

**The oxytocin bridge**: The connection between oxytocin deficiency and the full range of major mental health disorders (autism, ADHD, depression, schizophrenia, OCD, borderline personality) is a strong and potentially reductive claim. While oxytocin is implicated in social bonding and some of these conditions, the neuroscience is more complex than a simple deficiency model suggests.

**Note**: Mainstream personality psychology and epidemiology support the core claim: conscientiousness is robustly predictive of health and longevity. The specific neurochemical mechanisms Shealy proposes (oxytocin as the root of conscientiousness; Ring Air as the clinical fix) go beyond what the research currently supports.
