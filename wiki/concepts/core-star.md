# Core Star

**Summary**: The deepest dimension of the human energy system in Brennan's four-level model — the individuated divine essence, the "spark of God" at the centre of each person; the source from which all creative energy originates and flows upward through the haric level and auric field into physical life.

**Sources**:
- [Brennan, B. A. (1993). *Light emerging: The journey of personal healing*. Bantam Books.](../sources/light-emerging.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-24

---

## Overview

The core star is the fourth and innermost dimension in [Brennan's](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) [four-dimensional model of being](four-dimensions-of-being.md). It underlies the [haric level](hara-level.md), which underlies the seven-layer auric field, which underlies the physical body. In Brennan's language, the core star is not a concept or a metaphor — it is a directly observable energy phenomenon visible through advanced [High Sense Perception (HSP)](high-sense-perception.md).

> "The core star is the source of life. It is the place in the centre of your universe. It is a localised individuality. It has been with you since the beginning of time. It has no limitations of time or space or belief. This is the aspect of the divine in you."
> — Brennan (1993), Chapter 18

The name reflects what is seen through HSP: a star — a point of brilliant, multi-coloured light radiating outward from a single location in the body — at the centre of the person.

## Location and Appearance

**Location**: Approximately 1.5 inches below the navel, on the midline of the body. This is the same vertical axis as the [Tan Tien](hara-level.md) (which is the haric-level structure at the same region). The core star is at a deeper dimension than the tan tien — further "inward" rather than further "downward."

**Appearance through HSP**: A star-like point of brilliant light radiating in all colours simultaneously. When the core star is fully active and unobstructed, it expands to fill the entire room and beyond — seen as a field of stars in which each person in a group appears as their own star radiating into and interpenetrating the others.

**Temporal nature**: The core star is described as existing "before the beginning of time" — it is not created by the biographical experience of this or any lifetime. No experience, no matter how devastating, has changed or tainted the core star. This is what makes it the source of genuine (not manufactured) healing: it has never been damaged, only obscured.

## The Creative Flow

Brennan describes a directional flow from core to world that constitutes the creative process and, when unobstructed, produces health, aliveness, and fulfilment:

**Core Star** → **Haric Level** → **Auric Field** → **Physical Body** → **World**

1. Creative energy originates as a pulse of light from the core star
2. It rises through the haric level, where it takes on the quality of intention and purpose (particularly through the soul seat's longing and the tan tien's grounding force)
3. It enters the auric field (personality level), where it takes on the qualities of emotion, thought, and relational expression
4. It manifests in the physical body as vitality, cellular health, and action in the world

When this flow is blocked at any level, the result is:
- Auric-level blockage: emotional repression, mental patterns, relational dysfunction → illness
- Haric blockage: misaligned purpose, meaninglessness, loss of will → chronic illness that auric work cannot resolve
- Core star blockage: disconnection from divine essence → creative drought, existential emptiness, severe illness

## Core Star Dysfunction

Brennan distinguishes several forms of core star blockage:

**Shrouding**: The core star becomes encased in a dense, resilient energy substance. Its light cannot reach the haric or auric levels. The person experiences a complete disconnection from creativity, vitality, and the sense of their own divinity. They may function on the surface but feel "empty inside" in a way that no external achievement can fill.

**Haric blocking of core energy**: If the hara line is severely distorted, the core star's energy cannot flow upward even when the star itself is relatively unobstructed. This requires haric-level healing as the prerequisite for core star activation.

**Cultural/biographical disconnection**: The normal process of childhood socialisation (the development of the [mask self](mask-self-lower-self-higher-self.md)) produces progressive disconnection from the core. The mask self's fundamental project is maintaining the appearance of adequacy in the face of felt inadequacy — and this requires suppressing the authentic impulses that come from the core. Over a lifetime, the mask's operations can reduce core awareness to near zero.

## Core Star Healing

Brennan describes core star healing as the culminating act of a complete healing session, performed after auric-level work and hara alignment:

> "The healer expands the client's core star essence. The client fills with intention at the haric level, the personality field, and then every cell of the body."

**Process**: After clearing the auric field (chelation, layer-specific work) and aligning the hara line (haric healing), the healer works to:
1. Clear any shrouding from around the core star
2. Facilitate the upwelling of core essence through the hara line
3. Allow the core light to flood all seven auric layers simultaneously
4. Extend the core star's light to fill the physical body at the cellular level — the "light that shines from the skin"

**Observable effects**: Brennan describes watching patients' fields transform as core essence upwells: all seven auric layers become simultaneously coherent, bright, and aligned. Physical healing is often faster and more stable when core star work has been done, because the physical body is being nourished from the deepest possible level.

**Sarah's knee**: One of the most detailed clinical examples in the book involves a student named Sarah whose cruciate ligament was torn in a ski accident. Brennan demonstrates auric repair followed by hara alignment and core star upwelling in a class setting. Sarah's ligament healed without surgery; subsequent orthopedist examination confirmed healing. Brennan acknowledges this as a case study, not a controlled trial.

## The Core Star Meditation

Channelled from Heyoan (Brennan's spirit guide) and presented in Chapter 18, this is the self-healing technique for connecting with the core star. The sequence:

**Step 1: Hara alignment**
- Bring awareness to the tan tien (1.5 inches below the navel)
- Feel the heat, the connection to the earth's core
- Bring awareness to the soul seat (chest area) — the light and the longing
- Bring awareness to the ID point (above the head) — the connection to the divine
- Feel the laser line connecting all three, straightening the spine

**Step 2: Core star access**
- From hara alignment, drop awareness to the core star (just below the navel, deeper than the tan tien)
- Allow the light to expand
- Stay with the experience: "I am light. I have always been light."

**Step 3: Expansion and integration**
- Allow the core light to expand through the haric line, the auric field, and into the physical body
- Let it extend to fill the room and beyond
- Affirm the creative intention arising from the core

Brennan suggests five minutes daily of core star meditation produces profound life changes over time.

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

**Myss's Divine Potential**: Myss's concept of [Divine Potential](divine-potential.md) — the soul's inherent capacity for its highest expression — maps closely to what Brennan calls the core star. Both identify a divine dimension that is not developed through effort but uncovered through the removal of obstacles.

**Hillman's acorn**: Hillman's [acorn theory](acorn-theory.md) locates the soul's unique image at a pre-biographical level — corresponding to what Brennan calls the haric level (the calling in the soul seat) and possibly the core star (the source of the soul's character). Where Hillman emphasises the individuality of the acorn, Brennan emphasises its divinity.

**Jung's Self**: Jung's archetype of the [Self](the-self.md) — the centre and totality of the psyche, typically symbolised by the mandala — corresponds to the core star at a psychological level. Both are understood as pre-existing, inexhaustible sources of meaning that ego-consciousness can approach but never fully contain.

**Myss's Sacred Contracts (eighth chakra)**: Myss locates the soul's Sacred Contract in an eighth chakra above the body. This corresponds structurally to Brennan's ID point at the haric level — the point above the head that carries the soul's reason for incarnating.

## Critique

**Empirical basis**: The core star is described entirely through HSP. Brennan acknowledges that no instruments currently measure this level. This places the core star in the same epistemic position as other HSP observations — phenomenologically vivid but scientifically unverified.

**Theological claims**: Calling the core star the "individualised God" or "a localised spark of the divine" makes metaphysical claims that are theology rather than science. Within Brennan's M-3 framework (consciousness is fundamental reality), these claims are coherent; from an M-1 perspective, they are unfalsifiable.

**Healing claims**: The specific claim that core star upwelling can heal physical tissue (ligaments, tumours, etc.) is presented through case studies without controls. The cases are striking; the mechanism is not established.

**Note**: No peer-reviewed scientific research has verified the existence of the core star as a distinct energy structure. The concept belongs to the tradition of energy medicine and can be evaluated as a clinical and phenomenological map, not as established anatomy.

**Zukav's soul as a parallel, not clearly derivative construct**: [Gary Zukav](../thinkers/gary-zukav.md)'s [*The Seat of the Soul*](../sources/seat-of-the-soul.md) (1989) describes the soul as a "force field of being" underlying the personality in terms structurally similar to the core star — both frame an innermost, non-physical, individuated divine essence as the source of authentic selfhood. Brennan's [*Hands of Light*](../sources/hands-of-light.md) (1987) predates Zukav's book, while her fuller elaboration of the core star in *Light Emerging* (1993) postdates it, so the direction of influence (if any) between the two authors is unclear; they are best treated as independently developed, parallel constructs. See [Authentic Power](authentic-power.md).
