# Daskalos's Cosmology

**Summary**: The Cypriot mystic Daskalos's multi-body, multi-world cosmology — a person built of material, psychic/etheric, and noetic bodies, existing within nested "worlds" of matter, images, ideas, and causes, all emanating from the Absolute through the Holy Monad, the "Idea of Man," and the Logos (Christ) — an eclectic synthesis of Orthodox Christian mysticism, Gnosticism, and reincarnation doctrine.

**Sources**:
- [Markides, K. C. (1985). *The magus of Strovolos: The extraordinary world of a spiritual healer*. Routledge & Kegan Paul.](../sources/magus-of-strovolos.md)

## The Three Bodies

Daskalos teaches that each person is constituted of three bodies, corresponding to three "worlds" of existence:

1. **Material body**: the physical body, existing in the material world (the "world of effects" or the visible dimension).
2. **Psychic (etheric) body**: the body of "sentiments" — the vehicle of feeling and vitality, existing in the "world of images and forms" (the psychic/astral dimension).
3. **Noetic body**: the body of thought, existing in the "world of ideas" (the causal or noetic dimension).

All three bodies are held together and animated by an energy field Daskalos calls "the ether" — a term he uses in a bioenergetic rather than classical-physics sense, distinguishing four properties of the ether: the **vitality property** (keeps the body alive and functioning), the **sensate property** (produces feeling and sensation), the **imprinting property** (enables thought-form/image construction — the mechanism behind [elementals](elementals-daskalos.md)), and the **property of ether proper** (constructs and maintains the material body itself). Mastery of these four properties, in ascending difficulty, is Daskalos's account of how a healer develops the ability to diagnose, heal, and eventually materialize or dematerialize matter.

At physical death, Daskalos teaches, the material body is discarded; the etheric double takes roughly forty days to fully dissolve (the traditional basis, in his account, for the Orthodox forty-day memorial candle), and the personality continues to exist and evolve on the psychic and noetic planes between incarnations.

## The Worlds and the Absolute

Beyond the three worlds tied to the individual's bodies, Daskalos describes a hierarchy of "worlds" corresponding to broader levels of manifestation: the material world, the world of images/forms, the world of ideas (archetypes and causes), and beyond these the undifferentiated ground of the **Absolute** (or "Absolute Beingness") — a term Daskalos prefers to "God" because he holds "Being" implies limitation.

From the Absolute emanates the **Holy Monad** — myriad "rays" that pass through the **Idea of Man** (the archetype of humanity) to become individual souls entering the cycle of existence. The **Logos** (identified with Christ) and the **Holy Spirit** are, in Daskalos's account, the two modes by which the Absolute manifests through Mind: the Logos as the principle of self-consciousness and will, the Holy Spirit as the creative, superconscious power that constructs the universes. Jesus of Nazareth, in this framework, is the historical vehicle through which the Logos was fully expressed, rather than being simply identical to it.

Daskalos's soul doctrine holds that the soul itself ("part of the Absolute... an ocean of souls, of which each soul is a drop") never incarnates directly; rather, it emanates a "personality" — the accumulating vehicle of individual experience across many lifetimes — that does the actual incarnating, learning, and (eventually) returning toward reunification with the source, a state he calls **Theosis**.

## Comparison to Other Wiki Cosmologies

|---|---|---|---|
| Daskalos | Material, psychic/etheric, noetic | The Absolute → Holy Monad → Logos/Holy Spirit | Yes — personality reincarnates; soul does not |
| Hindu-Tantric (Johari) | Five koshas (annamaya → anandamaya) | Brahman/Atman | Yes — samsara governed by karma |
| Brennan (HEF) | Seven auric layers | Core Star / Universal Energy Field | Implied via past-life "time conglomerates" |

Daskalos's system most closely parallels the [Hindu-Tantric kosha model](koshas.md) and Woolger's [subtle body](subtle-body.md) framework in positing an intermediate energetic vehicle between mind and matter, and closely parallels the [Zohar](../sources/zohar-book-of-enlightenment.md)'s emanationist cosmology (an unknowable ground giving rise to graded, nameable levels of manifestation) — though Daskalos's system is his own oral synthesis rather than a transmission from either tradition's textual lineage. See [Ein Sof](ein-sof.md) for the Kabbalistic parallel to the "Absolute."

## Critique

**Syncretic construction, not single-lineage transmission**: Daskalos's cosmology combines Orthodox Christian liturgical vocabulary, a reincarnation doctrine alien to mainstream Orthodox theology, Kabbalah-derived symbolism, and Gnostic-inflected dualism between matter and spirit, in a pattern typical of twentieth-century Western esoteric syncretism (compare Theosophy) rather than a demonstrable continuous transmission from any single ancient tradition, notwithstanding Daskalos's own claims (relayed by Markides) to millennia-old lineage and named "Masters."

**Unfalsifiable metaphysics**: Concepts such as the Holy Monad, the four properties of the ether, and the noetic body are not defined in terms that admit of independent verification; the entire system rests on Daskalos's own trance-derived and clairvoyant testimony as reported by a single participant-observer (Markides).

**Note**: Mainstream comparative religion and academic study of new religious movements would classify Daskalos's system as a personal esoteric synthesis in the broader lineage of twentieth-century Western spiritual teachers (alongside figures like Rudolf Steiner or the Theosophical Society's founders) rather than as the recovery of an unbroken ancient tradition.
