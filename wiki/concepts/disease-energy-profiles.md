# Disease Energy Profiles

**Summary**: Myss and Shealy's organ-level mapping of the emotional and biographical patterns associated with specific diseases — the most detailed disease-emotion taxonomy in the *Creation of Health* corpus, documented through dual-perspective case studies.

## Overview

The disease energy profiles are Myss and Shealy's paired clinical observations: for each major disease category, Shealy presents the medical diagnosis and treatment history, and Myss presents her energy analysis — identifying the chakra-level disruptions, emotional patterns, and biographical stresses she perceives as underlying or preceding the physical pathology. Together, these constitute what the authors call **holistic evaluation of the origin of disease**.

The method rests on the principle that illness does not arise randomly: each disease has a characteristic energetic signature corresponding to specific patterns of unresolved challenge in one or more of the [four challenge areas](four-challenge-areas.md) (Power, Responsibility, Wisdom, Love). The location of illness in the body reflects the chakra domain most implicated in the patient's biographical stress.

## The Profiles

### Heart Disease (Fourth Chakra)

**Shealy's framework**: Cardiovascular disease results from a complex of factors — diet, sedentary behaviour, stress chemistry, and genetic predisposition. He cites research (University of North Carolina) showing that hostility scores correlate with a fourfold increase in heart attack risk and a sixfold increase in mortality, and Duke University findings that hostility, anger, and cynicism correlate with mortality rates 6.4 times higher than low-hostility individuals.

**Myss's energy analysis**: Heart disease reflects accumulated stress in the domain of love — unexpressed grief, chronically blocked forgiveness, resentment toward those who "should have" loved the person, and the inability to receive love even when offered. The heart's energy system becomes depleted when a person's emotional life is organised around fear of vulnerability.

**Illustrative case — Frank**: An ex-priest who left the priesthood, married an ex-nun, and developed angina preventing him from walking more than a hundred feet. Myss's reading: Frank had not released his priest identity; he continued to organise his inner life according to obligations that no longer existed. The transition stress — leaving a vocation he had made heart-commitments to — was held in his cardiac system. Shealy treated with diet, biofeedback, and counselling; Frank's angina resolved significantly.

### Stroke (Fourth/Fifth Chakra Transition)

**Shealy's framework**: Stroke results from blockage or haemorrhage in cerebral blood vessels. Risk factors: hypertension, atherosclerosis, atrial fibrillation.

**Myss's energy analysis**: Strokes cluster around major life transitions — moments when a person's identity or world structure collapses before a new one is established. The energy pattern is one of "stopping": the person's life has arrived at a point they cannot navigate forward from, and the stroke enacts — physiologically — the halt.

### Cancer (Third/Fourth Chakra)

**Shealy's framework**: Cancer is abnormal cell proliferation; triggers include genetic predisposition, environmental carcinogens (cigarettes, asbestos, radiation), and immune suppression. Shealy notes that the majority of smokers do not develop lung cancer — evidence that unknown individual factors determine outcomes even within shared exposures.

**Myss's energy analysis**: Cancer energy profiles reflect deep, sustained unexpressed rage combined with extreme fear of being powerless. The person with a cancer-prone energy pattern has often spent years suppressing the expression of their authentic needs and feelings — not in a surface way but at a level that has become structural. The tumour often appears in the organ most closely related to the dominant life issue (colon: unexpressed anger and rigidity; breast: issues of nurturing and self-sacrifice; cervix/uterus: creative power and self-worth).

**Illustrative case — Lori**: A woman with cervical cancer that spread to uterus and intestines. She refused surgery and pursued alternative therapies intensively. Myss identified that Lori's treatment programme served as a form of denial — she was avoiding confronting her terror of the cancer. The "dragon" she refused to face was her own fear, not the cancer itself. She died two years after her diagnosis. This case is the book's most important self-critique: alternative medicine can serve avoidance as effectively as any other defence.

### Diabetes and Pancreatic Disorders (Third Chakra)

**Shealy's framework**: Diabetes is a disorder of insulin production or response; Type 1 involves destruction of insulin-producing cells, Type 2 involves insulin resistance.

**Myss's energy analysis**: The pancreas is read as the body's fuel-allocation system — governing how energy is directed to personal projects and life tasks. The dominant energy pattern in diabetes is resentment at having to take responsibility for one's own life, combined with a pull toward dependency. Myss reads the pancreas's disruption as the body enacting the resentment signal: insulin, as the allocation mechanism, becomes dysregulated when the person resents the terms of their own survival.

**Illustrative case — Eugene**: A thirty-one-year-old who lost his job, became depressed, and developed diabetes after his mother visited and prepared his childhood favourite desserts, which he consumed in large quantities. Myss's reading: losing the job triggered fear of personal responsibility; his mother's visit triggered a regressive desire to return to childhood dependency. The pancreas responded to the resentment inherent in this collision. Shealy treated with biofeedback, diet, and counselling; Eugene's blood sugar normalised.

### AIDS and Immune Disorders (First/Second Chakra)

**Shealy's framework**: AIDS is caused by HIV; immune dysfunction creates vulnerability to opportunistic infections.

**Myss's energy analysis**: Immune system disorders reflect first-chakra vulnerabilities — issues of tribal belonging, the right to exist, and the experience of being fundamentally rejected by one's community or family of origin. AIDS in the early 1980s context specifically often reflected the energetic experience of those who had been rejected by religious, family, and social structures for their sexuality — a rejection at the tribal level that the immune system mirrored by losing the ability to defend the self.

### Chronic Pain Syndromes (Multiple Chakras)

**Shealy's framework**: Chronic pain involves sensitisation of the pain pathways, often without ongoing tissue damage.

**Myss's energy analysis**: Chronic pain syndromes reflect sustained energy investment in maintaining patterns that the person's spirit is demanding be released. The pain becomes the spirit's loudest available signal. Migraines specifically reflect rage and frustration at perceived loss of control combined with the inability to find any outlet for that rage. Fibromyalgia-type patterns reflect the exhaustion of holding a self together against severe emotional depletion across multiple chakra domains.

### Multiple Sclerosis and Chronic Neurological Disease (Multiple Chakras)

**Myss's energy analysis**: MS and related conditions are associated with extreme rigidity of self-concept — the person who cannot yield to life's demands for change. The neurological system, which governs the relationship between central direction and peripheral response, mirrors the psychological pattern of a person who must control everything from the centre while refusing feedback from the periphery.

### Mental and Emotional Disorders

**Myss's energy analysis**: 
- **Addiction** (alcohol, drugs): reflects the attempt to manage the pain of unmet needs in all four challenge areas simultaneously — a shortcut to altered states of power, love, and belonging.
- **Panic attacks**: reflect the terror of losing control — specifically, the collapse of the illusion of control that many people use as a substitute for genuine inner security.
- **Alzheimer's disease**: Myss's reading (cautiously offered) associates it with a long history of never fully inhabiting the present — people who spent their lives preparing for a future that never arrived or living in a past they could not release.

## The Energy Analysis Method

Myss's energy analysis follows a consistent structure:

1. Identify the **chakra domain** most implicated in the illness location
2. Identify the **dominant biographical stress** in that domain (using the four challenge areas as a framework)
3. Read the **specific quality** of the energy depletion: is it resentment? fear? grief? suppressed rage? unacknowledged loss?
4. Identify the **particular life circumstance** that most directly precipitated the illness
5. Note what the patient **has not yet been willing to change** — because healing, in this framework, requires action, not just insight

## Knowing Is Not Healing

A consistent finding across the case studies: receiving an accurate energy reading does not produce healing unless the patient acts on it. This is one of the book's most important empirical observations. Shealy notes multiple cases where patients were given the same quality of intuitive information and treatment — one acted, one did not; only the one who acted recovered.

The book explicitly refuses the conclusion that a person who does not act "chose" their illness or is responsible for their death. The capacity to act is itself conditioned by biographical history, current resources, and the magnitude of the pattern that needs to change.

## Critique

**Selection**: The cases reported are selected to illustrate the framework. Cases where energy readings were inaccurate, or where a person with no apparent emotional stress pattern developed serious illness, are not included. This selection creates a representational bias.

**Specificity**: The organ-level emotion-disease mappings are presented with more certainty than the evidence supports. Pancreatic cancer arising from resentment, heart disease from blocked love — these specific claims are not validated by prospective epidemiological research.

**Complexity**: Real disease etiology is multifactorial. Genetics, environmental exposure, socioeconomic factors, access to care, and chance all play substantial roles that the energy analysis framework does not account for.

**The direction of causality**: Even if the emotional patterns consistently precede illness (which these case studies suggest), this does not establish that they caused the illness. Pre-symptomatic physiological changes could produce emotional changes before the illness becomes clinically apparent.

**Note**: Mainstream medicine does not validate the specific disease-emotion mappings proposed here. The broader relationship between chronic psychological stress and physical health outcomes is well-established (via psychoneuroimmunology), but the specific organ-level correspondences are not. These profiles should be read as clinical hypotheses, not established diagnoses.
