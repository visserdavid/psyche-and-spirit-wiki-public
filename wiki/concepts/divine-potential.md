# Divine Potential

**Summary**: In Myss's framework, divine potential is the soul's inherent capacity for its highest expression — not defined by career, relationship, or achievement, but by the degree to which a person aligns their choices, perceptions, and actions with their Sacred Contract and the guidance of the Divine.

**Sources**:
- [Myss, C. (2001). *Sacred contracts: Awakening your divine potential*. Harmony Books.](../sources/sacred-contracts.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

---

## The Concept

Divine potential is the organising idea behind the subtitle of *Sacred Contracts*. It refers not to human achievement in the conventional sense — not status, career success, or even spiritual attainment — but to the soul's capacity to express its unique Sacred Contract in full. Myss argues that potential is already present at birth; it is not created by effort but *uncovered* through the progressive alignment of will with Divine guidance.

The concept distinguishes between:

- **Potential as ego aspiration** (what the personality wants to become — usually defined in terms of external markers)
- **Divine potential** (the soul's specific curriculum for a lifetime — defined by the terms of the Sacred Contract)

These two can coincide, but often diverge. A person may be highly successful by external measures while living almost entirely outside their Contract; conversely, a person whose life appears to involve little worldly accomplishment may be precisely on course.

## The Role of Choice

Potential is not realised by striving but by choosing — specifically, by choosing in alignment with conscience, intuition, and soul-level guidance rather than from fear or habit. Myss argues that every moment contains what she calls a "choice point": an opportunity to respond to one's life from the level of potential rather than from the level of the survival archetypes.

The four [survival archetypes](four-survival-archetypes.md) (Child, Victim, Prostitute, Saboteur) represent the primary forces that prevent the realisation of potential by keeping choices anchored in fear of survival. Working with these archetypes is therefore inseparable from the realisation of divine potential.

## Potential and the Charism

Myss connects divine potential to the theological concept of *charism* — the unique gift or endowment given by the Divine as evidence that the soul is fulfilling its Contract. The charism is not a talent so much as a form of grace — a quality of energy and capacity that flows when a person is aligned with their purpose. See [Grace and Charism](grace-and-charism.md).

## Potential and the Shadow

A recurring theme in *Sacred Contracts* is that potential is not blocked by external circumstances so much as by the fear of empowerment itself. Myss observes that many people fear what full alignment with their potential would demand of them — the relationships it would require ending, the security it would require releasing, the exposure it would involve. The shadow archetypes are maintained partly *because* they protect against the demands of potential.

She quotes Jean-Paul Sartre: people are "condemned to be free." The freedom of divine potential is felt as a burden by the parts of the psyche that would rather remain defined by limitation.

## Potential and the Chakra System

In Myss's extended chakra model (see [Chakra System](chakra-system.md)), the eighth chakra — located above the crown of the head — is the seat of the soul's agreement with the Divine. It is the chakra of divine potential: the point where individuality merges with purpose, where the personal soul connects to the universal plan. The work of all seven lower chakras can be understood as the progressive clearing of obstacles to what the eighth chakra already contains.

## Illustrative Cases

- **Liza** (workshop participant): Paralysed in an accident, she came to see her limitation as the "boat she was given to row" — a vehicle for discovering her purpose working with children with disabilities. Her potential was not diminished by the accident; it was redirected by it.
- **Christopher Reeve**: Myss uses his choice to continue living as a quadriplegic and to become an advocate as an image of potential being expressed through, rather than despite, extreme physical limitation.
- **Ram Dass**: Myss reads Ram Dass's stroke (which occurred after the primary period described in the book) as a continuation of his Contract — a further assignment that produced new insight into ageing, dependency, and grace.

## Critique

**Definitional flexibility**: Divine potential is defined so broadly that it cannot be falsified — almost any life path can be reread as the expression of potential if the interpretation is creative enough. This makes the concept inspiring but analytically weak.

**Tension with accountability**: If divine potential is always and already present, and if life circumstances are Contract assignments, there is a risk of accepting unjust conditions as "meant to be" rather than working to change them. Myss insists that the Contract includes the call to resist injustice — but the framework's internal logic does not always make this clear.

**Note**: Mainstream developmental psychology discusses the realisation of human potential in terms of self-actualisation (Maslow), flow states (Csikszentmihalyi), and meaning-making (Frankl). Myss's framework overlaps with these but adds a metaphysical dimension (the pre-birth contract, the Divine as active partner) that these secular frameworks do not include.
