# Elementals (Daskalos)

**Summary**: Daskalos's term for thought-forms — semi-autonomous energy constructs projected by desire and emotion that persist, seek to return to their creator for renewed energy, and (in his account) underlie phenomena ranging from ordinary bad habits and addictions to psychic attack and possession.

**Sources**:
- [Markides, K. C. (1985). *The magus of Strovolos: The extraordinary world of a spiritual healer*. Routledge & Kegan Paul.](../sources/magus-of-strovolos.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-07-10

---

## The Claim

In Daskalos's cosmology, every thought and every sentiment "projects" — condenses substance from the "ether" (his term for the energetic substrate of the psychic/noetic worlds) into a semi-independent form he calls an **elemental**. He distinguishes "elementals of thought" (desire made ideational) from "elementals of desire" (thought made emotional), though in practice the two categories function together.

Once created, an elemental behaves according to a consistent set of rules in Daskalos's account:

- It tends to return to its creator, "like an embryo seeking food," to draw further energy and extend its own existence — the mechanism Daskalos gives for compulsive habits (smoking, gambling, obsessive desire): the elemental, not merely the person's willpower, is what must be starved of energy to be dissolved.
- Clairvoyants can perceive elementals taking on symbolic, often culturally-shaped shapes — snakes, animals, geometric forms — that Daskalos says reflect the emotional charge and cultural vocabulary of their creator rather than any fixed objective form.
- An elemental built with sufficient obsessive focus can become powerful enough to escape the creator's control — Daskalos's cautionary example is a student who built an image of a guard dog through forty days of concentrated visualization and found it had become an autonomous, attacking entity that had to be forcibly dissolved.
- Elementals projected with hostile intent toward another person do not simply strike their target; Daskalos teaches they "bounce back" against a person's protective aura if the target's own vibration does not resonate with the attacking intention — a formalized version of the "what you send returns to you" principle common across esoteric traditions.

## Elementals, Karma, and Ethical Practice

Elementals are, in Daskalos's system, the actual mechanism of [Karma](karma-and-possession-daskalos.md): because an unresolved elemental persists until its energy is exhausted or redeemed, unaddressed patterns of thought and feeling (hatred, resentment, craving) generate a literal, if invisible, backlog of consequence that must eventually be met — whether in this life or a future one. He teaches his students (the "Researchers of Truth") a discipline of nightly self-examination specifically to prevent the unconscious proliferation of hostile or fear-based elementals, framing this as more important than any ritual observance.

Notably, Daskalos frames the appropriate response to another person's aggression not as counter-attack but as non-resistant refusal to feed the interaction with matching emotional energy — a principle he illustrates with an anecdote about tolerating a habitually rude acquaintance by observing, rather than acting from, the urge to retaliate.

## Comparison to Other Wiki Frameworks

**Jung's complex and shadow-projection**: An elemental functions, structurally, very much like a Jungian [complex](individuation.md) or an externalized [shadow](shadow.md) projection — an autonomous psychic content that acts with its own apparent agency and returns to trouble its originator until consciously engaged. Daskalos's framework externalizes this dynamic into an energetic object with its own quasi-physical existence, where Jung keeps it within the structure of the psyche.

**Woolger's samskaras**: Daskalos's claim that unresolved elementals accumulate across lifetimes as karmic debt closely parallels [Woolger](../thinkers/roger-woolger.md)'s clinical concept of [past-life complexes](past-life-complexes.md) (samskaras) — both frame current-life symptoms as the reactivation of unresolved energetic residue from the past. Woolger's model, however, is offered as a therapeutic working hypothesis bracketing metaphysical truth-claims, while Daskalos asserts elementals as literally existing, perceptible (to clairvoyants) objects.

**Zukav's karma as energy dynamics**: [Zukav](../thinkers/gary-zukav.md)'s reframing of karma as an impersonal "law of energy" (see [Karma as Energy Dynamics](karma-as-energy-dynamics.md)) is close in spirit to Daskalos's elemental mechanism — both treat karmic consequence as the automatic operation of an energy-balancing law rather than moral reward or punishment administered by a personal deity. Neither offers an independently testable account of the underlying mechanism.

## Critique

**Unfalsifiable and self-sealing**: Elementals are, by definition, perceptible only to trained clairvoyants (in Daskalos's framework, advanced Researchers of Truth); ordinary observers cannot verify their existence, shape, or behavior independently. The theory also has no failure condition: any outcome (an addiction persists, a hostile intention "bounces back," an obsessive project succeeds) can be explained post hoc by elemental dynamics.

**Cultural relativity of form**: Daskalos himself states that an elemental's perceived shape depends on the "language and culture" of the person creating or perceiving it — an observation that, taken seriously, undercuts any claim that elementals have an objective form independent of the observer's own conceptual and cultural framework, and is at least as consistent with a purely psychological (projected, culturally-scripted imagery) explanation as with an objectively existing energetic entity.

**Note**: Mainstream psychology would recognize the phenomenology Daskalos describes (obsessive thought patterns feeling like they have a "life of their own," compulsions that seem to resist willpower, culturally-patterned imagery in altered states) as well documented, but would locate the mechanism entirely within neurocognitive and learned-behavior processes rather than in an independently existing energetic substance.
