# Exomatosis and Exteriorization

**Summary**: Daskalos's term for consciously abandoning the material body while retaining full memory and self-awareness, distinguished by him from ordinary clairvoyance (receiving vibrations remotely, without leaving the body) and "expansion" (spreading consciousness across space without a localized point of travel).

**Sources**:
- [Markides, K. C. (1985). *The magus of Strovolos: The extraordinary world of a spiritual healer*. Routledge & Kegan Paul.](../sources/magus-of-strovolos.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-07-10

---

## The Claim

Daskalos describes three distinct modes of extended perception and action, which he insists his students learn to differentiate carefully:

1. **Clairvoyance**: perceiving events at a distance without any sense of "traveling" — akin, in his own analogy, to watching a television broadcast originating elsewhere.
2. **Exomatosis**: the mystic's consciousness fully detaches from the material body (which remains in a sleep-like or trance state) and travels, retaining memory of the experience upon return. Daskalos teaches this is achieved through concentration and relaxation exercises — envisioning spheres of light moving progressively through the body's centers ("chakras," in his usage, or "discs") — and requires years of disciplined practice to master reliably and to retain memory of what is experienced.
3. **Expansion**: a state in which consciousness spreads outward to encompass a wide area or even, in the most advanced description Daskalos offers, to "become" the object of attention (a grain of sand, a planet) rather than traveling to observe it from a fixed vantage point.

Daskalos claims to have used exomatosis for purposes ranging from mundane (locating lost objects, as in the recurring "lost scissors" and "lost binoculars" anecdotes Markides records) to dramatic — intervening, in his account, in the 1979 Skylab re-entry alongside extraterrestrial "guardian" intelligences, and diagnosing and treating patients at a distance by projecting his etheric body to their location.

## Risk and the Silver Cord

Daskalos describes a connecting link between the exteriorized consciousness and the sleeping material body (echoing the "silver cord" motif found across the wider Western esoteric astral-projection literature), and states that harm experienced by the etheric body in exomatosis does not automatically transfer to the material body unless the practitioner fails to "disconnect" properly — an account he offers to explain an episode in which he claims to have taken a physical blow (a broken ship's mast striking his exteriorized form) that left a corresponding mark on his material body upon return.

## Comparison to Other Wiki Frameworks

**Brennan's Human Energy Field work and out-of-body claims**: Exomatosis functions similarly to the astral projection and remote-viewing claims documented across the wiki's energy-medicine literature, though [Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md)'s [Human Energy Field](seven-layer-auric-field.md) framework emphasizes in-person perception (High Sense Perception) over travel, and Daskalos's tripartite distinction (clairvoyance/exomatosis/expansion) is more granular than any single typology offered elsewhere in the wiki.

**Myss's and Joy's medical intuition**: Daskalos's claim to diagnose and treat patients at a distance by projecting consciousness to their location parallels [Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md)'s remote symbolic diagnosis and [Joy](../thinkers/w-brugh-joy.md)'s tactile body-scanning, discussed together in [Medical Intuition](medical-intuition.md) — though Daskalos frames his own method as active projection of the etheric body to the patient's location rather than passive remote reception of impressions.

**Near-death and out-of-body experience research**: Daskalos's account of a "silver cord" linking the exteriorized consciousness to the sleeping body echoes the classic NDE/OBE literature discussed in [Near-Death Experiences](near-death-experiences.md) — Ring's Omega Study and related NDE research document strikingly similar first-person reports of consciousness perceiving from outside the body, a convergence across independent traditions and eras that this wiki treats as noteworthy without treating it as proof of a shared underlying mechanism.

## Critique

**No independent verification available**: All the exomatosis episodes recorded in the book are Daskalos's own first-person narration, corroborated at most by Markides's or a disciple's observation of Daskalos's physical state (breathing, pulse, apparent trance) during the episode — never by independent confirmation of what was supposedly witnessed at the remote location. The lost-scissors and lost-binoculars anecdotes are the closest the book comes to an outcome that could in principle be checked (an object was found where Daskalos said it would be), but even these rest entirely on secondhand retelling within the book itself, decades after the fact.

**The "cannot remember" caveat is convenient and unfalsifiable**: Daskalos states that most people cannot recall exomatic experiences upon waking, comparing it to the routine failure to remember dreams — a claim that conveniently explains the absence of corroborating testimony from anyone except Daskalos and his most devoted students.

**Note**: Mainstream neuroscience explains subjectively compelling out-of-body sensations (including many NDE/OBE reports) via known mechanisms — temporal-parietal junction disruption, hypoxia, REM intrusion, and sleep paralysis — without requiring literal consciousness travel to a real remote location; this remains the dominant scientific account of the phenomenology Daskalos and the NDE literature both describe.
