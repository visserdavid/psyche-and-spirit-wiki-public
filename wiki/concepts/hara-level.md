# Hara Level

**Summary**: The dimension of intentionality that underlies the seven-layer auric field — structured as a vertical line through the body (the hara line) with three key points corresponding to the individual's divine connection, soul's longing, and will to live; central to Brennan's expanded four-dimensional model in *Light Emerging* (1993).

## Overview

The hara level is the third dimension in [Brennan's](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) [four-dimensional model of being](four-dimensions-of-being.md), sitting between the seven-layer auric field (personality dimension) and the [core star](core-star.md) (divine essence). "Hara" is a Japanese term meaning "belly" — the region of the body traditionally understood in Eastern martial arts, Zen, and Japanese culture as the seat of centred power and vital energy.

Brennan first encountered the hara concept through Karlfried Graf Dürckheim's book *Hara* and through the martial arts traditions of Japan. Using [High Sense Perception (HSP)](high-sense-perception.md), she began observing a structured energy line running through the body at a dimension deeper than the auric field — a dimension she came to call the haric level.

> "Heyoan said that the haric level exists in another dimension of the field. It exists at the level of intentionality."
> — Brennan (1993), Chapter 17

The haric level is the energetic correlate of **purpose and intention** — not the changing moment-to-moment intentions of personality, but the deep underlying intention that determines the direction and meaning of a life.

## The Hara Line

The hara line is a vertical laser-like line of energy running through the centre of the body. It originates from a point above the head, passes through the chest and abdomen, and continues down through the feet to the core of the earth. In a centred, purposeful person, this line is straight, bright, and fully connected to the earth.

The hara line has three key structural points:

### 1. Individuation Point (ID Point)

**Location**: Approximately 3–3.5 feet above the top of the head.

**Appearance**: An inverted funnel with the narrow end (about one quarter inch) pointing downward toward the head, and the wider end opening upward into the void.

**Function**: Represents the individual's point of entry into incarnation from the divine source. It carries the individual's "reason for being" — the divine intention for this incarnation. Brennan calls it "individuation into the godhead" — the individual as a localised expression of the divine choosing to enter embodied existence.

**Dysfunction**: A clogged or distorted ID point results in disconnection from the divine — cynicism about the existence of God or higher meaning, intellectual materialism, a sense that life has no ultimate purpose. Healing the ID point often releases childhood memories of spiritual connection and opens a genuine (not doctrine-based) experience of the divine.

### 2. Soul Seat

**Location**: In the chest area, approximately 2–2.5 inches above the heart chakra.

**Appearance**: A diffuse, luminous light that can expand from 1–2 inches in diameter during meditation to 15 feet or more.

**Function**: Carries the soul's longing — the felt sense of what the soul came to accomplish and express in this lifetime. Distinct from personal desires (which arise from personality-level wanting), the soul seat carries the deeper pull that, when followed, leads to the life task.

**The soul's longing as compass**: Brennan argues that the longing is the most reliable guide to the life task. Not what one thinks one should want, not what culture endorses, but the persistent pull that resurfaces no matter how many times it is suppressed. Following the longing leads toward the life task; denying it leads toward illness.

**Dysfunction**: The soul seat can become covered by a cloud of energy — a shroud — often the result of grief, loss, or sustained disconnection from one's own desires. A shrouded soul seat produces the experience of having no will for the future, no sense of what one wants, a pervasive sadness without obvious cause. Healing the soul seat releases mourning (which may have been blocked) and allows eros — the drive toward life — to re-emerge.

### 3. Tan Tien

**Location**: Approximately 2–2.5 inches below the navel, on the midline.

**Appearance**: A gold-coloured membrane about 1.5 inches in diameter; compact, like a rubber ball; does not change in size (unlike the soul seat).

**Function**: The Tan Tien (also transliterated as "Dantian" in Chinese; the centre of power in the martial arts traditions) is, in Brennan's model, the point that holds the body in physical manifestation. She describes it as sounding a single fundamental "note" — the harmonic of the sound the core of the earth makes — that keeps the body physically coherent and grounded.

> "The tan tien carries the one note — the note tone that holds the body in manifestation. The note is a harmonic of the sound the core of the earth makes."
> — Brennan (1993), Chapter 17

**Connection to the earth**: The hara line runs through the tan tien and continues down to the core of the earth. When the tan tien is functioning well and connected, the person is grounded in the deepest sense — not just energetically grounded in the auric-field sense, but rooted in the fundamental vibration of the planet.

**Dysfunction**: The tan tien can be displaced (shifted to one side), its membrane can be torn or damaged, or it can become "blown" — fragmented. Tan tien damage is associated with severe back problems, profound identity instability, catastrophic collapse in crisis, and extreme difficulty recovering from physical or psychological shocks. Martial arts practices develop and protect the tan tien; healers with damaged tan tiens cannot sustain the grounding required for deep healing work.

## Haric Level and Health

The haric level is the foundation on which the auric field rests. If the hara line is misaligned, the entire auric field above it is distorted — and distortions cannot be stably corrected at the auric level if the haric foundation is compromised.

Brennan identifies a class of illnesses and life conditions whose roots lie in haric dysfunction rather than in the auric-biographical level:

- Chronic inability to find one's direction in life (disconnected ID point or shrouded soul seat)
- Recurring cycles of collapse and crisis not explained by current-life stress (damaged tan tien)
- Illness that improves temporarily with auric-level healing but does not hold (haric misalignment undermining the auric repair)
- Deep existential emptiness or meaninglessness (soul seat dysfunction)
- Pervasive cynicism about life purpose or God (ID point dysfunction)

## Intentionality and Mixed Purposes

The hara line is the level at which intention manifests. Every action, word, and choice is informed by an underlying intention — and that intention is visible at the haric level as an alignment or misalignment of the line.

Brennan argues that many of life's difficulties, relationship conflicts, and health problems arise from **mixed intentions** — parts of the psyche carrying different or contradictory purposes simultaneously. When the lower self, mask self, and higher self are pointing in different directions (see [Mask Self, Lower Self, and Higher Self](mask-self-lower-self-higher-self.md)), the hara line shows multiple misalignments. This is expressed in life as procrastination, conflict, failed projects, and self-defeating patterns.

Argument and conflict between people, viewed at the haric level, is often the sign that two people's hara lines are out of alignment — their underlying purposes are crossed. When two people's hara lines are aligned, they can disagree on details and still work in harmony because their underlying purpose is shared.

## Hara Healing

Healing at the haric level involves working directly with the hara line and its three points:

**Assessment**: The healer uses HSP to perceive the state of the hara line — its alignment, brightness, the condition of each of the three points, and the connection to the earth.

**Techniques**:
- Placing hands on or near the tan tien, soul seat, and ID point to clear, restructure, and realign each
- Reconnecting the hara line to the core of the earth (grounding at the haric level)
- Clearing the shroud from the soul seat through directed healing energy and presence
- Declogging the ID point to restore the sense of divine connection
- Repairing tears in the tan tien membrane

**Integration with auric and core star healing**: Hara healing is typically done in combination with auric field work and core star activation. The sequence in a complete healing session may be: auric-level clearing → hara alignment → core star upwelling → sealing.

**Self-healing at the haric level**: The Core Star Meditation (channelled by Heyoan, described in Chapter 18) begins with hara alignment — establishing awareness of each of the three points in sequence before opening to the core star. This is the practical entry point for self-directed haric healing.

## Scientific Context

Karlfried Graf Dürckheim's *Hara* (1962) provides the philosophical and therapeutic context for Brennan's haric model. Dürckheim — a German philosopher and psychotherapist who spent years in Japan — explored the significance of the hara as a quality of centred presence that goes beyond the physical location.

The martial arts traditions of Japan, China, and Korea have worked with the hara/tan tien/dantian for thousands of years as the centre of martial power, meditative stability, and vital energy (ki/chi). Brennan explicitly acknowledges this lineage while noting that her model extends it through HSP observations of the field structures.

**Note**: The haric level exists, in Brennan's description, in a dimension "beyond" the four-dimensional spacetime that current scientific instruments can access. No mainstream scientific evidence supports the existence of a hara line, soul seat, or tan tien as anatomical structures distinct from the abdominal anatomy. These are phenomenological descriptions from an extended perceptual system.

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

**Myss's Sacred Contracts**: The pre-birth agreements that Myss calls Sacred Contracts can be understood as haric-level intentions — the purposes that the soul seat carries and the ID point connects to. The "life task" of Brennan's haric level is structurally parallel to Myss's "Sacred Contract."

**Hillman's daimon**: Hillman's daimon — the guardian that carries the soul's image throughout a life — functions at a level equivalent to what Brennan locates in the soul seat: the felt pull that insists on expression.

**Keleman's formative psychology**: The tan tien's "one note" holding the body in form resonates with Keleman's description of the fundamental pulsatory tone that organises biological form. Both locate a basic organisational vibration at the deepest level of somatic life.
