# High Sense Perception

**Summary**: Brennan's term for the systematic extension of the five physical senses into ranges that allow direct perception of the Human Energy Field — each of the seven chakras governing a distinct perceptual mode, from kinesthetic body sensing to cosmic knowing.

**Sources**:
- [Brennan, B. A. (1987). *Hands of light: A guide to healing through the human energy field*. Pleiades Books.](../sources/hands-of-light.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-24

---

## Overview

High Sense Perception (HSP) is [Brennan's](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) term for the expanded sensory capacity through which she perceives and works with the [Human Energy Field (HEF)](seven-layer-auric-field.md). It is not one faculty but seven distinct perceptual modes, each associated with one of the [chakras](chakra-system.md) and each accessing a different quality of information from the HEF and from other people.

HSP is distinct from the culturally familiar terms "clairvoyance" or "psychic ability." Brennan frames it as a natural extension of human sensory capacity — analogous to the way instruments extend the range of vision into infrared or ultraviolet — rather than as a supernatural gift. She documents its development in her own biography as a gradual process beginning with childhood nature experiences and culminating in fifteen years of clinical practice.

## The Seven Modes of HSP

Each chakra governs a distinct perceptual channel:

| Chakra | Mode | Quality of information |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (Root) | Kinesthetic body sense | Physical health and pain in one's own or a patient's body; shivers, energy running along the spine; sensing presence |
| 2 (Sacral) | Emotional sensing / clairsentience | Feeling others' emotional states; the quality and quantity of love present; empathic body resonance |
| 3 (Solar plexus) | Intuition | Gut knowing; sensing intent, size, shape; knowing without knowing how — the classic "intuition" |
| 4 (Heart) | Loving sense | The quality of heart connection; empathy; sensing relational cords between people |
| 5 (Throat) | Clairaudience / channelling | Hearing inner sounds, words, music; receiving verbal guidance from spirit teachers; the mode of channelling |
| 6 (Third eye) | Internal vision / clairvoyance | Receiving pictures and images in the mind's eye; seeing the HEF, organs, past-life scenes; remote viewing of the body's interior |
| 7 (Crown) | Cosmic knowing | Unity consciousness; knowing God; the Messianic or Christ-consciousness mode; available through deep meditation |

In practice, healers tend to favour one or two modes and receive information primarily through those channels. Brennan describes using multiple modes simultaneously during healing sessions — seeing (chakra 6), feeling (chakra 1), and channelling guidance (chakra 5) at once.

## Key Distinctions

### Perceiving vs. Visualising

Brennan draws a foundational distinction:
- **Perceiving**: Passive reception of information that arises from the field. The image comes; the seer does not create it.
- **Visualising**: Active creation of mental imagery by the mind. The image is constructed.

Both are real processes, but they serve different functions. Visualisation (directed imagination) is creative — it sends energy and helps build reality. Perception (HSP) is receptive — it reads reality as it is. Confusing them is the most common error in HSP development: projecting one's fears or wishes onto the field as though reading it.

### Seeing vs. Imagination

Brennan carefully distinguishes HSP vision from imagination. The key indicator: HSP images are often unexpected, unwanted, or contrary to what the healer hoped to find. Imagination produces what the mind already knows or wishes; HSP produces what the field actually contains.

### Sensing vs. Dreaming

Higher HSP modes (particularly channelling and cosmic knowing) can resemble hypnagogic or dreamlike states. Brennan teaches that authentic HSP maintains a quality of alertness and objectivity — the perceiver observes rather than participates in what they see.

## Development

HSP is developed through:

1. **Meditation**: Entering an expanded state of consciousness in which ordinary ego chatter subsides; various forms (walking, sitting, focused awareness) all work
2. **Sensory practice**: Deliberate exercises for sensing energy fields — holding palms close, sensing spaces in rooms, observing colour and form around living objects (plants, trees, people)
3. **Feedback and verification**: Working with teachers and in clinical settings where HSP impressions can be compared against known information
4. **Energy system purification**: Clearing energy blocks, opening chakras, raising the frequency of one's own field — because what one can perceive is limited to frequencies one's own system can sustain
5. **Silencing the mind**: The most demanding practice; the bandwidth of HSP information is narrow relative to mental noise

Brennan emphasises that HSP is already active in most people at low levels: the experience of "vibes," knowing when someone is staring, feeling someone else's pain in one's own body, sensing room atmospheres — these are ordinary expressions of HSP through chakras 1–4.

## The Cone of Perception

Drawing on her guide Heyoan, Brennan describes human reality perception as a "cone" — a frequency range that defines what each individual takes as real. Objects or events outside the cone are not perceived and therefore not considered real.

HSP expands the cone. What was previously imperceptible — the HEF, the movements of the astral plane, the communications of spirit guides — becomes perceptible, and is thereby added to one's experienced reality. This framework connects to David Bohm's concept of the implicate order (the enfolded reality behind the explicate): HSP allows perception of deeper orders of reality that ordinary sense-perception cannot access.

The cone also maps onto the concept of Maya in Buddhism: ordinary perception is defined by what lies within the cone; what lies outside it is not illusion but simply beyond the current frequency range of awareness.

## HSP and Channelling

At the chakra 5 and 7 levels, HSP shades into what Brennan calls channelling — receiving direct communication from spirit teachers or guides. Brennan's principal teacher is Heyoan, who began communicating through vision and hearing and progressively moved into fuller channelling, including direct speech through Brennan's body in the late stages of their relationship.

She describes the entry into channelling as: the guide arrives in the field → the healer enters an expanded state → the guide moves closer and eventually merges with the healer's energy system → information flows as words, concepts, or images. The exit involves the guide withdrawing and the healer's consciousness sinking back into normal ego awareness.

Brennan distinguishes authentic channelling (which she tests by whether the information is accurate, unconditional, and beyond her normal knowledge) from ordinary self-talk or projection.

## Relationship to Medical Intuition

Brennan's HSP and [Myss's medical intuition](medical-intuition.md) share the same foundational claim — that the HEF carries information about a person's health and biography that can be read by a skilled perceiver. They differ in practice:

| Aspect | Brennan's HSP | Myss's Medical Intuition |
|---|---|---|
| Mode | Primarily visual (Layer 6); also kinesthetic | Primarily symbolic/imagistic; biographical |
| Application | Diagnostic AND therapeutic — reads and restructures | Diagnostic only — reads, does not treat |
| Framework | Seven distinct perceptual modes, chakra-governed | Unified intuitive perception through symbolic sight |
| Spirit guidance | Central (Heyoan) | Not a feature |
| Training emphasis | Progressive development of perceptual capacity | Read the Seven Sacred Truths through the field |

## Critique

**Subjectivity**: HSP observations are not inter-rater reliable in any controlled sense. Brennan, Bruyere, Karagulla, and other sensitives produce broadly consistent observations of major features (chakra locations, major colours, block types) but differ on details. Whether this represents legitimate variation in the field or differences in perception/interpretation is unresolved.

**Perception vs. projection**: Brennan herself acknowledges this is the central challenge. The training she describes cannot fully distinguish between authentic field perception and the unconscious projection of expectations. The Hunt/Bruyere UCLA study is the closest to a partial validation, showing colour-frequency correlations; but this covers a narrow band of HSP claim.

**Scientific status**: HSP is not recognised as a validated perceptual capacity by mainstream science or medicine. Claims of internal organ viewing, past-life perception, and spirit guide communication are not testable by current scientific methods.

**Note**: While the body generates measurable electromagnetic fields that can be detected externally (SQUID, MEG), no mainstream instrument has detected the seven-layer HEF structure or confirmed that it carries the biographical and psychological information Brennan describes.
