# Holy Instant

**Summary**: In *A Course in Miracles*, the holy instant is the moment outside sequential time in which the mind releases the past, accepts the present, and recognises its oneness with the Holy Spirit — the experiential heart of forgiveness and the gateway to the real world.

**Sources**:
- Schucman, H. (1976). *A course in miracles*. Foundation for Inner Peace.

**Last updated**: 2026-06-25

---

## Definition

The Text devotes Chapter 15 to the holy instant. The Course defines it as "a little island of instant" between the ego's past and its projected future — a moment in which neither the past nor the anticipated future holds any power. In the holy instant, the mind is simply present, and in that presence the Holy Spirit can be heard.

The Course states: "The holy instant is the recognition that all minds are in communication." It is not an extraordinary mystical event reserved for advanced spiritual practitioners; it is available in any moment, to anyone who is genuinely willing to step back from the ego's narrative.

## Time and the Holy Instant

The ego operates through time: it uses the past to generate guilt and the anticipated future to generate fear. Its control over the mind depends on keeping attention in one of these two zones, never in the present. The present is where God is — where the Holy Spirit speaks — and the ego cannot survive contact with the present.

The holy instant interrupts this mechanism. It is not a technique for making time more pleasant; it is a temporary suspension of the ego's time-construct. In the holy instant, the mind recognises that the past is gone and the future is not yet, and that what is present is simply awareness itself — which is the beginning of recognising the self as spirit rather than body.

This resonates with the [centering prayer](centering-prayer.md) practice of Thomas Keating — "identifying with the river of consciousness rather than its contents" — and with [mindfulness](mindfulness.md) in Borysenko's framework. The distinction is that ACIM grounds the practice in a specific metaphysical framework: the holy instant is not about relaxation or wellbeing but about Atonement.

## Holy Instant and Relationships

The Text presents the holy instant primarily in the context of [special vs. holy relationships](special-vs-holy-relationships.md). The transformation of a special relationship into a holy one occurs through shared holy instants — moments when both people release their ego investment in the relationship and see each other, briefly, as the Holy Spirit does.

"The holy instant is a time in which you receive and give perfect communication. This means, however, that it is a time in which your mind is open, both to receive and give."

The holy instant is the vehicle through which the other person becomes a "brother" in the Course's sense: not a rival, a dependent, or a mirror for one's own needs, but a fellow Son of God, temporarily dreaming with oneself.

## Holy Instant and Miracles

Every [miracle (ACIM)](miracles-acim.md) is an expression of a holy instant. The miracle occurs in the instant the mind releases a grievance and sees the other's sinlessness. This instant is outside time — hence miracles "collapse time." The learning that would otherwise require years of painful experience is accomplished in the holy instant of genuine forgiveness.

"The holy instant is a miniature of eternity. It is a picture of timelessness, set in a frame of time."

## Practical Use

The Course treats the holy instant as something to be practised rather than waited for. The Workbook lessons, particularly in the middle and later sections, include instructions such as:

> "Let me be still and listen to the truth."
> "I will be still an instant and go home."
> "Father, I come to You today."

These are not mantras but gestures of willingness — invitations to the Holy Spirit to enter the space the ego temporarily vacates. The emphasis is on willingness, not on achieving a particular state. The Holy Spirit does the rest.

## Comparison with Peak Experiences

Abraham Maslow's [peak experiences](../thinkers/abraham-maslow.md) — moments of intense unity, timelessness, and clarity — are the secular psychological correlate of the holy instant. Maslow documented their occurrence and noted their transformative aftereffects. ACIM gives them a theological location: they are moments in which the veil of the ego thins and the underlying reality is glimpsed. See also [near-death experiences](near-death-experiences.md) as the most complete version of this category.
