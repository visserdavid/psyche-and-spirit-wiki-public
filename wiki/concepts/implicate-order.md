**Summary**: David Bohm's holographic cosmology: the universe is an undivided whole ("holomovement") in which the visible world of separate objects (explicate order) continuously unfolds from and enfolds back into a deeper level of enfolded, interconnected reality (implicate order); the philosophical physics foundation of non-local consciousness, the holographic healing model, and the mechanism for synchronicity and morphic resonance.

## The Central Distinction

David Bohm proposed that reality has two interpenetrating orders:

**Explicate order** ("unfolded order"): The observable domain of separate objects in space and time. Particles have positions; events have causes; people have bodies distinct from other bodies. This is the world as conventional science studies it.

**Implicate order** ("enfolded order"): The deeper domain in which everything is enfolded into everything else — an undivided whole without fundamental separateness. Separation, location, and causal succession are features of the explicate unfolding, not of the implicate ground. As Bohm wrote: "The notion of a separate organism is clearly an abstraction."

**Holomovement**: Bohm's preferred term for the flowing, dynamic reality that encompasses both orders. The universe is not a collection of things but a continuous movement of unfolding and enfolding. Electrons, waves, fields, consciousness, and life are all aspects of this one movement — like eddies in a river, distinguishable but never actually separate.

## The Holographic Analogy

Bohm uses the hologram as a metaphor for the implicate order (though he explicitly says the universe is not literally a hologram but is more dynamic — hence "holomovement"):

- A holographic plate contains an interference pattern in which the full image is enfolded across every region of the plate (implicate order)
- Illuminating the plate with a laser projects a three-dimensional image of the object (explicate order)
- Destroying half the plate does not destroy half the image — the whole remains, with reduced resolution (each piece contains the whole)

Similarly, in the implicate order:
- Every region of space contains information about the whole universe
- A particle is not a localised thing but an "element" of the implicate whole that briefly "unfolds" into the explicate as a measurable event
- Consciousness, matter, and field are aspects of the same enfolded whole

## The Ink-in-Glycerine Model

Bohm illustrated the implicate order with a physical device: a cylinder of viscous glycerine with an ink droplet. When the cylinder is turned, the drop spreads and disappears — enfolding into the implicate. When the cylinder is reversed, the drop reconstitutes itself — unfolding back into the explicate. The drop's order is preserved in the glycerine even when invisible; it is implicate (enfolded) when distributed, explicate (unfolded) when reconstituted.

## Soma-Significance: Meaning as Bridge

Bohm introduced the concept of "soma-significance" to describe how consciousness and body interact without the need for causal force: meaning is the bridge. The body does not respond to information as a mechanical force; it responds to the significance of what is perceived. A thought that a shadow is an assailant immediately produces adrenaline, muscle tension, and elevated heart rate — not because the thought exerted physical force but because of the meaning the thought carried.

This is the holographic mechanism for psychosomatic effects, placebo response, and the entire field of [psychoneuroimmunology](psychoneuroimmunology.md): the body is a meaning-responsive system, not merely a chemical machine.

## Quantum Non-locality as Implicate Evidence

The 1982 Aspect experiment (Alain Aspect, Paris Institute of Applied Optics) produced definitive confirmation of quantum non-locality: two photons, separated by 6.5 metres, instantaneously correlated their polarisation states even when the analyser settings were switched in the 10 nanoseconds between their emission and detection — ruling out any known signal transmission. Bell's theorem had previously established that local hidden variables could not account for quantum correlations.

Bohm interpreted non-locality as direct evidence of the implicate order: separated particles are not truly separate but remain aspects of one enfolded whole. Their correlation is not a signal between them but an expression of their unbroken implicate connection.

## Hierarchies Within the Implicate

Bohm did not stop at one implicate level. He proposed that below the implicate order lie "super-implicate" and still deeper orders — an infinite regress of increasingly enfolded wholeness. This infinite regression allows for the possibility of levels of reality that include not just particles and fields but life, consciousness, and intelligence at every scale.

## Cosmology: The Sea of Energy

In *Wholeness and the Implicate Order*, Chapter 7, Bohm extends the implicate order to cosmology. Extrapolating the zero-point energy of quantum fields down to the Planck length (roughly 10⁻³³ cm — far beyond what any experiment has probed, current tests reaching only about 10⁻¹⁷ cm), the calculated energy density of "empty" space vastly exceeds the energy density of all the ordinary matter in the observable universe. Bohm proposes that this "sea of energy" is the deeper implicate ground from which both the ordinary vacuum and matter emerge as relatively minor ripples — and that the Big Bang itself can be pictured as a single such ripple, "exploding" outward the way a wave pulse forms on an ocean from many smaller waves coming into phase.

He also proposes that mind and matter are not two interacting substances but "projections of a common ground" — the implicate order itself being prior to, and neither, mind nor matter. He cites Leibniz's monads and Whitehead's "actual occasions" as historical anticipations, and Jean Piaget's developmental psychology as empirical evidence that a child's experience of a world of separate, persisting objects in fixed space, time, and causal order is *constructed* through development, not simply given by unmediated perception.

## Critique

**Minority physics position**: The implicate order is Bohm's personal interpretation of quantum mechanics. Mainstream physicists find it metaphysically suggestive but empirically equivalent to standard interpretations — making no additional testable predictions. The Copenhagen interpretation, many-worlds, and other frameworks are not ruled out by the same data.

**Not a literal hologram**: Bohm himself resisted the "holographic universe" label. The hologram is a metaphor; the holomovement is more dynamic and cannot be fully captured by the static plate-and-laser image.

**Consciousness claims**: The proposal that electrons possess a rudimentary analogue of mind is speculative and not derivable from Bohm's physics alone; it is a philosophical extension.

**Note**: Mainstream physics treats quantum non-locality as a confirmed experimental fact but does not require Bohm's implicate order as its explanation. The implicate order remains a minority philosophical interpretation.
