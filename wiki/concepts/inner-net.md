# Inner Net

**Summary**: Myss's metaphor for the unconscious web of archetypal power patterns — an interconnected grid through which every thought, choice, emotion, and relational impulse transmits energy across the psyche, proposed as the original "internet" of human consciousness.

**Sources**:
- [Myss, C. (2013). *Archetypes: Who are you?* Hay House.](../sources/archetypes-who-are-you.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-24

---

## The Concept

In [*Archetypes: Who Are You?*](../sources/archetypes-who-are-you.md) (2013), [Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) introduces the **Inner Net** as the central image for understanding how archetypes operate in the unconscious. Where the internet is a global network through which information transmits in real time, the Inner Net is the psychic network through which power — in the form of thoughts, choices, feelings, and sensations — transmits across the human energy system.

> "Picture the Earth and imagine billions of lines crisscrossing its surface, serving as a fire network transferring activity at a constant rate. Every thought, feeling, sensation, and vibration transmitted on this grid transfers one ingredient of experience: power."
>
> — Myss (2013)

Every decision a person makes is, in Myss's framework, a **power calculation** transmitted on this grid. The question is always: does this choice empower or disempower me? Speak or stay silent? Act on intuition or seek more information? Choose the comfortable or the true? Each decision — however small — is a transmission of power. Life, in this model, is a continuous negotiation of power across the Inner Net.

---

## Archetypes as Programs

Each archetype functions on the Inner Net like a **computer program**: a set of myths, a reservoir of symbols, and a behavioural logic that organises and energises life in a particular domain. When an archetype is active, it draws power, generates motivations, interprets events, and guides choices according to its characteristic logic.

This is not a conscious process. The archetype operates from the unconscious — much as software runs in the background regardless of the user's awareness. The Inner Net metaphor makes Jung's collective unconscious legible to a contemporary audience: the archetypes are not abstract philosophical constructs but the actual operating system of the psyche.

---

## Archetype Families

The Inner Net is not populated by isolated programs but by **archetype families** — clusters of related patterns sharing a power theme. The ten contemporary archetypes profiled in the book are each the representative of a larger family:

| Representative | Family Name | Related Patterns |
|---------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Advocate | Change | Organizer, Naturist, Defender |
| Artist/Creative | Expression | Performer, Storyteller, Visual Artist |
| Athlete | Body | Competitor, Thrill Seeker, Iron Man/Woman |
| Caregiver | Caring | Mother, Nurturer, Rescuer, Teacher, Companion |
| Fashionista | Beauty | Gentleman, Metro Male |
| Intellectual | Thinking | Professional, Student |
| Queen/Executive | Authority | Leader, Matriarch |
| Rebel | Freedom | Maverick, Warrior, Hero |
| Spiritual Seeker | Meaning | Mystic |
| Visionary | Possibility | Entrepreneur, Innovator, Pioneer |

Recognising the family gives more precision: two people may share the Caregiver archetype but express it quite differently — one as Mother, another as Teacher — because the family holds multiple expressions of the same power theme.

## Archemates

The Inner Net enables **Archemates**: two people whose archetypal patterns are mutually magnetizing. The experience of instant connection — "I felt a buzz from the moment I met you" — is, in Myss's framework, the recogni­tion of compatible archetypes in the unconscious. The attraction is not merely personal chemistry but the resonance of two Inner Nets interacting.

Archemates may share the same archetype (two Rebels who ignite each other's fire) or may hold complementary patterns (a Visionary whose Inner Net resonates with an Executive's drive to implement). The degree of intensity varies; not all Archemate connections are deep. What is consistent is the sense of being recognised at the archetypal level — not just as a personality but as a power pattern.

## Intuition as Inner Net Communication

Myss argues that the language of the Inner Net is **intuition**: hunches, gut instincts, chills, and bodily sensations that convey information faster than the reasoning mind. The archetypes communicate not through words or prayer but by animating myths, energising fantasies, and generating meaningful coincidences — what Jung called [synchronicity](synchronicity.md).

The Inner Net is therefore both descriptive (it explains how the unconscious operates) and practical (developing intuition is learning to read the Inner Net). This connects the 2013 book to Myss's wider project of cultivating [symbolic sight](symbolic-sight.md) — the capacity to perceive the archetypal patterns operating beneath the surface of daily life.

---

## Relationship to Jung's Collective Unconscious

The Inner Net is Myss's contemporary reformulation of [the collective unconscious](collective-unconscious.md) as described by [Carl Jung](../thinkers/carl-jung.md). In Jung's original framework, the collective unconscious is the inherited, universal layer of the psyche beneath the personal unconscious — structured by archetypes as "inherited modes of psychic functioning." It is not a metaphor but a theoretical claim about the structure of the psyche.

Myss's Inner Net preserves the core intuition — that archetypes are impersonal power patterns operating through individuals — while translating it into a contemporary idiom accessible to readers who may not engage with analytical psychology. The shift from "collective unconscious" to "Inner Net" is primarily rhetorical: one is a clinical theoretical term, the other a digital-age metaphor. The underlying claim is the same.

Key difference: Jung's collective unconscious is primarily a theoretical construct explaining the universality of mythic imagery and the behaviour of neurotic symptoms. Myss's Inner Net is primarily a practical tool — something you can use to read your choices, identify your archetypes, and consciously redirect your power. The clinical depth is reduced; the practical application is expanded.

## Critique

**The metaphor's limits**: The internet is a designed artefact transmitting discrete digital information. The unconscious is neither designed nor digital. The metaphor serves pedagogy but can mislead: it implies a precision and accessibility to archetypal processes that does not exist in practice.

**Power as the universal currency**: Myss's claim that every thought, feeling, and sensation transmits "power" is unfalsifiable. It is a metaphysical assertion, not an empirically testable hypothesis. Mainstream psychology does not support the idea of a quantifiable power grid in the unconscious.

**Positive contribution**: As a pedagogical device, the Inner Net is one of Myss's most effective. It gives the abstract concept of the collective unconscious a tangible, contemporary image — and positions intuition as the faculty for reading it. For readers already familiar with the internet's networked logic, the metaphor makes archetypes feel immediate rather than academic.
