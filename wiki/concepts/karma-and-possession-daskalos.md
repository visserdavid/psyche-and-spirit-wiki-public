# Karma and Possession (Daskalos)

**Summary**: Daskalos's teaching that Karma is an impersonal law administered by named "Masters of Karma," that illness and suffering represent debts being paid (sometimes voluntarily assumed by a healer or loved one on another's behalf), and that possession results from departed-human spirits or malevolent "elementals" occupying a living person's etheric double — distinct from, but administratively related to, his broader system of [elementals](elementals-daskalos.md).

**Sources**:
- [Markides, K. C. (1985). *The magus of Strovolos: The extraordinary world of a spiritual healer*. Routledge & Kegan Paul.](../sources/magus-of-strovolos.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-07-10

---

## Karma as Assumed Debt

Daskalos teaches Karma as a "law of balance": every debt (harm caused to another, in this life or a previous one) must eventually be repaid, either by the person who incurred it or — crucially, in Daskalos's account — by another person who voluntarily "assumes" the debt out of love, absorbing the suffering to spare the original debtor. He illustrates this with a series of first-person healing anecdotes in which he claims to have taken on a family member's or patient's Karma directly, at a physical cost to himself: a grandson's birth defect connected to a past-life debt from an incarnation as a pirate; his own hospitalization and near leg-amputation in 1983, which he attributes to assuming his son-in-law's illness. He is explicit that a healer merely channeling energy to assist a cure (which he calls ordinary therapeutic work) is categorically different from a Master "bearing" another's Karma, which he says only advanced practitioners can do and always exacts a physical toll.

Four named entities — "Masters of Karma," associated with the archangels Michael, Raphael, Ouriel, and Siamael (Satan) — administer, in Daskalos's system, the pairing of souls into circumstances ("holes" in a "sieve," in his own metaphor) that will allow accumulated debts to be worked out between the right parties across incarnations.

## Possession

Daskalos's account of possession (Chapter 11, "Tales of Possession") distinguishes several categories:

- **Elemental possession**: a person's own unresolved obsessive elemental grows powerful enough to dominate their behavior — his account of ordinary addiction and compulsion (see [Elementals (Daskalos)](elementals-daskalos.md)).
- **Departed-human possession**: the etheric remnant of a deceased person, unable to fully "dissolve" or move on (often due to unresolved craving, trauma, or violent death), attaches to and draws vitality from a living person's etheric double. His central example is "Hadas," an Israeli patient he diagnoses as possessed by the etheric remnants of two Nazi war criminals killed in an Allied bombing, whom he treats with a Cabbalistic-Christian ritual invoking the Star of David, the phrase "Shalom Alehem," and a concept he calls ***Erevos*** — a state of induced forgetting he frames as an act of Divine Mercy (not punishment) that allows destructive spirits to be neutralized without being "destroyed," since Daskalos holds no entity is ever annihilated, only transformed.
- **Demonic entities**: Daskalos describes "demons" as a distinct category from human departed spirits — beings that, in his telling, are emanations from a "side of existence" associated with the archangel Lucifer, morally simple (comparable, he says, to animals) rather than calculatingly evil, and capable in principle of eventual evolution toward the Holy Spirit. He recounts befriending and "taming" one such entity ("Baalbook") rather than expelling it outright.

## Comparison to Martin's Possession Framework

Daskalos's possession material offers the wiki's clearest same-phenomenon contrast to [Malachi Martin](../thinkers/malachi-martin.md)'s [possession process](possession-process-martin.md) in [*Hostage to the Devil*](../sources/hostage-to-the-devil.md):

| Dimension | Daskalos | Martin |
|---|---|---|
| Nature of possessing entity | Departed human remnants, or morally simple/evolving "demons" | A wholly other, strategically deceptive, non-human, malevolent intelligence |
| Moral status of the entity | Not intrinsically evil — evil is described as "ignorance," not substance; even demons can evolve toward the Holy Spirit | Substantial, calculating evil with independent will and intent |
| Role of the exorcist/healer | Redirect and neutralize (via *Erevos* forgetting, ritual, or negotiation); befriending rather than only expelling | Direct confrontation and expulsion via the Roman Catholic exorcism rite |
| Underlying cosmology | Karma, elementals, reincarnation; possession as one manifestation of a broader energetic-karmic system | Roman Catholic theology; possession as a discrete category requiring the Church's specific rite |
| Consent structure | Possession follows from unresolved craving or trauma (in the possessed or the possessing spirit), not necessarily from a "disposing factor" chosen by the victim | A slow, consent-dependent process requiring the victim's ongoing (if unwitting) yielding at each of four stages |

Both frameworks agree that possession is a real clinical phenomenon distinct from ordinary illness and that a specialized ritual practitioner (Daskalos or Martin's exorcist-priests) is required to resolve it — but they diverge sharply on the possessing entity's ultimate nature, moral status, and the possibility of its own redemption, a genuinely different theology of evil despite the surface-level similarity of "possession by an outside entity" language. Both also converge with [Jung](../thinkers/carl-jung.md)'s and [Fromm](../thinkers/erich-fromm.md)'s accounts in this wiki's broader [Human Evil](human-evil.md) cluster on treating evil (or its Karmic analogue) as psychologically and clinically real, while diverging from each of them, and from each other, on where it is ultimately located.

## Comparison to Zukav's Karma as Energy Dynamics

[Zukav](../thinkers/gary-zukav.md)'s reframing of karma as an impersonal "law of energy" administered without moral judgment (see [Karma as Energy Dynamics](karma-as-energy-dynamics.md)) closely parallels Daskalos's own "law of balance" framing — both explicitly reject the idea of Karma as reward/punishment dispensed by a judgmental deity, and both hold that compassionate, non-judgmental response to another's suffering is the appropriate stance precisely because Karma's full causal history is not visible to the ordinary personality. Daskalos's system is more institutionally elaborate, however, positing named administering Masters and a specific mechanism (elementals) by which karmic debt accrues.

## Critique

**No independent verification**: As with all of Daskalos's healing and diagnostic claims, the possession cases and karmic-debt narratives rest entirely on his own first-person account as recorded by Markides, with no clinical documentation, psychiatric records, or third-party corroboration.

**Standard psychiatric alternative**: The "Hadas" case — dramatic personality change, reported voices, extreme distress, resolution following a culturally-resonant ritual (the Star of David, Hebrew prayer) — is consistent with a culturally-shaped dissociative or conversion presentation, addressed in the mainstream clinical literature under "possession trance" and related categories, following the same differential-diagnosis logic detailed in [Martin's Stages of Possession § Critique](possession-process-martin.md#critique).

**The Karma-assumption claims are unfalsifiable**: The claim that a healer's own illness (Daskalos's leg wound, his eventual death from complications) is caused by voluntarily "assuming" another's Karma cannot be distinguished, from the outside, from ordinary illness with mundane causes — the theory offers no way to test the causal claim independently.

**Note**: Mainstream medicine and psychiatry do not recognize karmic debt-transfer as a mechanism of illness causation in any form; this places Daskalos's illness narratives in the same evidentiary category as the wiki's other karma- and possession-based illness explanations (see [Karma as Energy Dynamics § Critique](karma-as-energy-dynamics.md#critique)).
