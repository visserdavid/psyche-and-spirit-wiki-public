# Koshas (Five Sheaths of Being)

**Summary**: The Hindu-Vedantic model of five interpenetrating sheaths (koshas) surrounding the Self — from the gross physical body to the subtle bliss body — providing a layered anatomy of human existence that complements the chakra system and informs the understanding of how Kundalini and Yoga practices work at each level.

**Sources**:
- [Johari, H. (2000). *Chakras: Energy centers of transformation* (Rev. expanded ed.). Destiny Books.](../sources/chakras-energy-centers.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-24

---

## Overview

The *kosha* model (*kosha* = sheath, covering) comes from the Taittiriya Upanishad and is developed throughout the Vedantic and Tantric literature. It describes five progressively subtler bodies that surround and obscure the Atman (Self). The Self itself is pure, unchanging consciousness; the koshas are the garments it wears during embodied existence. [Harish Johari](../thinkers/harish-johari.md) uses this model in [*Chakras: Energy Centers of Transformation*](../sources/chakras-energy-centers.md) to explain how the five pranas, the mind, the intellect, and the [Kundalini](kundalini.md) energy operate across different levels of the being, and why different yogic practices (asana, pranayama, dharana, dhyana) address different koshas.

## The Five Koshas

### 1. Annamayi Kosha — Sheath of Matter

*Anna* = food. The gross physical body, made of the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, akasha) and the seven *dhatus* (body tissues: flesh, bone, clay, blood, marrow, fluids). The Annamayi Kosha is nourished by food and sustained by prana. It is the densest sheath and the one most visible to ordinary perception.

Yogic practices primarily addressing this kosha: *asana* (postures).

### 2. Pranamayi Kosha — Sheath of Vital Air

The vital or energetic body. Prana (life force) maintains the organism and creates the foundation through which the physical body can function. The five pranas (Prana, Apana, Samana, Vyana, Udana) operate through this sheath; the [nadi system](prana-and-nadis.md) is its infrastructure. When prana is withdrawn or disturbed — through illness, emotional shock, or improper living — the physical body suffers accordingly.

Yogic practices primarily addressing this kosha: *pranayama* (breath control).

### 3. Manomayi Kosha — Sheath of Mind

The mental-emotional body. Contains *manas* (sense-mind, the faculty that registers sensory experience and generates reactive thought), the five sense organs, and the five work organs. The Manomayi Kosha is where desires arise, where the world is perceived through the filter of past experience and emotional colouring, and where the sleep-waking-dream cycle plays out.

In Johari's psychology, most human beings live primarily at the level of the Manomayi Kosha — governed by sense experience, reactive thought, and the desires of the lower chakras. The aim of Yoga is to transcend the mind's trap (*manas* as maya-maker).

Yogic practices primarily addressing this kosha: *pratyahara* (withdrawal of the senses) and *dharana* (concentration).

### 4. Vijnanamaya Kosha — Sheath of Wisdom/Intellect

The wisdom or discriminative body. Contains *buddhi* (intellect, faculty of discernment and decision), *ahamkara* (I-consciousness, the sense of separate identity), and *chitta* (feeling-self, accumulated impressions). This is the sheath of higher cognitive function — judgment, discrimination, spiritual insight — but also of the ego's subtler attachment.

Note: In the source the sheath is spelled "Vijnanamaya" (more common in Vedantic texts) though some editions use "Vijnaanamaya." Johari uses it as the seat of buddhi and ahamkara.

Yogic practices primarily addressing this kosha: *dhyana* (meditation).

### 5. Anandamayi Kosha — Sheath of Bliss

The causal or bliss body. The subtlest sheath, closest to the Atman. In deep sleep (dreamless) the other four koshas are absorbed into the Anandamayi Kosha; the person rests in undifferentiated bliss (ananda) without knowing it consciously. The Self (Atman) remains undaunted by pleasure and pain, undisturbed by the activity of the grosser koshas, dwelling always in the bliss sheath's protection.

The Anandamayi Kosha is veiled by *divine maya* (the creative power that produces the appearance of multiplicity). Neither prana nor mind can reach it; consciousness alone can pierce it.

Yogic practices primarily addressing this kosha: *samadhi* (absorption).

## The Self Beyond the Koshas

The Atman — pure Self — lies beyond all five koshas. It is *sat-chit-ananda* (being-consciousness-bliss) in its essence, neither born nor dying, neither created nor destroyed. The purpose of Yoga is to progressively remove the identification with each kosha and realise the Self as the unchanging witness behind all five sheaths.

Johari maps this using the Yoga teaching: the Annamayi Kosha is removed through asana; Pranamayi through pranayama; Manomayi through pratyahara and dharana; Vijnanamaya through dhyana; and the Anandamayi Kosha is dissolved in samadhi, leaving only the Atman.

## Relationship to the Chakra System

The koshas and the [chakra system](chakra-system.md) are complementary anatomies rather than alternatives. The chakras describe the energy centres along the Sushumna channel; the koshas describe the layers of existence through which those energy centres operate. A fuller Tantric picture integrates both: the chakras describe the landscape through which Kundalini moves; the koshas describe the levels of the being that each practice must address for that movement to be safe and complete.

## Relationship to Western Energy Medicine

[Barbara Ann Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md)'s [seven-layer auric field](seven-layer-auric-field.md) model parallels the kosha system in spirit: both describe progressively subtler "bodies" interpenetrating the physical. Brennan's seven layers (etheric through ketheric template) map roughly onto the five koshas, though from a different tradition and without the Tantric soteriological framework. Neither Brennan nor [Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) uses the kosha terminology.

Brennan's [four-dimensional model](four-dimensions-of-being.md) — physical, HEF, haric, core star — also rhymes with the kosha model's progression from gross to subtle, with the core star parallel to the Anandamayi Kosha or the Atman itself.

## Critique

**From Vedanta vs. Tantra**: The kosha model is primarily Vedantic (following Shankaracharya's interpretation of the Taittiriya Upanishad). Some Tantric schools integrate it differently, emphasising the body as sacred vehicle rather than as layers to be transcended. Johari uses the model pragmatically without dwelling on this tension.

**From Western psychology**: The five-kosha model pre-dates modern psychology but maps partially onto it: Annamayi ≈ soma, Manomayi ≈ ego/emotions, Vijnanamaya ≈ higher cognitive functions, Anandamayi ≈ the unconscious substrate. These parallels are suggestive, not exact.
