# Kundalini

**Summary**: The dormant divine energy coiled at the base of the spine in Hindu-Tantric philosophy — when awakened through Yoga, it rises through the seven chakras to the crown, uniting Shakti with Shiva-consciousness and producing liberation; a term adopted and reinterpreted in Western energy medicine.

## Overview

Kundalini (Sanskrit: *kundal*, coil; *Kundalini Shakti*, coiled power) is the divine feminine energy understood in the Hindu-Tantric tradition to lie dormant at the base of the spine, coiled three and a half times around the Svayambhu Lingam within Muladhara Chakra. In its dormant state it sustains the organism's ordinary functioning; when awakened through yogic practice it ascends the Brahma Nadi inside Sushumna, piercing each of the six chakras in turn and ultimately uniting with Shiva-consciousness at Sahasrara (the crown) — an event described as samadhi, liberation (moksha), and the goal of the entire Yoga system.

The primary source in this wiki for the traditional Kundalini teaching is [Harish Johari](../thinkers/harish-johari.md)'s [*Chakras: Energy Centers of Transformation*](../sources/chakras-energy-centers.md).

## Traditional Description

### Nature and Symbolism

Johari draws on numerous Tantric scriptures to characterise Kundalini:
- **Shankaracharya** (*Saundarya Lahari*): Kundalini Shakti pierces the six chakras step by step and enters the abode of Shiva
- **Mundamalatantra** (ch. 6): Kundalini Shakti is the force of the body
- **Varahopanishad** (5.51): Kundalini is the supreme power
- **Yajurveda**: Kundalini as energy that "moves like a wife" and destroys evils in its movement

Three states of Kundalini (from the Yajurveda/Tantric sources): (1) virgin state — coiled and resting; (2) sleeping form — begins movement and emits a slow sound; (3) illuminated — enters the cave of Brahma (Brahma Nadi) and unites with Shiva.

Kundalini is simultaneously:
- **Saguna** (with attributes): the Divine Mother, the Great Goddess, personalised as Shakti
- **Nirguna** (without attributes): the power of will-consciousness (iccha shakti), pure energy preceding manifestation

### Relationship to Consciousness

In Tantric cosmology, the universe is made of two principles: Shiva (pure consciousness, unmoved) and Shakti (creative energy, dynamic). The individual's Kundalini is Shakti in microcosm — the cosmic creative power operating within the human body. Arousing Kundalini is not merely a physiological event; it is a return of individual energy to its cosmic source.

## The Three Granthis (Knots)

Before Kundalini can rise freely through Sushumna, three energetic knots (granthis) must be pierced:

1. **Brahma Granthi** (Muladhara–Manipura region): the attachment of consciousness to the material world — to names, forms, and meanings; the trap of the creative mind (*maya*)
2. **Vishnu Granthi** (Anahata region): the attachment of consciousness to preservation — to compassion, service, love for the world; the trap of the caring heart
3. **Rudra Granthi** (Ajna region): the attachment to I-consciousness (*ahamkara*) — the subtle ego that remains even after the grosser attachments are dissolved; the final trap before liberation

Each granthi corresponds to one of the principal deities: Brahma (creator), Vishnu (preserver), Rudra/Shiva (destroyer). Untying each reveals a progressively subtler dimension of consciousness.

## Arousal Methods

Johari describes several interlocking methods for awakening Kundalini, all requiring preparation through the eight-limbed Ashtanga Yoga path:

- **Pranayama**: breath control, particularly suspension (kumbhaka); the fusion of prana (incoming) and apana (downward-moving) in the navel region creates heat that rouses the sleeping Kundalini
- **Bandhas**: Mula Bandha (perineal lock), Uddiyana Bandha (abdominal lock), Jalandhara Bandha (throat lock) — together they redirect prana into Sushumna
- **Mudras**: Khechari Mudra (tongue folded back, blocking the air passage) sustains the aroused Kundalini and prevents the descent of soma nectar; Shambhavi Mudra (gazing inward at Ajna Chakra)
- **Mantra japa**: repetition of bija (seed) mantras, particularly the AUM and the specific bija of each chakra; sound activates the relevant energy
- **Concentration (dharana)**: fixing attention successively on each chakra, its yantra, its deity, and its bija mantra

Johari cautions that teachers and texts warn against premature or unguided arousal of Kundalini. Without adequate preparation (ethical conduct, physical purification, mental steadiness), the rise of Kundalini can produce physical, emotional, and psychological disruption rather than liberation.

## Five Movements of Kundalini

As Kundalini ascends, its movement varies with the element of the chakra being traversed:
1. **Serpent movement** (earth/Muladhara) — slow, sweeping, sinuous; felt as a current in the spine
2. **Fish movement** (water/Svadhishthana) — flowing, gentle, wave-like; felt in the heart region
3. **Bird movement** (fire/Manipura) — fluttering, oscillating; felt as light and sensation in the spine
4. **Monkey movement** (air/Anahata–Vishuddha) — jumping, leaping; erratic, not earth-bound
5. **Akasha movement** (beyond elements/Ajna–Sahasrara) — instantaneous; no sense of movement; the storm that ascends to the centre in no time

## Kundalini and Liberation

When Kundalini reaches Sahasrara, the yogi attains asamprajnata samadhi — consciousness beyond consciousness, where knower, knowing, and known dissolve into undivided awareness (sat-chit-ananda: being-consciousness-bliss). After this realisation Kundalini may descend back to Muladhara, restoring the faculties and powers of each chakra's presiding deities. The yogi then lives in *sahaja samadhi* — ordinary life experienced as liberated awareness.

## Kundalini in the Western Energy-Medicine Context

[Caroline Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) mentions kundalini briefly in [*Anatomy of the Spirit*](../sources/anatomy-of-the-spirit.md) as dormant spiritual energy that rises especially in women approaching menopause, illuminating unresolved biographical issues. This is a therapeutic reframing — kundalini as catalyst for healing rather than route to liberation.

[Barbara Ann Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) does not use the term kundalini systematically in [*Hands of Light*](../sources/hands-of-light.md) or [*Light Emerging*](../sources/light-emerging.md), though the [core star](core-star.md) concept parallels the Tantric understanding of Kundalini's ultimate source.

The Western energy-medicine use of chakras has largely decoupled the system from its Kundalini context. Johari's book reinstates that context.

## Campbell's Comparative Account: Ramakrishna and Western Art

[Joseph Campbell](../thinkers/joseph-campbell.md) devotes the central section of [*The Mythic Image*](../sources/the-mythic-image.md) to Kundalini yoga as a universal psychological map, reading it alongside Western mysticism and art. His primary experiential source is Sri Ramakrishna (1836–1886), whose first-person account of Kundalini ascent is the most detailed available in English:

> "The Spiritual Current rises along the spine, crawling like an ant. Sometimes I experience a sensation like that of a fish gliding in water. Sometimes it seems to me as though a monkey were jumping from branch to branch... Sometimes the Current moves like a bird hopping from one branch to another. Sometimes again it moves like a snake, going in zigzag fashion; and as it reaches the head, I go into samadhi."

Ramakrishna's five movements (ant, fish, monkey, bird, serpent) correspond to the five elements of the ascending chakras (earth, water, fire, air, space). Campbell also quotes Ramakrishna's detailed phenomenological account of his first full Kundalini experience: the Current entering the Sushumna nerve at the base of the spine, the drooping lotuses rising and opening as it touched them in sequence, and the final opening of the thousand-petalled lotus with the resultant samadhi.

Campbell's most striking comparative evidence for Kundalini's universality is the **caduceus** — two serpents entwined around a central staff — which he shows appearing identically as:
- The libation cup of Gudea of Lagash, Sumer, 2000 BC (serpent god Ningishzida as two entwined vipers around a staff)
- Indus Valley seals, 2000 BC
- Greek Hermes/Mercury's caduceus (guide of souls) and Asklepios's healing serpent
- Mesoamerican feathered serpent columns (Quetzalcoatl)
- Native American engraved shell disks (Mississippi Culture, 1200–1600 AD)

The two entwined serpents are ida and pingala; the central staff is sushumna. The universality of the symbol across civilisations separated by thousands of miles and years suggests either a shared Bronze Age cultural matrix or (as Jung's patient mandalas showing spontaneous Kundalini imagery suggest) a universal psychological structure that operates independently of cultural transmission.

## Ring's Kundalini-NDE Hypothesis

[Kenneth Ring](../thinkers/kenneth-ring.md) proposes in [*Heading Toward Omega*](../sources/heading-toward-omega.md) (1984) that the near-death experience (NDE) is biologically mediated by the arousal of kundalini energy — offering the most explicit Western empirical engagement with kundalini outside the Yoga tradition.

### The Hypothesis

Ring argues that during cardiac arrest, drowning, or other near-death events, the disruption of breathing may trigger a kundalini response. The kundalini surge then mediates the NDE itself (the tunnel, the light, the consciousness expansion) and, once activated, continues to reshape the person's nervous system and experiential life. Ring draws on Joseph Dippong's formulation: "in instances where a person is deprived of his supply of oxygen, the life energy, kundalini, will actually rush to the brain in order to sustain life."

### Post-NDE Symptoms as Evidence

Ring documents post-NDE symptoms in his Omega Study subjects that parallel clinical descriptions of kundalini awakening:

- **Heat sensations in the hands and body**: Multiple subjects report heat radiating from hands; Barbara Harris's husband confirmed her hands were burning to the touch
- **Energy movement along the spine**: Barbara Harris describes a tingling current moving from the toes up through the legs, base of spine, chest, and head in a "cascade"
- **Light experiences in waking life**: Nel's account ("Light Energy Cycle") describes surges of brilliant light energy filling her cranium in ordinary waking states
- **Reduced sleep need**: Several NDErs report functioning well on 4 hours of sleep
- **Apparent cognitive enhancement**: Tom Sawyer reports accessing physics knowledge; Barbara Harris notes enhanced information absorption; Ed Rossiter reports enhanced comprehension and problem-solving
- **Healing capacities**: Multiple NDErs develop the ability to transmit healing energy through touch

### Comparison with Hiroshi Motoyama

Ring draws extensively on the parallel between his NDErs' accounts and Hiroshi Motoyama's kundalini awakening through prolonged Yoga practice. Motoyama's progression — energy rushing to the head, OBE states, encounter with brilliant light, expanded consciousness, subsequent healing and psychic development — parallels NDE phenomenology despite being induced through an entirely different mechanism.

### Caution

Ring acknowledges the hypothesis cannot be confirmed with available instruments. The symptoms he cites are consistent with multiple explanations (anxiety, temporal lobe sensitisation, the psychological impact of a transformative experience). He presents the kundalini-NDE link as "the most promising framework available" — suggestive, not established.

The hypothesis does, however, provide a bridge between the traditional Hindu-Tantric account of kundalini (Johari, Campbell, Gopi Krishna) and the Western empirical NDE literature — a bridge with significant implications for understanding both.

## Joy's Contribution: The Quickening

[W. Brugh Joy](../thinkers/w-brugh-joy.md) describes in [*Joy's Way*](../sources/joys-way.md) (1979) a phenomenon he calls the "quickening" — a spontaneous motor vibration and light experience that began several weeks after meeting his teacher Eunice Hurt (1971) and continued at intervals over several years. Joy provides one of the most detailed Western first-person accounts of a spontaneous Kundalini-like arousal outside formal Yoga practice.

The progression Joy documents:

- **Initial phase**: Waking in the night feeling the bed shake; on investigation, the building was not moving — his own body was vibrating. Pulse normal. The sensation resembled a motor running in the chest.
- **Intermediate phase**: The vibration became centred in specific areas (chest, neck, forehead); associated with flashes of light in the visual field, both in meditation and falling asleep.
- **The November 1976 event**: Six hours of sustained vibration described as a "Diesel truck" shaking the body, centred in the chest and spreading to the abdomen and head — accompanied by continuous non-flash light. The following morning Joy felt rested; his capacity to see energy fields appeared to increase significantly afterward.

Joy explicitly connects this to the Kundalini phenomenon described in Gopi Krishna's *Kundalini: Evolutionary Energy in Man* (1971) and in Alice Bailey's esoteric teachings. He regards it as the arising of what he calls the "Fire of Transformation" or Kundalini energy — spontaneously activated, without deliberate yogic practice, through his association with an expanded-consciousness teacher and the opening of his meditation practice.

His experience parallels Ring's NDE-Kundalini link: arousal not through Yoga but through proximity to expanded states (the teacher's induction field and sustained meditation). Joy regards the quickening as a developmental marker — not the goal, but a sign that chakra development is reaching a new level. He warns against seeking it as an end in itself and against pathologising it as cardiac arrhythmia or restless legs syndrome.

## Critique

**From mainstream science**: No peer-reviewed neuroimaging or physiological study has identified Kundalini as a distinct biological structure or documented its described ascent. Accounts of Kundalini awakening (including physical sensations, visions, altered states, and crisis) overlap with descriptions of neurological events, psychotic episodes, and meditative altered states — none of which confirms the Tantric metaphysical interpretation.

**From within Yoga traditions**: Different Yoga schools (Raja Yoga, Jnana Yoga, Bhakti Yoga) offer alternative paths to liberation that do not require deliberate Kundalini arousal. Patanjali's *Yoga Sutras* do not mention Kundalini explicitly. The Tantric focus on Kundalini as the primary mechanism is one school's emphasis, not the universal Hindu position.

**Intercultural concern**: Western popular presentations of Kundalini often strip away the ethical preparation (yama/niyama), the requirement for a qualified teacher, and the cosmological context, reducing it to a technique for spiritual experience. Johari consistently warns against this.
