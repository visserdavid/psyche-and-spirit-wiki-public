# M-1/M-2/M-3 Metaphysics

**Summary**: Willis Harman's three-fold taxonomy of fundamental metaphysical positions — materialistic monism (M-1), dualism (M-2), and transcendental monism (M-3) — maps the philosophical terrain of the relationship between consciousness and matter; introduced in *Global Mind Change* (1988) and adopted by Barbara Ann Brennan as the explicit theoretical foundation of her holographic healing model.

## The Three Positions

[Willis Harman](../thinkers/willis-harman.md) proposed that the entire history of Western thought about the relationship between mind and matter can be organised into three fundamental positions:

### M-1 — Materialistic Monism

**Core claim**: Matter (energy) is the fundamental stuff of the universe. Mind, consciousness, and experience are produced by matter — specifically by brain processes — and have no independent existence.

**Implications**:
- Consciousness is an epiphenomenon: it accompanies brain activity but does not cause it
- There is no purpose or direction in evolution; organisms are products of mutation and selection
- Consciousness does not survive bodily death
- ESP, remote viewing, and non-local healing are impossible in principle
- Knowledge comes only through the senses (possibly extended by instruments)
- Illness has material causes (viruses, genes, toxins); emotions may correlate with illness but do not cause it in any primary sense

**Historical dominance**: M-1 became the working metaphysic of Western science from Descartes onwards. The "ten unspoken assumptions of conventional science" Harman lists in *Global Mind Change* are all M-1 premises. It is not that scientists chose M-1; they absorbed it as the background framework of their culture and education.

### M-2 — Dualism

**Core claim**: Both mind and matter are real, but they are fundamentally distinct substances. They interact (the mind can affect the body; physical events affect consciousness) but neither reduces to the other.

**Historical presence**: The Cartesian res cogitans (thinking substance) and res extensa (extended substance). Also implicit in most religious frameworks that affirm the soul's independence from the body.

**Implications for healing**: Mind and body can influence each other (the basis of psychosomatic medicine and biofeedback); spiritual experience is real without being reducible to neuroscience. Most mind-body medicine operates from M-2 premises — acknowledging mental causation without claiming that matter is consciousness.

**Harman's assessment**: M-2 avoids the explanatory gap of M-1 (how does epiphenomenal consciousness "do" anything?) but introduces a new problem: how do two completely different kinds of substance interact at all?

### M-3 — Transcendental Monism

**Core claim**: Consciousness is the fundamental stuff of the universe. Matter and the physical world are manifestations within consciousness — not the reverse. This is sometimes called "idealism" in philosophy.

**Historical presence**: M-3 is the oldest and most widespread view. Harman argues it is the shared metaphysics of virtually all wisdom traditions: Vedanta ("the universe is Brahman/consciousness"), Buddhism ("phenomena arise in mind"), Western mysticism (Meister Eckhart, Plotinus), shamanic worldviews, and many indigenous cosmologies. The perennial philosophy is an M-3 document.

**Implications for healing**:
- Health originates at the level of consciousness and intention; illness manifests physically because of prior disruptions at the consciousness level
- Mind can causally affect matter, including at a distance (non-local healing; prayer)
- The placebo effect is not a confound to be eliminated but an instance of consciousness creating biological reality
- Near-death experiences, ESP, and channelling are all coherent on M-3 premises
- Healing operates "from the inside out": changing the consciousness/intention level changes the body

**Modern science parallels**: Harman cites Karl Pribram's holographic brain model (the brain projects rather than receives reality) and David Bohm's implicate order (consciousness and matter unfold together from a deeper level) as instances where M-3 thinking has re-entered physics.

## The Copernican Analogy

Harman's central thesis is that the shift from M-1 to M-3 dominance is underway — and is comparable in scale and consequence to the Copernican revolution of the 16th century. See [Consciousness Paradigm Shift](consciousness-paradigm-shift.md) for the full argument.

Copernicus did not intend heresy; his reordering of the cosmos dethronement the Earth from the centre of the universe. The comparable dethronement today is of matter from its assumed position as the ground of reality. Just as the Copernican heresy prevailed despite the authority structure it challenged, Harman argues M-3 will prevail despite the institutional weight of M-1 science.

## Application to Healing

[Barbara Ann Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) adopts Harman's taxonomy explicitly in *Light Emerging*, Chapter 3, as the philosophical foundation of her [holographic healing model](holographic-healing-model.md):

> "Old scientific mechanistic models of health care are based on M-1 metaphysics: the idea that mind comes from matter contains a set of premises that have served us well for an age."

Brennan argues:
- M-1 medicine works well for acute conditions and physical trauma
- M-1 medicine systematically fails for chronic conditions, lifestyle diseases, and meaning-crises — because these have their primary cause at the consciousness level
- Healing that works at the [haric level](hara-level.md) (intentionality) and [core star](core-star.md) (divine essence) operates from M-3 premises: the healer accesses the consciousness level from which the physical level is shaped

The practical implication: the healer's metaphysical frame matters clinically. Working from M-1 premises excludes the causal level most relevant to healing. Working from M-3 opens access to it.

## Relationship to Other Wiki Frameworks

**[Dossey's Era III Medicine](non-local-consciousness.md)**: Dossey's framework maps onto M-1/M-2/M-3 directly. Era I (mechanical medicine) = M-1. Era II (mind-body medicine) = M-2. Era III (non-local healing; prayer at a distance) = M-3.

**[Myss's biography-is-biology](biography-is-biology.md)**: The biography-is-biology thesis requires M-2 at minimum (emotions causally affect the body) and points toward M-3 (the Sacred Contract — a pre-birth intention — shapes the biography, which shapes the biology; this requires consciousness preceding matter).

**[Two Paradigms of Medicine](two-paradigms-of-medicine.md)**: The allopathic/holistic divide in Myss and Shealy's framework maps onto M-1 vs M-2/M-3. Allopathic medicine assumes M-1 causation; holistic medicine requires at minimum M-2.

**[Sacred Contracts](sacred-contracts.md)**: Pre-birth soul agreements require M-3: if consciousness is primary, it can shape the conditions of embodied life before birth. On M-1 premises, Sacred Contracts are incoherent.

**[ACIM](../sources/a-course-in-miracles.md)**: *A Course in Miracles* is a thoroughgoing M-3 document: the physical world is "a dream of the mind"; illness is a projection of beliefs; healing is a shift in perception that changes the physical manifestation.

**[The Four Bardos](bardos.md)**: [Sogyal Rinpoche](../thinkers/sogyal-rinpoche.md) in *[The Tibetan Book of Living and Dying](../sources/tibetan-book-of-living-and-dying.md)* presents the Tibetan Buddhist bardo framework as an M-3 cosmology with a detailed map of what happens as the M-1 structure (the physical body) dissolves at death. The element-dissolution sequence in the bardo of dying (earth → water → fire → air → space) describes consciousness progressively withdrawing from its grosser manifestations toward its ground in pure awareness. The bardo of dharmata — in which mind's energy appears as sound, colour, and radiant light before condensing again into form in the bardo of becoming — is an M-3 event: the display of consciousness-as-primary, without material substrate. Sogyal explicitly invokes Bohm's implicate/explicate order to map this: the explicate order (ordinary physical reality) corresponds to the bardos of life and becoming; the implicate order (the undivided whole) corresponds to Ground Luminosity and Rigpa; the bardos of dying and dharmata are the domain of transition between them. From the M-1 standpoint, none of this is coherent; from the M-3 standpoint, it is a precise phenomenology of the ground-level of reality.

## Critique

**The taxonomy is philosophically simplified**: Professional philosophers of mind have mapped the field more finely (eliminative materialism, property dualism, panpsychism, idealism, neutral monism). Harman's three categories are designed for breadth and clarity, not precision.

**M-3 is unfalsifiable within M-1 science**: If consciousness is fundamental, then all physical evidence — including negative evidence for any particular healing claim — can be accommodated by the framework (wrong consciousness level, collective unconscious contribution, etc.). This makes M-3 practically unfalsifiable through the methods science currently uses.

**The wisdom-tradition consensus overstated**: Harman claims M-3 is universal across wisdom traditions. Buddhist schools differ significantly on whether consciousness is primary or whether consciousness and phenomena co-arise. The "perennial philosophy" of M-3 agreement requires selective reading.

**Note**: Mainstream science continues to operate from M-1 assumptions. The M-3 framework is the philosophical presupposition of energy medicine and holistic healing — a working assumption, not an established scientific finding.
