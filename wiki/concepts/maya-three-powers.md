# Maya: Three Powers

**Summary**: The Vedantic teaching that the universe is sustained by maya — the divine power of illusion/measurement — which operates through three interlocking modes: Veiling Power (hiding the Self), Projecting Power (generating the appearance of a separate world), and Revealing Power (the redemptive function of art, scripture, and meditation that dissolves the veil and allows the Self to shine).

**Sources**:
- [Campbell, J. (1974). *The mythic image*. Princeton University Press.](../sources/the-mythic-image.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-24

## Overview

The Sanskrit term **maya** (from the root *ma*, to measure or to form) refers to the divine power that creates, projects, and sustains the phenomenal universe — the world of names, forms, and relationships as apparently separate from consciousness. In the Vedantic and Tantric traditions, maya is not mere illusion in the sense of non-existence, but a real creative power: the universe is real *as maya*, real as a projected display, even though it is not independently real in the way the ego assumes.

[Campbell](../thinkers/joseph-campbell.md) places maya's three-power analysis at the centre of *The Mythic Image*, using it to explain both the source of human suffering (Veiling and Projecting powers) and the purpose of mythological imagery and art (Revealing Power). This framework provides a metaphysical account of why symbolic and mythological engagement is not a luxury but a necessity — the principal means by which consciousness retrieves itself from its own projected entanglement.

## The Three Powers

### 1. Veiling Power (*Avarana Shakti*)

The Veiling Power is the power that hides the true nature of the Self (*Atman*) and of consciousness (*Brahman*). Campbell quotes from a Sanskrit text: "Hidden by the veiling power of the Self, the Self shines not."

Characteristics of the Veiling Power:
- Produces the sense that the ego is the real and final self — rather than the surface expression of a deeper ground
- Generates the sense of separateness between self and world, observer and observed, inside and outside
- Is the root of *avidya* (ignorance) — not false information but a structural occlusion: the light is present but the lens is opaque

In clinical terms, the Veiling Power is the inner mechanism that allows biographical experience to be received as absolute fact rather than as one passing configuration of a deeper field of awareness. It is what makes illness feel like identity — the person *is* the wound, rather than *experiencing* it against a background of wholeness. Myss's [woundology](woundology.md) describes the Veiling Power's operation in the therapeutic context.

### 2. Projecting Power (*Vikshepa Shakti*)

The Projecting Power generates the world of apparent objects and their relationships. Campbell's example from Sanskrit: "Having concealed by the Veiling Power, the Projecting Power sends forth impressions, ideas, and desires."

The prism analogy: the Projecting Power is like a prism that splits white light into the spectrum of apparent colours. Set those seven colours on a spinning disk and they resolve back to white — the prism's reality is a real physical event, but the colours are not separately existent entities with the status the eye grants them.

In Hindu mythology, the Projecting Power is associated with:
- **Lakshmi** in her generative aspect: the goddess of beauty and abundance whose presence *in* the world keeps consciousness bound to the world's charms
- **Kali** in her destructive aspect: the goddess who dissolves what the Projecting Power has generated
- The **serpent** Ananta ("Unending"): the infinite projecting ground on which Vishnu dreams

In psychological terms (as Jung would recognise), the Projecting Power is what generates the *persona* — the face one presents — and the *shadow* — the hidden counter-face. It is why the world appears populated by exactly the qualities one has denied in oneself. Projection, in the technical Jungian sense, is a specific application of the Vedantic Projecting Power.

### 3. Revealing Power (*Prakasha Shakti*)

The Revealing Power is the redemptive mode: the function of maya that allows the veil to be lifted. Campbell identifies three principal vehicles of the Revealing Power:

1. **Art**: Images that speak from the ground of consciousness rather than to the ego's preferences — the lotus, the rose window at Chartres, the mandala, Blake's engravings. These are vehicles of Revealing Power because they communicate what cannot be stated — they point beyond their surface to the source from which they arise.

2. **Scripture**: Mythological narratives read poetically rather than literally — not as historical records but as maps of inner territory. The Osiris legend, the Krishna birth story, the Gospel accounts: when read as myth rather than as history, they reveal the patterns of the unconscious rather than asserting facts about the outer world.

3. **Meditation**: The direct yogic practice of stilling the Projecting Power by steadying the fluctuations of the mind (*chitta vritti nirodha*, in Patanjali's formulation). As the projections subside, the Veiling Power is deprived of its support and the light of the Self begins to shine through.

## The Goddess as Vehicle of Revealing Power

Campbell's most striking application is his reading of the Goddess — Tara, the Madonna, Prajnaparamita, Shri Lakshmi — not as objects of worship (which would reinforce the Projecting Power's apparent reality) but as the Revealing Power in person.

The Virgin Mary window at Chartres, the Buddhist Tara, the Javanese Queen Dedes as Prajnaparamita — all function identically: they are sensory forms so saturated with the quality of ground consciousness that prolonged contemplation of them begins to dissolve the ego's identification with the projected world and allows a glimpse of the light from which they and everything else arise.

This is the same logic as [Myss's symbolic sight](symbolic-sight.md): learning to see through the outer event or symptom to the deeper pattern it carries. The outer image is the vehicle; the Revealing Power is the destination.

## Eve and Mary as Projecting and Revealing Powers

Campbell discusses the Christian mythological pair Eve-and-Mary as a precise enactment of the Projecting and Revealing poles of maya:

- **Eve** in the garden closes the door of paradise by engaging the full Projecting Power — she and Adam are expelled into the world of labour, time, and death, the fully projected reality of the ego
- **Mary** (whose name, reversing "Eva," gives "Ave") reopens the door — she is Gate of Heaven, Mystical Rose, Revealing Power in person; through her the divine enters the projected world and the projected world is given a window back to its source

The medieval Catholic hymn "Ave Maris Stella" (Hail, Star of the Sea) encodes this: "Changing Eva's name... bind us in peace." The narrative of the Fall and Redemption is, in Campbell's reading, a mythological description of maya's three powers and their resolution.

## Relevance to Healing

The three-powers framework offers a rigorous metaphysical account of what healing requires:

1. **Naming the Veiling Power** is the first step of any healing: recognising that one's sense of being *only* the wounded, ill, depleted self is a veil — the deeper Self remains whole. This is what Myss calls the [cellular energy bank](cellular-energy-bank.md) (the reserve always available below the level of depletion) and what Brennan calls the [core star](core-star.md) (the divine essence never damaged by biography).

2. **Working with the Projecting Power** — which in therapeutic terms means recognising what the illness or symptom is projecting (what biographical material it is externalising as physical experience). This is the work of Symbolic Sight and the clinical application of the chakra system.

3. **Activating the Revealing Power** — moving from seeing symptoms as facts to seeing them as symbolic communications; using art, imagery, meditation, and mythological amplification to dissolve the Veiling Power's opacity and restore the experience of wholeness that underlies even serious illness.

## Tensions and Critique

**From mainstream science**: Maya's three powers describe a metaphysical structure that cannot be directly verified or falsified. The claim that all phenomenal experience is a projection of divine consciousness is a philosophical position, not an empirical finding. The healing claims that follow from this metaphysics (e.g., that recognising maya's operation could accelerate recovery) lack controlled trial evidence.

**From Christian theology**: The identification of Eve with Projecting Power and Mary with Revealing Power, while philosophically elegant, does violence to the traditional theological reading in which Eve's sin is a historical event with cosmic consequences, not a mythological symbol. Campbell's "Moon Grammar" reading treats all religious narrative as inner psychology, which orthodox traditions across all faiths would reject.

**From within the Advaita tradition**: Some Advaita teachers argue that maya is not so much a power operating *on* consciousness as a mistaken *identification* of consciousness — there is no actual veiling of the Self, only a misidentification. In this reading, maya's three powers are a pedagogical device, not a description of a cosmic mechanism.
