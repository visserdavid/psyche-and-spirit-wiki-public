# Miracles (ACIM)

**Summary**: In *A Course in Miracles*, a miracle is not a supernatural event but a correction — a shift of perception from the ego's world of sin, guilt, and fear to the Holy Spirit's world of forgiveness and love; miracles occur naturally as expressions of love and require no special effort, only the willingness to see differently.

**Sources**:
- Schucman, H. (1976). *A course in miracles*. Foundation for Inner Peace.

**Last updated**: 2026-06-25

---

## The Fifty Miracle Principles

Chapter 1, Section 1 of the Text opens with fifty principles that define what a miracle is. Selected principles:

1. **"There is no order of difficulty in miracles."** — All miracles are equally available; the mind that believes one problem is harder than another is simply investing in the reality of problems themselves.
2. **"Miracles occur naturally as expressions of love."** — They are the mind's natural output when it is not blocked by fear. "The real miracle is the love that inspires them."
3. **"Miracles are healing because they supply a lack."** — They correct the only real lack: the failure to perceive one's own completeness.
4. **"Miracles are everyone's right, but purification is necessary first."** — They require the willingness to relinquish the ego's perspective.
5. **"Miracles collapse time."** — They compress the learning that would otherwise require thousands of years of experience.
6. **"The miracle is a service."** — It is performed by those who temporarily have more for those who temporarily have less.
7. **"Miracles should not be under conscious control."** — Consciously selected miracles can be misguided. The Holy Spirit chooses which miracles are appropriate.

## What a Miracle Is

In ACIM's usage, a miracle is a shift in perception — specifically, the shift from seeing a brother as guilty, sinful, or attacking to seeing him as innocent, dreaming, and deserving of love. The miracle is always a correction in the mind, not a change in the external world. The external world may change as an effect, but it is never the primary target.

The Course states: "A miracle is a correction. It does not create, nor really change at all. It merely looks on devastation, and reminds the mind that what it sees is false."

This definition separates ACIM's miracle concept sharply from its popular usage (an unlikely fortunate event). In ACIM, every act of genuine forgiveness is a miracle; every moment the mind chooses the Holy Spirit over the ego is a miracle.

## Miracles and Healing

Because the Course locates all illness in the mind's decision to believe in separation and guilt, miracles — which correct that belief — are inherently healing. The Text states: "Miracles are healing because they supply a lack; they are performed by those who temporarily have more for those who temporarily have less."

This parallels the distinction between [healing vs. curing](healing-vs-curing.md): the Course is not primarily concerned with whether the body improves, but with whether the mind relinquishes its investment in sickness as a way of demonstrating that it has been wronged or punished.

The Manual for Teachers states: "Healing is accomplished the instant the sufferer no longer sees any value in pain." Sickness, in ACIM's framework, is always a decision — not a deliberate conscious choice, but a choice made in the unconscious mind that can be rescinded when brought to awareness.

Note: Mainstream medicine does not accept the premise that illness is a decision of the mind. The ACIM framework is metaphysical, not clinical. Applied literally, it risks victim-blaming — an objection also levelled at [biography is biology](biography-is-biology.md). The Course is careful to distinguish unconscious decision from blame: it is not saying the ill person "wanted to be sick."

## Miracles and Time

One of the more striking miracle principles is that miracles collapse time: they substitute for the learning that would otherwise require long painful experience. This is consistent with the Course's broader teaching that time itself is a product of the separation — an illusion — and that the Holy Instant (the experience of stepping outside sequential time) is the miracle's vehicle.

See [Holy Instant](holy-instant.md).

## Miracles vs. Magic

The Course distinguishes miracles from magic. Magic is the attempt to solve problems at the level of the world — drugs, therapies, behaviours — without correcting the underlying misperception. Miracles address the cause (the mind's false beliefs); magic addresses the effect (the symptom in the world). This does not make the Course anti-medicine; rather, it argues that even effective physical treatment is not complete healing unless accompanied by the mind's correction.

## Relation to Other Wiki Concepts

The ACIM miracle is functionally similar to [spontaneous remission](spontaneous-remission.md) in the mind-body literature: an unexpected reversal that exceeds the predictive scope of ordinary models. Dossey's prayer research documents correlates; ACIM offers the metaphysical account of the mechanism. Where Dossey grounds miracles in non-local consciousness and Bell's theorem, the Course grounds them in the mind's release of false perception.
