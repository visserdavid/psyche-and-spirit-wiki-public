# Near-Death Experiences

**Summary**: Clinical accounts and research findings on the near-death experience (NDE) — a consistent cross-cultural phenomenon in which people clinically dead or close to death report leaving their bodies, moving through a tunnel toward light, encountering a Supreme Being of Love, undergoing a panoramic life review, and returning permanently changed; systematically researched by Kenneth Ring (the Omega Study) and integrated by Borysenko as evidence of a loving universe and as the ultimate context for forgiveness and meaning.

## The Phenomenon

The near-death experience (NDE) is reported by people who have been clinically dead or close to death and subsequently revived. Raymond Moody's *Life After Life* (1975) was the first systematic collection of NDE accounts; subsequent researchers — Kenneth Ring, [Melvin Morse](../thinkers/melvin-morse.md), Michael Sabom, P.M.H. Atwater, and others — have documented the phenomenon across cultures, age groups, and belief systems.

According to a 1981 Gallup poll, approximately 5% of the American population — roughly 12–15 million people — had had a near-death experience by that date. The figure has likely increased as resuscitation technology has improved, creating more survivors of clinical death. [Andrew Greeley's](../thinkers/andrew-greeley.md) surveys document that the majority of NDE survivors never disclose their experience, fearing ridicule or pathologising.

[Joan Borysenko](../thinkers/joan-borysenko.md) integrates NDE research across two books. In [*Guilt Is the Teacher, Love Is the Lesson*](../sources/guilt-is-the-teacher.md), NDEs provide the largest temporal context for the forgiveness work: the life review demonstrates that love and knowledge are the only things that transfer through death; unforgiveness costs nothing to the forgiven and everything to the forgiver. In [*Fire in the Soul*](../sources/fire-in-the-soul.md), the NDE accounts of Donna Elsten and Melvin Morse's children provide richly detailed evidence that the universe is, in Einstein's formulation, a friendly place.

## Consistent Elements

Researchers have documented a consistent structure to NDE accounts across cultures, belief systems, ages, and historical periods:

1. **Separation from the body** — the person observes their own body from above, often reporting verifiable details of the resuscitation room, conversations of medical staff, or events occurring in other parts of the building

2. **Movement through a tunnel** — passage through a dark tunnel or corridor toward a distant light

3. **Entry into light** — a light perceived as radiating warmth, love, and intelligence; experienced as personal

4. **Meeting beings of light** — guides, deceased relatives, or what Moody calls the "Supreme Being of Light" — a luminous presence experienced as the source of all love and knowing

5. **The life review** — a panoramic re-experiencing of the person's entire life, often occurring in the presence of the Supreme Being, in which the emotional impact of one's actions is felt *from the perspective of those one affected* — feeling what one caused others to feel

6. **The return** — often unwilling; the person is told they must return, or makes a choice to return based on responsibilities (children, relationships, unfinished tasks)

7. **Permanent transformation** — the NDE is not a temporary altered state; it produces lasting changes in values, relationships, and fear of death

## Donna Elsten's Account

Donna J. Elsten's two NDEs (both occurring when her heart stopped during surgery) provide the most detailed account in Borysenko's work. Donna kept written notes:

*Entering the light:* "I am experiencing affection at a level I have never experienced before. I am sending out affection and it is coming back to me at this same time. The affection I feel is not of the body. I understand... I feel a feeling of total acceptance. I am loved... weight of life has been lifted from my shoulders."

*Meeting the Supreme Being:* Donna encounters God face-to-face. "God makes a gesture holding out His hand... We touch. There is a bond — an emotional bond... I have been gifted with the capacity to understand, to grasp knowledge, to have understanding. I have the ability that I never had before... I am at the Source of all things... God is forgiving... it is not that God forgives, it is that God *forgives*." [italics in original]

*The experience of eternity:* "I am not a prisoner of death. There is no death. Death does not exist. I'm not afraid of death. I can see the miracles of life. I love life. I will treasure it... life is to be treasured."

*The choice to return:* Donna had difficulty leaving eternity to return to the body. Her appreciation for and reverence for life — described as a feature of all NDEs — remained permanently.

## Melvin Morse: Children's NDEs

Pediatrician [Melvin Morse](../thinkers/melvin-morse.md) (author of *Closer to the Light: Learning from the Near-Death Experiences of Children*) provides Borysenko's most compelling NDE research. Children who have NDEs are significant subjects precisely because they:
- Have less cultural conditioning about what death "should" be like
- Are less likely to have formed ideological frameworks that could shape the account
- Are less subject to medication effects at the time of the experience than elderly patients

Morse's case of Terry, a child who entered a coma after eating painkillers, is representative. As an adult, Terry described leaving her body, travelling through a tunnel, and entering a landscape:

> "There was a tunnel. The tunnel branched. I passed through an arch and emerged into... light. It was called the light. It represented love, peace, happiness, joy. I emerged from the experience and reported seeing 'pieces of the light.' I could see how the world fits."

Morse reports that of the children he studied who had NDEs, none used drugs or alcohol as teenagers — "understanding at some level that substances dim the light." The NDE functioned as a permanent immune factor against the drive toward numbing.

## The Life Review: Love and Knowledge as the Measure

The most psychologically significant element of the NDE for Borysenko's framework is the life review. Consistent across accounts:

**Panoramic**: the entire life is seen at once, in full detail, not linearly but simultaneously

**Bi-directional empathy**: the experiencer re-lives their actions not from their own perspective but from the perspective of those they affected. Actions that caused pain are re-experienced as the pain of the recipient; actions that extended love are re-experienced as the joy of the recipient

**Non-judgmental witnessing**: the Supreme Being does not judge; there is no condemnation. The life review is conducted in a context of total love and understanding. The person judges themselves through the experience of having felt what they caused

**The two criteria**: consistently across NDE accounts, people return knowing that when they die, two things will matter: how much they loved, and how much they grew in understanding. Material success, status, achievement, wealth — none of these appear in the life review as significant

The businessman who returned from an NDE and wept at a flower — "I am now part of one living universe; to hurt any living thing is to hurt myself" — exemplifies the post-NDE shift. The life review creates permanent first-person knowledge of interconnection rather than merely second-hand belief in it.

## Heaven, Hell, and the Long Spoons

Borysenko cites a teaching story that appears in various traditions: everyone in both heaven and hell has long spoons attached to their arms that cannot bend. In hell, people starve, unable to get the food to their mouths. In heaven, they feed each other. Heaven and hell are not places but *states of relationship* — the presence or absence of the compassionate turn toward the other.

NDE accounts consistently depict an afterlife that matches this: not punitive judgment but the natural consequence of the quality of love one cultivated in life. People who lived from fear and self-protection re-experience, in the life review, the isolation their choices created; people who lived from love re-experience the web of connection they wove.

## Implications for the Psychology of Illness

For Borysenko, NDE research has three clinical implications:

1. **Dissolving fear of death** — the primary fear that drives avoidance, denial, and the contraction of life is the fear of annihilation. NDE research, while not constituting proof of afterlife, documents that the experience of dying is not terrifying but transcendent for the vast majority of experiencers. Clinical work with dying patients changes when this data is held

2. **The life review as motivational frame** — if love and knowledge are the only things that matter at the moment of death, the present moment's quality of love and understanding becomes the only relevant measure of a life. This dissolves certain forms of success-anxiety and amplifies certain forms of relational commitment

3. **Non-local consciousness** — the verified-detail accounts (experiencers accurately reporting events in rooms they were not present in, conversations they could not have heard) challenge the premise that consciousness is generated by and confined to the brain. If consciousness can observe from outside the body, the materialist framework for understanding illness, healing, and suffering requires revision

Note: mainstream neuroscience proposes alternative explanations for NDE features — temporal lobe activation, REM intrusion during resuscitation, hypoxia, endorphin release during dying. These explanations are not mutually exclusive with the experiential accounts; the neuroscience may describe the mechanism while the spiritual interpretations describe the meaning. Borysenko does not resolve this ambiguity, acknowledging that the question of what NDEs ultimately *are* remains open.

## Kenneth Ring's Systematic Research: The Omega Study

[Kenneth Ring](../thinkers/kenneth-ring.md) is the primary NDE researcher in this wiki. His two-book programme — *Life at Death* (1980) and [*Heading Toward Omega*](../sources/heading-toward-omega.md) (1984) — forms the empirical backbone of NDE research.

**Life at Death (1980)** established three foundational findings:
- The invariance hypothesis: NDEs occur regardless of the circumstances that bring a person close to death (illness, accident, suicide attempt) and regardless of demographic background (age, sex, religion, race)
- Approximately 35–48% of near-death survivors report NDEs (consistent across Gallup, Sabom, and Ring's own samples)
- The five-stage structure of the NDE (peace → body separation → entering darkness → seeing light → entering light)

**The Omega Study (1984)** documented what happens to people *after* their NDE — the systematic, corroborated transformation detailed in [NDE Transformation](nde-transformation.md):

### Incidence

Based on Gallup's 1982 survey, approximately 5% of the American adult population — roughly 8 million people by 1982 — had experienced an NDE. As resuscitation technology has improved, this number has increased.

### The Core NDE: Sixteen Elements

Ring maps 16 elements of the deepest form of the experience (the "core NDE"):

1. Speed of approach and acceleration toward the light
2. The light — brilliant, white, non-blinding, perceived as aware and personal
3. Sense of the light as a presence or being
4. Love — overwhelming, unconditional, all-embracing
5. Total acceptance
6. Forgiveness — absolute, effortless
7. Sense of homecoming and recognition ("I was where I belonged")
8. Communication — not verbal; direct transfer of understanding
9. Knowledge of universal principles imparted
10. Life review — panoramic, simultaneous, from perspective of those affected
11. Knowledge that only love and growth matter
12. Music of surpassing beauty
13. Environments of surpassing beauty
14. Cities of light (a subset of experiencers)
15. Profound yearning to remain — the return experienced as loss
16. Return reluctant or compelled

Tom Sawyer's formulation captures the encounter with the light: "It's not a person, yet it has being, character, and something... size covers a vista. It engulfs the horizon... the light communicates... it's instant. Doesn't matter the language spoken... Relax... you're put at ease... it's a feeling of love. It's THE feeling."

Joe Geraci: "It becomes you, you become it. It's just — peace and love and brightness part of... I mean it's just — knowing."

### Aftereffects: What the Research Shows

Ring's multi-instrument Omega Study (26 NDErs completing the full questionnaire battery; 172 on the Religious Beliefs Inventory) documents consistent, corroborated changes:

**Value shifts** (Life Changes Questionnaire):
- Appreciation for life and others: strong increase
- Concern for and love of others: strong increase
- Materialism and status concern: decrease
- Quest for meaning and consciousness: strong increase

**Spiritual universalism** (Religious Beliefs Inventory, 172 respondents): 49% of NDErs qualify as "universalism shifters" vs. 15% of those with no death experience (χ² p < .0005). NDErs shift toward: felt closeness to God, deemphasis of formal religion, openness to reincarnation, belief in the unity of all religions.

**Psychic development** (Psychic Experience Inventory): 58% of Omega NDErs report increases in psi phenomena. Confirmed by Greyson's independent study (80 IANDS NDErs, p < .0001) and Kohr's ARE study (84 NDErs vs. 105 death survivors vs. 358 others).

Third-party corroboration: Ring's Behavior Rating Inventory (observers who knew the NDEr before the experience) shows a modal 8 of 11 items confirmed as changed, reducing concern that self-reports are retrospective reframing.

For Ring's explanation of *why* NDEs produce these transformations, see [Omega Hypothesis](omega-hypothesis.md) and [NDE Transformation](nde-transformation.md).

## Cross-Links to Other Wiki Frameworks

**[Closet Mystics](closet-mystics.md)**: The NDE is the most dramatic form of the mystical experience that Andrew Greeley's surveys document as widespread. The 5% NDE rate (12–15 million people by 1981) is the upper end of a spectrum of mystical experience that includes contact with deceased loved ones, visions, and ESP — all reported by large minorities of the general population.

**[Forgiveness](forgiveness.md)**: The life review is the ultimate context for the forgiveness teaching. If one will re-experience, at death, everything one caused others to feel, then unforgiveness — the harbouring of resentment — becomes literally self-inflicted suffering, experienced in full at the moment of greatest lucidity. Conversely, acts of forgiveness and love create what the experiencer will carry into that final review.

**[Spiritual Optimism](spiritual-optimism.md)**: NDE accounts constitute the strongest empirical argument for [spiritual optimism](spiritual-optimism.md) available in the literature — more viscerally compelling than theological arguments or philosophical frameworks. People who have died and returned universally report: the universe is not punitive; love is the fundamental reality; knowledge and love are what matter.

**[Crisis as Initiation](crisis-as-initiation.md)**: The NDE is the extreme case of the initiation structure — the most complete death-and-rebirth experience possible. Stanislav Grof's perinatal Stage 4 (see [Crisis as Initiation](crisis-as-initiation.md)) is structurally parallel to the NDE: ego death, blinding light, experience of liberation, return transformed.

**[Centering Prayer](centering-prayer.md)**: Thomas Keating's description of the contemplative's encounter with the divine presence in centering prayer — entering the light, resting in pure being, the dissolution of the ego's grip — uses the same phenomenological vocabulary as NDE accounts. The mystic's path and the NDE survivor's experience arrive at overlapping descriptions of the same reality.

## Critique

**Scientific status**: NDE research is observational and retrospective. The most rigorous studies (Pim van Lommel's prospective NDE study, AWARE study by Sam Parnia) have documented verified perceptions but have not established a scientific explanation for out-of-body accuracy. The brain-based explanations (temporal lobe, hypoxia, REM intrusion) are biologically plausible but do not account for the verified perceptual details.

**Selection effects**: Borysenko's sources (Moody, Morse, Ring) were pioneers who selected compelling cases. The full range of dying experiences — including cases with no positive features — may not be represented in published NDE literature.

**Cultural filter**: The form NDEs take shows some cultural variation (the light is experienced as Jesus by Christians, as ancestors by Native Americans, as the Buddha by Buddhists). This may suggest the NDE is filtered through cultural templates, which neither confirms nor denies its ultimate nature.

**Trauma**: A minority of NDEs are reported as terrifying rather than loving. These are underrepresented in the popular literature and deserve equal attention; their existence complicates any simple claim that NDEs always support spiritual optimism.

## The Tibetan Buddhist Perspective

[Sogyal Rinpoche](../thinkers/sogyal-rinpoche.md) in *[The Tibetan Book of Living and Dying](../sources/tibetan-book-of-living-and-dying.md)* (1992) dedicates a full chapter to NDE research as Western corroboration of the bardo teachings. His reading: the "being of light" and the overwhelming love reported by NDE experiencers correspond to the [Ground Luminosity](ground-luminosity.md) — the Clear Light of the fundamental nature of mind that dawns at the moment of death in Tibetan Buddhist teaching.

The specific correspondence Sogyal draws:
- The "being of light" = [Ground Luminosity](ground-luminosity.md) or early bardo of dharmata appearances
- The life review = karma's mirror, the mind reviewing its habitual tendencies
- The overwhelming peace and love = the natural quality of [Rigpa](rigpa-nature-of-mind.md) when unveiled
- The resistance to returning = recognition of what was obscured throughout ordinary life
- The permanent transformation = the lasting effect of having briefly touched Ground Luminosity

Sogyal does not claim NDEs are identical to the full bardo traversal; the NDE is understood as a partial glimpse — the dying person touches Ground Luminosity briefly before returning to the body. Ring's Omega Study findings (value shifts, spiritual universalism, loss of death fear) are consistent with what the Tibetan teachings predict would follow from such a glimpse.
