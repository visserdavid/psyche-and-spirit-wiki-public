# Non-local Consciousness

**Summary**: The theory that consciousness is not confined to individual brains, bodies, or moments in time — it is non-local in the sense that physicist John Bell's theorem proved for subatomic particles — with implications for prayer, healing at a distance, intuitive diagnosis, and the soul.

## The Central Claim

Non-local consciousness (NLC) is Larry Dossey's theoretical proposal that mind — the seat of thought, intention, imagery, and prayer — is not produced by, confined to, or coextensive with individual brains and bodies. Consciousness, on this view, is not a private property of persons separated by distance and time; it is in some sense unified or at minimum non-locally correlated across persons and across moments.

NLC is the theoretical cornerstone of Dossey's Era III medicine. It explains why:
- Prayer can affect patients who are unaware they are being prayed for
- Healers can affect organisms (bacteria, cancer cells, mice) at distances of 15 miles
- Intuitive diagnosis (as practised by [Caroline Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md)) can operate 1,200 miles from the patient
- Random event generators can be retroactively influenced by observers who encounter the data later

See [Healing Words](../sources/healing-words.md) and [Meaning & Medicine](../sources/meaning-and-medicine.md) for Dossey's full development of Era III medicine.

## Physics Grounding: Bell's Theorem

The theoretical anchor is **Bell's theorem** (John Stewart Bell, 1964) and the experiments it generated. Classical physics required that distant objects be independent — no faster-than-light influence, no "spooky action at a distance." Bell proved mathematically that any locally realistic theory (one in which correlations between distant particles are explained by shared local properties, not genuine non-local connections) makes predictions incompatible with quantum mechanics. Subsequent experiments (Aspect et al., 1982; many successors) confirmed the quantum prediction: particles that have interacted remain correlated regardless of subsequent separation. Change the spin of one and the other changes instantaneously — non-locally, with no signal travelling between them.

Nick Herbert (*Quantum Reality*, 1985) states: "Bell's theorem requires our knowledge of the world to be non-local." Herbert also notes that this non-locality does not permit sending information — the correlations are only visible retrospectively. Dossey takes both features seriously:

- **Non-locality without signal transmission**: prayer does not "send" anything across space. It is not a radio wave that weakens with distance. Consistent with the experimental finding that healing effects do not diminish over distance.
- **Non-locality visible retrospectively**: the PEAR Lab's random event generator experiments show that persons can retroactively correlate with output that was generated before they engaged with it. Time-displaced prayer is thus coherent in principle.

Whether Bell's theorem, established at the subatomic scale, applies to minds at the human scale is not proven. Dossey acknowledges this gap; physicist Erwin Schrödinger's dictum — "Mind is *natura*, singular" — is his strongest support for scaling up.

## Evidence from Healing Research

Multiple lines of experimental evidence support NLC as a working hypothesis:

**1. Distant healing effects**
Daniel J. Benor's survey of 131 controlled healing experiments found effects in roughly half — on bacteria, yeast, fungi, plants, animals, and humans — with effects occurring at distances from inches to 15 miles and undiminished by physical barriers. Standard forms of energy (ELF radiation, electromagnetic fields) cannot explain this pattern; they attenuate with distance and are blocked by shielding. See [Prayer and Healing](prayer-and-healing.md).

**2. Transpersonal imagery (Braud and Schlitz)**
William G. Braud and Marilyn Schlitz (Mind Science Foundation, San Antonio): 13 experiments showing one person's calming or activating imagery measurably alters another isolated person's electrodermal activity — from a separate room with no physical contact and no subject awareness. Effect did not diminish across building distances.

**3. Telesomatics**
Documented cases (and systematic evidence from Louisa Rhine's 169-case analysis) where one person experiences physical symptoms, emotions, or knowledge at the moment another person — usually someone emotionally close — is in danger or distress. Mothers who experience burning sensations the moment their daughters burn their hands in chemistry class miles away; the bleed-synchrony between a woman and her daughters in premature labour. Myers proposed love and empathy as the mechanism; physics would call it non-local correlation between formerly interacting systems.

**4. The PEAR Lab**
Princeton Engineering Anomalies Research Laboratory (Robert G. Jahn, Brenda J. Dunne): over 2.5 million trials showing that individuals — and especially emotionally bonded pairs — can influence random event generators beyond chance. Effects: (a) work at global distances; (b) work *retroactively* (influencing outputs already generated but not yet observed); (c) are larger for bonded couples than for individuals.

**5. Retroactive effects (Schmidt)**
Physicist Helmut Schmidt (Mind Science Foundation): subjects could influence the output of REGs retroactively — events already taped but not yet observed. Psychologist Hans Eysenck and researcher Carl Sargent estimated the likelihood of Schmidt's results by chance at a million to one. Ray Hyman (prominent critic of parapsychology) acknowledged Schmidt's work confronted methodological criticisms in parapsychology head-on and was not easily dismissed.

**6. Intuitive diagnosis**
[Caroline Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) diagnosed patients from 1,200 miles away, given only their name and birthdate, with an estimated 93% accuracy ([Norman Shealy](../thinkers/norman-shealy.md)'s assessment, documented in *The Creation of Health* and cited by Dossey in both *Meaning & Medicine* and *Healing Words*). See [Medical Intuition](medical-intuition.md).

## Why Consciousness Rather Than "Energy"

Healers commonly describe sending "healing energy" — and the popular literature speaks of energy fields, energy transfer, and energy medicine. Dossey argues this language is imprecise and theoretically inconsistent with the evidence:

- Known forms of energy (electromagnetic, gravitational, acoustic) all follow an inverse-square law — they weaken with distance.
- Healing effects in controlled experiments do not weaken with distance.
- Healing effects pass through lead-lined rooms and electromagnetic shielding.
- Healing effects work retroactively — something energy cannot do.

The evidence better fits a non-local consciousness model than an energy transmission model. NLC does not require "sending" anything; it operates through correlation and resonance, not transmission. See [Energy Anatomy](energy-anatomy.md) for comparison with Brennan's auric field model, which does use energy language — the tension between these frameworks is unresolved.

## Implications

**For medicine**: If consciousness is non-local, then physician beliefs, intentions, and emotional states are not background noise — they are therapeutic agents. Physician "hanging crepe" (predicting death) may function as nocebo: the physician's consciousness can harm. See [Meaning and Health](meaning-and-health.md) and [Voodoo Death](voodoo-death.md).

**For prayer**: Prayer does not "go" anywhere. It does not need to reach God like a radio signal. It is an invitation — an alignment of the pray-er's consciousness with the Absolute — that allows non-local correlations to manifest. The "Thy will be done" approach honours this structure better than outcome-directed petition.

**For the soul**: Non-local consciousness has implications for personal identity and survival of death. If consciousness is not produced by, and therefore not destroyed with, the brain, then the death of the body does not necessarily extinguish consciousness. Dossey calls this the "Radical Cure" — the realisation that part of consciousness does not die is the ultimate antidote to the fear of death as the motivator of compulsive illness prevention.

**For cosmology**: NLC bridges science and religion at a point that neither can own. If it is confirmed, it constitutes scientific support for the claim — made by every major wisdom tradition — that consciousness is fundamental, not derivative. This is what Brennan's holographic healing model (via Harman's M-3 metaphysics) and Dossey's Era III medicine both claim, arriving from different directions. See [Holographic Healing Model](holographic-healing-model.md).

## Critique

The critical objections are serious:

**The scale problem**: Bell's theorem proves non-locality at the subatomic scale. Scaling it to human consciousness requires bridging quantum physics and neuroscience — a bridge that does not currently exist. Schrödinger's aphorism is poetic, not scientific.

**The evidence problem**: The controlled experiments supporting distant healing are real but controversial. The file-drawer problem (unpublished negative results skewing the literature toward positive findings) is a persistent concern. Meta-analyses of prayer studies in particular have found inconsistent and frequently null results (STEP, 2006). Braud-Schlitz and Schmidt's work has been criticized for methodological weaknesses and insufficient independent replication.

**The mechanism problem**: Even if distant healing effects are real, "consciousness is non-local" is a description, not an explanation. No coherent physical or biological mechanism has been proposed. Dossey himself acknowledges (Chapter 12) that no existing theory adequately accounts for the phenomena.

**Mainstream neuroscience position**: The dominant scientific consensus holds that consciousness is produced by brain activity — a position consistent with all ordinary clinical and experimental neuroscience. NLC would require abandoning this assumption, which is not justified by current evidence.

**Note**: Non-local consciousness as described by Dossey is a speculative framework — intellectually serious, empirically motivated, and consistently contested. It should not be treated as established science, but it should not be dismissed as mere speculation either.

## Holographic Grounding (Talbot / Bohm)

Talbot's *The Holographic Universe* provides the physical framework that Dossey invokes but does not fully develop. David Bohm's [implicate order](implicate-order.md) explains non-locality mechanistically: separated particles (and, by extension, separated minds) are not truly separate — they are aspects of one enfolded holomovement. Their correlation is not a signal between them but the explicate expression of their unbroken implicate connection. Alain Aspect's 1982 experiment (Paris Institute of Applied Optics) is the definitive empirical demonstration: two photons, separated by 6.5 metres with analyser settings switched in 10 nanoseconds, maintained instantaneous polarisation correlation — ruling out any known signal transmission.

Talbot extends this: the PEAR lab's REG and pinball experiments show that human consciousness correlates with random physical processes at distances up to global scale, and even retroactively — an empirical grounding for Dossey's retroactive prayer. See [The Holographic Universe](../sources/the-holographic-universe.md) for the full evidence survey.
