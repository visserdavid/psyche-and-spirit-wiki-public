# Personal Power

**Summary**: The third chakra's domain — the development of self-esteem, individuation, and inner authority independent of external approval or tribal identity — and its central role in biological health.

**Sources**:
- [Myss, C. (1996). *Anatomy of the spirit: The seven stages of power and healing*. Harmony Books.](../sources/anatomy-of-the-spirit.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

---

## Overview

Personal power in Myss's framework refers to the third chakra (solar plexus) energy of self-esteem, inner authority, and the capacity to act from one's own values rather than from fear, approval-seeking, or tribal obligation. It is the bridge between the tribal identity of chakra 1 and the relational life of chakra 2, and the gateway to the heart's domain.

The sacred truth of chakra 3 is "Honour Oneself." Its development is the central task of individuation — becoming a distinct self whose sense of worth is not dependent on others' validation.

## The Solar Plexus: Body and Power

The third chakra governs the solar plexus region: stomach, pancreas, adrenals, small intestine, gallbladder, liver, and mid-spine. [Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) describes this centre as the "centre of gravity" of the personality — the place where group energy (chakra 1) and relational energy (chakra 2) are internalised and processed into individual identity.

Illnesses arising from third-chakra depletion include:
- Stomach ulcers, gastritis
- Pancreatic disorders (including cancer)
- Liver and gallbladder conditions
- Adrenal fatigue
- Eating disorders, diabetes
- Chronic fatigue related to people-pleasing and over-responsibility

## Self-Esteem and Health

Myss argues that self-esteem is not a psychological luxury but a **biological necessity**. Without it, the body lacks the energetic foundation it needs to maintain health. Treatments fail when the patient has no power in their own recovery — as illustrated by the woman with depression and neck pain who sought Shealy's help. No technique could succeed because her entire sense of self was vested in controlling her children. When they grew and left, she lost her power base.

The development of genuine self-esteem is the work of a lifetime, and Myss describes it as unfolding through four stages:

### Stage 1: Revolution
Discovering that one has opinions, values, and needs distinct from the tribe's. The first acts of individuation — voicing disagreement, making a choice that differs from the family's expectation — are small revolutions. They require courage because they risk the tribe's disapproval.

### Stage 2: Involution
Turning inward to investigate what one actually believes, wants, and needs. The decade of psychotherapy became mainstream in the 1970s because the culture entered this stage collectively. Self-examination leads to awareness of the gap between who one has been (tribal self) and who one could become (individual self).

### Stage 3: Narcissism
The 1980s cultural phase in which the self-focus of individuation became self-indulgent. Myss treats this as a developmental stage — necessary but incomplete. The culture (and individuals) learned that self-focus alone does not produce happiness or health.

### Stage 4: Evolution
The integration of self-knowledge with service and engagement with the world. The self becomes a vehicle for contributing to others, not the end-point of development. This stage marks the mature third chakra — confident enough in its own worth to give freely without fear of depletion.

## The Case of Penny

Penny's story illustrates the four stages in one life: she spent seventeen years in a marriage that systematically undermined her autonomy (she was kept semi-conscious through alcohol that her husband provided). When she finally hit bottom and entered AA, she began the revolution (Stage 1). Sobriety initiated the involution (Stage 2). She built a new career, changed her appearance, moved to a new city, and remarried — the evolution (Stage 4). Her health remained stable throughout, because each step reclaimed energy she had been pouring into the relationship.

## Confirmation as Self-Endorsement

The sacrament of Confirmation corresponds to the third chakra. Myss reads it as the act of endorsing one's own existence — saying "I am" with conviction, independent of others' approval. The religious ceremony of confirmation marks a coming-of-age; in Myss's framework, this is an ongoing process: every time we stand by our own values or make a choice that honours our own truth rather than managing others' reactions, we "confirm" ourselves.

## Sefirot: Nezah and Hod

The sefirot Nezah (endurance) and Hod (majesty/integrity) pair at the level of chakra 3. They represent:
- **Nezah**: The stamina to maintain one's commitment to development, especially under pressure. The ability to stay the course.
- **Hod**: The dignified integrity that allows transcendence of limitation and connection to Divine authority.

Together they suggest: self-esteem is not arrogance; it is the combination of quiet endurance and dignified presence.

## Critique

**The psychological parallel**: Myss's four-stage model loosely mirrors developmental psychology (particularly Erikson's stages of psychosocial development and Maslow's hierarchy of needs). The recognition that self-esteem is a developmental achievement, not a given, is consistent with developmental research.

**The causal claim**: Linking specific solar plexus illnesses (pancreatic cancer, stomach ulcers) directly to depleted self-esteem or entrapment is not supported by controlled medical research. While chronic stress and low perceived self-efficacy do influence physiological outcomes, the specific illness-esteem correspondences Myss proposes are not established.

**Note**: Mainstream medical consensus does not support the claim that pancreatic cancer or stomach ulcers arise primarily from deficits in self-esteem. While psychological factors affect overall health, no direct causal link between specific personality patterns and specific cancers has been conclusively established.

A non-clinical parallel outside Myss's own chakra system: [Zukav](../thinkers/gary-zukav.md)'s [authentic power](authentic-power.md) — alignment of personality with soul, as against externally-validated "external power" — describes a structurally similar move away from approval-seeking, though at the level of the whole personality rather than a single chakra.
