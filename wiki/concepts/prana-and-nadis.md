# Prana and Nadis

**Summary**: The Hindu-Tantric system of life force (prana) and its subtle energy channels (nadis) — the invisible anatomy that underlies and animates the physical body, determines which chakra is active at any moment, and provides the pathway through which Kundalini ascends to liberation.

## Overview

In the Hindu-Tantric system described by [Harish Johari](../thinkers/harish-johari.md), the physical body is animated by **prana** — life force or vital energy — distributed throughout the organism via an invisible network of subtle energy channels called **nadis**. Prana is not identical with breath (though breath is its primary vehicle); it is the energy that creates and sustains life in matter, mind, and consciousness. The nadi system is the transport infrastructure of prana, and its proper functioning is the physiological basis for the chakra system, Kundalini ascent, and all Yoga practice.

## The Five Pranas

The texts describe prana as differentiated into five main currents (vayus) by function and location:

| Prana | Location | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Prana** | Chest region | Inhalation; taking in nourishment; governs lungs and heart |
| **Apana** | Pelvic/intestinal region | Expulsion downward: excretion, urination, ejaculation, delivery; also opposes prana in the Kundalini arousal fusion |
| **Samana** | Navel region | Digestion and assimilation; distributes nutrients throughout the body |
| **Vyana** | Throughout the body | Circulation; coordinates all systems; governs blood flow, sweating, motor function |
| **Udana** | Throat and head | Upward-moving; speech, swallowing; during Yoga, carries Kundalini upward; governs the ascent of consciousness at death |

Five additional minor pranas are also named: Naga (belching/hiccupping), Kurma (blinking/vision), Krikala (sneezing/hunger), Devadatta (yawning/laziness), Dhananjaya (pervades the body; remains after death and causes decomposition).

The fusion of Prana (incoming) and Apana (downward) in the navel region — produced through pranayama and bandhas — generates the heat that rouses the sleeping Kundalini at Muladhara Chakra. This prana-apana fusion is the physiological trigger for the awakening of [Kundalini](kundalini.md).

## The Nadi System

Johari, following the Tantric scriptures (Shiva Samhita, Yoga Shikhopanishad), identifies 72,000 nadis in the body — subtle channels through which prana flows. These are not nerves (Sanskrit: *snayu*) or blood vessels; they are a parallel subtle-body anatomy. Two types are distinguished: *pranavahi* (prana-carrying) nadis and *manovahi* (mind-carrying) nadis.

### The Fourteen Principal Nadis

Fourteen nadis are named as most significant, of which three are pre-eminent:

| Nadi | Character | Path and Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Sushumna** | Central; nature of fire; tamas | Passes through the spinal column (meru danda); the central channel of Kundalini ascent; contains three inner channels |
| **Ida** | Lunar; cool; Ganga river; manovahi | Left side; terminates in left nostril; nature of moon; calming, sattvic; conserves energy |
| **Pingala** | Solar; hot; Yamuna river; pranavahi | Right side; terminates in right nostril; nature of sun; activating, rajasic; consumes energy |
| Gandhari | — | Left eye |
| Hastajihva | — | Right eye |
| Yashasvini | — | Right ear, from right toe |
| Pusha | — | Right ear |
| Alambusha | — | Mouth, from anus |
| Kuhu | — | Genitals, from throat |
| Shankhini | — | Left side, from anus |
| Sarasvati | Camphor color; lunar | Tongue, from kanda; when purified, the yogi speaks truth |
| Payasvini | — | Right ear |
| Varuni | — | Torso; removes apana toxins |
| Vishvodara | — | Naval region; aids digestion |

### Sushumna's Inner Structure

The Tantric texts (Lalita Sahasranama) describe Sushumna as containing three nested channels:
- **Vajrini Nadi**: within Sushumna; nature of sun; rajasic (active/passionate)
- **Chitrini Nadi**: within Vajrini; nature of moon; sattvic (luminous/clear); governs dreams, visions, hallucinations
- **Brahma Nadi**: within Chitrini; nature of void (akasha); the actual passage of Kundalini; connects the Brahma Randhra (the void between the brain hemispheres, at Sahasrara) to Muladhara

When Kundalini rises, it travels through Brahma Nadi — not through the outer Sushumna or the nerves.

### The Junction of the Three Nadis

Ida, Pingala, and Sushumna originate together at a region called the *kanda* (near Muladhara) and interweave up the spine. The point where the three rivers meet is called **Yukta Triveni** (combined three streams). In the Indian tradition this corresponds to the confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna, and subterranean Sarasvati rivers at Prayagraj — considered the most sacred bathing site.

At the level of Ajna Chakra, Sushumna bifurcates: one branch goes toward Brahma Randhra, the other continues as fire element. Ida and Pingala terminate at the two nostrils.

## Svara Yoga: The Science of Nasal Breath

**Svara Yoga** is the traditional science of reading the nasal breath cycle to understand which nadi, element, and chakra are dominant at any given moment. Johari draws on the *Shiva Svarodaya* and other texts:

- In ordinary life, the breath alternates between left nostril (activating Ida Nadi) and right nostril (activating Pingala Nadi) approximately every 90 minutes
- In each hour, about 890 breaths pass through one nostril or the other; approximately 10 breaths per hour are taken through both nostrils simultaneously — activating Sushumna
- The element dominant in each nasal breath cycle determines which chakra is most active:
  - Earth element (right nostril dominant): 20 minutes per cycle
  - Water element (left nostril dominant): 16 minutes per cycle
  - Fire element: 12 minutes per cycle
  - Air element: 8 minutes per cycle
  - Akasha element: 4 minutes per cycle
- Dawn and dusk are natural Sushumna times — optimal for meditation

Yogic pranayama practices aim to deliberately activate Sushumna by equalising or suspending the breath, drawing Kundalini into the central channel.

## Relationship to Chakras

Each chakra is a major energy centre located where Sushumna passes through the spine. When Kundalini energy rises through Brahma Nadi, it enters each chakra in succession. The chakra ceases to function as an ordinary centre of ego-bound experience and instead becomes a centre of transformation: the element's energy is absorbed into Kundalini, and the presiding deity and Shakti are also absorbed, until only pure consciousness remains at Sahasrara.

Outside of Kundalini arousal, the chakras function as ordinary centres governed by the element dominant through the nasal breath cycle. The practitioner of Svara Yoga can thus observe which chakra-element is active and use this knowledge for meditation, health management, and daily decision-making.

## Relationship to Western Energy Medicine

[Barbara Ann Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) in [*Hands of Light*](../sources/hands-of-light.md) refers to energy channels (she calls them "energy cords" or describes field flows) but does not systematically map the nadi system. [Caroline Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) does not discuss nadis at all, focusing on chakra energy levels and their illness correlates rather than the subtle-body anatomy that underlies them.

The nadi system parallels the acupuncture meridian system of Chinese medicine: Johari explicitly notes that acupuncture meridians are included within the broader concept of nadis (as one type of subtle channel). [Norman Shealy](../thinkers/norman-shealy.md)'s [Five Sacred Rings](five-sacred-rings.md) work in acupuncture-point circuits maps partially onto this territory from a bioelectromagnetic direction, though without reference to the Tantric nadi framework.

## Critique

**From mainstream anatomy**: No anatomical dissection has revealed a nadi network distinct from the nervous or lymphatic systems. The descriptions of Sushumna as passing through the spinal cord are not confirmed by neuroscience, which identifies the spinal cord with a canal that contains cerebrospinal fluid, not the properties Johari describes.

**From biomedicine**: The five-prana typology does not correspond cleanly to known physiological systems, though rough parallels to the respiratory, digestive, circulatory, and nervous systems have been proposed.

**From Yoga research**: Modern yoga research (on pranayama effects, nasal cycle, and meditation) does confirm that nasal breathing patterns alter brain activity and autonomic function — lending some empirical ground to Svara Yoga observations, though the Tantric interpretation of these effects remains unconfirmed.
