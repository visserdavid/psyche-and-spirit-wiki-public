# Spirit Retrieval

**Summary**: Myss's practice of consciously recalling and redirecting life force energy from investments in past events, resentments, and wound narratives back to the present and to healing — the primary energetic act of releasing the past.

**Sources**:
- [Myss, C. (1997). *Why people don't heal and how they can*. Harmony Books.](../sources/why-people-dont-heal.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

---

## Overview

Spirit Retrieval is a core healing practice introduced in [*Why People Don't Heal*](../sources/why-people-dont-heal.md) by [Caroline Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md). It addresses the fundamental energy dynamic behind illness: that significant portions of a person's daily life force (the [Cellular Energy Bank](cellular-energy-bank.md)) are invested in past events — trauma, betrayal, grief, resentment — and cannot be mobilised for healing as long as they remain there.

## The Concept

Myss describes life force as mobile and directional: we "send" our spirit (energy, attention, intention) into past events each time we replay them, relive them, or allow them to define our identity. This energy does not return automatically. The person's current vitality is therefore reduced by the sum of all unrecalled investments in the past.

The act of Spirit Retrieval is to *deliberately call this energy back* — to consciously withdraw investment from past events and return it to the present moment and to the body's healing processes.

Myss's formulation: "Go back to every event your spirit is still visiting. Tell it to come home. Harvest this energy. Bring it into the present."

## Practice

Myss offers Spirit Retrieval as both a conceptual shift and a structured exercise:

1. **Identify the investments**: Become aware of which past events, relationships, or grievances are still receiving regular energy — events you return to mentally, people you still resent, losses you still actively mourn.
2. **Change the vocabulary**: Shift from present-tense dwelling on the past to past-tense recall: "That happened" rather than "That is still happening to me."
3. **Make the call**: A deliberate internal act — visualising energy returning from the places it has been sent, flowing back into the body's present-moment systems.
4. **Release with forgiveness**: [Forgiveness](five-myths-of-healing.md) — not as moral absolution of the other, but as the energetic act of ceasing to invest — is the mechanism by which retrieval is completed.

The practice does not require that the past event be forgotten, denied, or reframed as acceptable. It requires only the withdrawal of active energy investment: "It happened. I am no longer feeding it with my life force."

## Relationship to Woundology

Spirit Retrieval is the direct counter-practice to [woundology](woundology.md). Where woundology keeps energy lodged in wounds by using them as currency, Spirit Retrieval actively withdraws that energy. Myss argues that this is non-negotiable for genuine healing: as long as the wound is profitable (providing intimacy, identity, leverage), the energy needed to heal it will remain in the wound rather than in the body's repair processes.

## Shamanic Parallels

The concept of Spirit Retrieval echoes shamanic traditions in which illness is understood as soul loss — parts of the self becoming fragmented or stuck in past trauma — and healing requires the shaman's intervention to retrieve and reintegrate these lost soul parts. Myss does not extensively develop this parallel in *Why People Don't Heal*, but it places her framework within a cross-cultural tradition of understanding illness as energetic fragmentation. See [Spiritual Healing Traditions](spiritual-healing-traditions.md).

## Relationship to the Chakra System

Spirit Retrieval operates across the [Chakra System](chakra-system.md): each chakra domain has characteristic forms of past-investment:
- **First chakra**: loyalty debts to family and tribe; ancestral wounds
- **Second chakra**: sexual trauma; relationship betrayals; money losses
- **Third chakra**: humiliations, failures, self-esteem wounds
- **Fourth chakra**: grief, broken heart, unforgiven betrayals
- **Fifth chakra**: lies spoken or received; vows broken; words that wounded
- **Sixth–seventh chakras**: loss of faith; abandonment by God; meaninglessness

Chakra-specific visualisations in Chapters 7–8 of *Why People Don't Heal* are designed to facilitate Spirit Retrieval at each level.

## Critique

**From trauma therapy**: Mainstream trauma-informed care emphasises that premature retrieval or "letting go" of traumatic material can be retraumatising if done without adequate support, titration, and somatic processing. The instruction to simply "call energy back" from severe trauma may be inadequate or harmful for people in acute PTSD. Spirit Retrieval as Myss frames it appears most appropriate for biographical-level life investments rather than for acute traumatic stress.

**On the mechanism**: The concept assumes that energy behaves in ways that consciousness can direct — a metaphysical claim not supported by conventional neuroscience. However, the psychological insight underlying it (that sustained preoccupation with past events has real costs in attention, motivation, and stress hormone regulation) is empirically grounded even without the energy language.

**On forgiveness**: See [Five Myths of Healing](five-myths-of-healing.md) — the critique of forgiveness as a healing requirement, particularly for ongoing or unacknowledged injustice.
