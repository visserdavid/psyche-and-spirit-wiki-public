# Symbolic Sight

**Summary**: Myss's term for the learnable capacity to perceive life events, the body, and relationships in terms of their energetic and symbolic meaning — seeing beyond appearances to the spiritual lessons and power dynamics at work.

## Overview

Symbolic sight is [Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md)'s term for the mode of perception that underlies [medical intuition](medical-intuition.md) — and that she believes can be developed by anyone. It is the capacity to see the world not only with the five physical senses but with an awareness of energetic patterns, symbolic meanings, and spiritual dynamics.

Where ordinary perception asks "What is happening?" symbolic sight asks "What does this mean? What does this reveal about where power has been invested or lost? What truth is this challenge asking me to recognise?"

## The Journalist's Discipline

Myss traces symbolic sight to an insight she had from journalism: objectivity. Her journalism professor's teaching — that a good reporter stands back, seeks facts, and resists the pull of personal interpretation — became the foundational discipline of her intuitive work.

Symbolic sight is not mere intuition in the sense of gut feeling or emotional response. It requires detachment: the ability to receive information without being contaminated by one's own needs, fears, or desires. An impression contaminated by emotional charge is no longer pure information.

## How Symbolic Sight Works

At its core, symbolic sight involves:

1. **Shifting attention**: Moving from the literal, material level of events to the energetic/symbolic level. A conflict at work is also a teaching about power. A physical symptom is also a message from the body about where energy has been lost.

2. **Reading the seven truths**: Each life challenge can be interpreted through the [Seven Sacred Truths](seven-sacred-truths.md) framework. When facing a crisis, ask: which truth does this situation involve? What power lesson is present?

3. **Tracking energy**: Noticing where energy flows, where it feels blocked, where one feels depleted after encounters. Energy dynamics precede physical events.

4. **The first impression**: In reading others, the first image or impression that arises is the most accurate. The desire to check, modify, or second-guess it introduces distortion.

## Practical Guidance

Myss offers guidelines for developing symbolic sight:

- **Intention**: Begin with the genuine wish to see — to understand the meaning of challenges rather than simply to survive them.
- **Self-scanning**: Regularly evaluate one's own energy: which chakra feels most active? Where is depletion occurring? What life situation corresponds to that centre?
- **Finding the lesson**: For every difficulty, ask what truth is being activated. Which of the seven sacred truths is this experience addressing?
- **Tracking energy leaks**: Notice what consistently drains power. The pattern of depletion reveals the pattern of attachment.
- **Simplicity**: Healing does not require elaborate systems. Identify the one truth relevant to the current challenge and organise the healing process around it.

## The Impersonal Mind

In discussing the sixth chakra (third eye), Myss describes the goal of symbolic sight as developing an **impersonal mind** — the capacity to perceive without the distortion of personal identity, cultural conditioning, or ego investment. She cites examples from her own formation: a professor who made students spend a weekend in silence without clocks, asking them to answer the deepest questions of Christian, Buddhist, and Hindu thought without loyalty to any one framework. The lesson was that truth, at its deepest level, transcends all particular forms.

The Nearings (Scott and Helen), Sogyal Rinpoche, and other figures in the book are cited as individuals who achieved a quality of perception in which the boundary between the personal and the universal had become permeable.

## Symbolic Sight and the Body

A key application of symbolic sight is reading the body: understanding its symptoms not only as pathology but as symbolic statements. The body speaks the language of the energy system. Illness in the chest (chakra 4) speaks of love, grief, and the wounds of intimacy. Illness in the solar plexus (chakra 3) speaks of self-esteem and entrapment. A person who cannot speak their truth develops throat problems. A person who has not forgiven develops heart disease.

This interpretive approach is not meant to replace medical diagnosis but to add a dimension of meaning that facilitates deeper healing.

## Three Column Sight (*Why People Don't Heal*, 1997)

In [*Why People Don't Heal*](../sources/why-people-dont-heal.md), Myss introduces the **Three Column Sight** as the book's central practical tool. It organises the three forms of power described in the [Three Ages of Power](three-ages-of-power.md) into a working framework for interpreting any life challenge or healing crisis:

| Column | Form of Power | Age | Mode |
|--------|--------------|-----|------|
| Tribal | External/group | Aries | Family loyalty, collective belief, survival |
| Individual | Internal/self | Pisces | Choice, willpower, self-esteem, emotion |
| Symbolic | Archetypal/transcendent | Aquarius | Grace, archetypes, energetic meaning |

The practice: take any belief, pattern, or illness-related situation and examine it through all three columns simultaneously. Tribal beliefs (e.g., "illness is punishment") are identified and then elevated to Individual reframing (e.g., "illness involves factors I can influence") and then to Symbolic interpretation (e.g., "this illness is an invitation to discover something I could not have found another way").

Myss provides extensive lists of Tribal, Individual, and Symbolic beliefs about healing, and guides readers to identify where their personal energy is concentrated — which column holds the most authority — and to build bridges between columns through deliberate action and reflection.

The Three Column model is a precursor to the more fully developed Three Column Model of *Sacred Contracts*.

## The Three Column Model (*Sacred Contracts*, 2001)

In *Sacred Contracts*, Myss extends symbolic sight into a structured three-dimensional perceptual framework she calls the **Three Column Model**. It maps three simultaneous levels of reality that any life situation can be read through:

| Column | Plane | Chakras | Question |
|--------|-------|---------|----------|
| Left | Material / world | 1–3 | What is happening in my survival, relationships, and self-esteem? |
| Centre | Psychological / psyche | 4–7 | What does my heart, will, mind, and soul perceive? |
| Right | Sacred / Contract | 8th chakra | What does this reveal about my Sacred Contract and archetypal purpose? |

The three-column framework operationalises symbolic sight as a daily practice: any event, encounter, or body symptom can be "walked through" all three columns to yield insight at each level. The goal is to maintain all three perspectives simultaneously — not collapsing into the material, nor escaping into the purely spiritual.

The model also provides the interpretive lens for the [Archetypal Wheel](archetypal-wheel.md): each archetype-house pairing in the Chart of Origin is read through all three columns to reveal its full significance.

## Critique

**The language of meaning vs. causation**: Symbolic sight, at its most defensible, is a framework for meaning-making: it helps people understand their experiences in ways that facilitate reflection and change. As a meaning system, it can be genuinely useful regardless of whether the causal claims underlying it are correct.

**The literalism trap**: Problems arise when symbolic sight's interpretations are taken as literal causal claims — when "heart disease means you have not forgiven" becomes a judgment rather than an invitation. The interpretive framework can produce insight or guilt, depending on how it is applied.

**Scientific status**: There is no empirical method for testing whether symbolic sight is "accurate" in the way that medical diagnosis is accurate. Its value is experiential and interpretive, not measurable.
