# Tantric Chakra Attributes

**Summary**: The complete traditional Hindu-Tantric attribute set for each chakra — element, yantra, bija mantra, carrier animal, presiding deity, Shakti doorkeeper, sense/work organs, ruling planet, loka (cosmic plane), and psychological-behavioral characteristics — as documented by Harish Johari from primary Sanskrit sources; the foundational layer beneath Western energy-medicine uses of the chakra system.

**Sources**:
- [Johari, H. (2000). *Chakras: Energy centers of transformation* (Rev. expanded ed.). Destiny Books.](../sources/chakras-energy-centers.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-24

## Overview

The [chakra system](chakra-system.md) as used in Western energy medicine (by [Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md), [Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md), and others) retains the basic seven-chakra structure and some element correspondences but strips away much of the traditional Hindu-Tantric attribute system. [Harish Johari](../thinkers/harish-johari.md)'s [*Chakras: Energy Centers of Transformation*](../sources/chakras-energy-centers.md) restores this fuller picture from the primary Sanskrit sources.

Each chakra in the Tantric system is understood as a complete psycho-cosmic unit:
- A **yantra** (geometric diagram) representing its element
- A **bija mantra** (seed sound) encoding its energy
- A **presiding deity** embodying the consciousness principle of that level
- A **Shakti doorkeeper** — the specific form of Kundalini energy active at that chakra
- A **carrier animal** (vahana) for the bija mantra's energy
- A **loka** (cosmic plane) corresponding to the consciousness level
- **Behavioral characteristics** describing how a person dominated by that chakra's energy thinks, feels, and acts

## The Seven Chakras Plus Soma and Sahasrara

### Muladhara Chakra — First Chakra

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Sanskrit name | Muladhara (mula = root, adhara = support) |
| Location | Base of spine / coccyx |
| Element (tattva) | Earth (prithvi) |
| Yantra | Square, chrome-yellow; eight spears emanate from corners |
| Bija mantra | LANG (prithvi bija / aindra bija) |
| Carrier animal | Elephant Airavata (seven trunks = seven aspects of consciousness) |
| Petals | 4; colour: vermilion/blood-red; seed sounds: VANG, SHANG, KSHANG, SANG |
| Presiding deity | Brahma (creator; sattvik twilight energy; resolves insecurity and fear) |
| Shakti doorkeeper | Dakini (carries light of knowledge; head indicates consciousness dissolving) |
| Sense organ | Nose (smell) |
| Work organ | Anus |
| Prana vayu | Apana (downward expulsion) |
| Ruling planet | Mars (masculine) |
| Loka (plane) | Bhu Loka (physical plane) |
| Also contains | Svayambhu Lingam (self-born Shiva symbol); red inverted triangle (yoni/kamakhya); Kundalini coiled 3.5 times |

**Behavioral characteristics**: Security, survival, earth energy, strength, endurance. Dominant life stage: birth to age 7. This level person focuses on food, sleep, sex (basic survival needs), is self-centered, follows orders, needs authority, and is attracted to material security. Can become criminally strong if unguided. The cure is yama/niyama and moderation in basic needs.

**Meditation effects**: Freedom from disease, vitality, vigor, endurance, stability, security, power of levitation. Knowledge of the world arises and can be released when this chakra is fully absorbed into Kundalini.

### Svadhishthana Chakra — Second Chakra

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Sanskrit name | Svadhishthana (sva = self, adhishthana = abode) |
| Location | Genitals / lower abdomen |
| Element | Water (jal) |
| Yantra | White circle with silver crescent (moon); gold centre |
| Bija mantra | VANG (water element) |
| Carrier animal | Crocodile (makara; sheds crocodile tears; connected to Varuna, lord of water) |
| Petals | 6; colour: red/vermilion; seed sounds: BANG, BHANG, MANG, YANG, RANG, LANG |
| Presiding deity | Vishnu (preserver; four arms holding gada/mace, chakra/wheel, padma/lotus, shankha/conch) |
| Shakti doorkeeper | Rakini (pink skin; source of inspiration in art/music; four arms: arrow, bow, ax, skull) |
| Sense organ | Tongue (taste) |
| Work organ | Genitals |
| Prana vayu | Apana |
| Ruling planet | Mercury |
| Loka | Bhuvar Loka (astral plane) |

**Behavioral characteristics**: Procreation, family, fantasy, creativity, sensuality. Water element: fluidity, emotionality, moon-influenced mood cycles. Dominant life stage: ages 8–14. This level person craves beauty, youth, fantasy, and sexual and emotional connection. "Butterfly behavior" — flirting, forgetting. Crocodile tears. May become a rebel against family and social rules. Second-chakra satisfaction requires sound, beauty, and controlled sensory engagement; Tantra offers a path of elevation through the very energies of this chakra (yoga bhoga — enjoyment as path).

### Manipura Chakra — Third Chakra

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Sanskrit name | Manipura (mani = jewel, pura = city) |
| Location | Navel / solar plexus |
| Element | Fire (tejas/agni) |
| Yantra | Inverted red triangle (fire symbol) |
| Bija mantra | RANG |
| Carrier animal | Ram (mesha; strength, courage, fire energy) |
| Petals | 10; colour: blue (flame/rain cloud); seed sounds: DANG, DHANG, RLANG, TANG, THANG, DANG, DHANG, NANG, PANG, PHANG |
| Presiding deity | Braddha Rudra (Old Shiva; ash-smeared; holds trident and damaru drum; seated on tiger skin; Rudra = weeping, destruction beginning new cycle) |
| Shakti doorkeeper | Lakini Devi / Bhadra Kali (three faces; fire pot; fire energy that radiates from the body) |
| Sense organ | Eyes (sight/form) |
| Work organ | Feet and legs |
| Prana vayu | Samana (digestion) |
| Ruling planet | Sun (masculine) |
| Loka | Svarga Loka (heaven/fire plane) |

**Behavioral characteristics**: Power, authority, status, recognition, immortality. Fire energy: dissatisfaction kindles ambition; intellect governed by ego. Dominant adult life stage. This level person demands respect, seeks power and position, can be self-centred and ego-driven. The ego's steering wheel. Balance achieved through service (karma yoga) — acting without desire for reward. A service orientation opens the path to the Anahata Chakra.

### Anahata Chakra — Fourth Chakra

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Sanskrit name | Anahata (unstruck sound; the sound of silence) |
| Location | Heart centre |
| Element | Air (vayu) |
| Yantra | Hexagram (two overlapping triangles; Star of David form); smoke-grey-green |
| Bija mantra | YANG (air element; gold colour; gives control of prana and breath suspension) |
| Carrier animal | Antelope / musk deer (symbol of the heart; leaps for joy; entranced by mirage like the mind by illusion) |
| Petals | 12; colour: red/vermilion; seed sounds: KANG, KHANG, GANG, GHANG, YONG, CANG, CHANG, JANG, JHANG, UANG, TANG, THANG |
| Presiding deity | Ishana Rudra / Sada Shiva (camphor skin; tiger skin; snake-adorned; no attachments to pleasures or humiliations; holds trident and damaru) |
| Shakti doorkeeper | Kakini (rose or yellow; goddess of creativity, poetry, music, art; Bhuvanesvati aspect) |
| Sense organ | Skin (touch) |
| Work organ | Hands |
| Prana vayu | Prana (life-giving inhalation) |
| Ruling planet | Venus |
| Loka | Maha Loka (plane of balance) |
| Also contains | **Ananda Kanda** (8-petaled lotus inside the Anahata, seat of desires in dream state; contains Shiva Bana Lingam / Itara Lingam); **Vishnu Granthi** (knot of preservation/attachment) |

**Behavioral characteristics**: Love, compassion, devotion, faith, balance. Persons centred at this level overcome the preoccupations of the lower three chakras. They live in harmony, exude love through their eyes and touch, require only 4–6 hours of sleep. Worries and anxieties dissolve. They make sources of faith for others. The Vishnu Granthi must be pierced for Kundalini to proceed past this chakra: attachment to love, compassion, and service — even these must be released.

### Vishuddha Chakra — Fifth Chakra

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Sanskrit name | Vishuddha (pure / purified) |
| Location | Throat |
| Element | Akasha (space/void) |
| Yantra | Silver crescent within white circle; circle = nabhomandala (sky region) |
| Bija mantra | HANG (akasha element; produces sound vibrating in the brain; brings quality to the voice) |
| Carrier animal | White elephant (gaja; carrier of knowledge, patience, memory, confidence; colour of clouds) |
| Petals | 16; colour: lavender/purple; seed sounds include all 16 vowels + A, ANG, I, ING, U, UNG, RING, ENG, AING, ONG, AUNG, ANG, AHANG, plus HANG and PHAT, VASHAT, SWADHA, SWAHA, NAMAH, seven musical tones |
| Presiding deity | Panchavaktra Shiva (Five-faced Shiva: Aghora/North/akasha, Ishana/Northeast/water, Tat Purusha/East/air, Vama Deva/South/earth, Saddyojat/South/fire; encompasses all five elements) |
| Shakti doorkeeper | Shakini (pure; five heads = five senses; rose skin, sky-blue raiment; bestows memory, wit, intuition) |
| Sense organ | Ears (hearing) |
| Work organ | Mouth and vocal cords |
| Prana vayu | Udana (carries air upward to head) |
| Ruling planet | Jupiter |
| Loka | Jana Loka (plane of knowledge) |
| Also contains | Rudra Granthi untied here — the I-consciousness finally dissolves |

**Behavioral characteristics**: Knowledge, consciousness growth, self-mastery, freedom from time conditioning. Dominant life stage: ages 28–35. The main obstacle at this level is intellectual doubt. When knowledge is confirmed through meditation and direct experience, doubt dissolves and consciousness becomes time-free (*kalatita*). Teachers, sages, and scriptural interpreters take birth from the Jana Loka level.

### Ajna Chakra — Sixth Chakra

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Sanskrit name | Ajna (command / perception) |
| Location | Between the eyebrows (third eye) |
| Element | Mahatattva (the combined essence of all five elements — beyond individual elements) |
| Yantra | Luminescent circle with 2 petals; Itara Lingam in centre |
| Bija mantra | AUM (reversed to form HAMSA mantra — the swan; *so'ham* = "I am that") |
| Carrier | Nada (primal sound; crescent form) |
| Petals | 2 (habenulae of pineal gland); seed sounds: HANG, KSHANG |
| Presiding deity | Paramashiva / Ardhanarishvara (the undivided; male-female united; Shiva's third eye; *sva netra* sees past, present, future) |
| Shakti doorkeeper | Hakini (six heads = enlightenment in all directions; indicates Kundalini having absorbed energies of five chakras) |
| Sense organ | Mind (*manas*) |
| Work organ | Mind |
| Ruling planet | Saturn |
| Loka | Tapas Loka (plane of austerity / penance) |

**Behavioral characteristics**: The yogi who reaches Ajna Chakra transcends the world; elements no longer bind; aging stops; past and present and future are known (*trikaladarshi*). No reversal is possible. The consciousness that reaches Ajna sees the world as a divine reflection; duality ceases. Verifying knowledge through meditation at this level removes all remaining doubts.

---

### Soma Chakra

Located between Ajna and Sahasrara; 7 petals; associated with the moon and the nectar (*soma*, *amrita*) that drips from the Sahasrara region. Contains **Kamadhenu** (the wish-fulfilling cow; face of crow, neck of horse, tail of peacock, wings of swan) and the **Kameshvara Chakra** (16 bindus of consciousness; seat of the union of Kameshvari/Kundalini with Kameshvara/Shiva-param). When the yogi masters Khechari Mudra, the soma nectar flow is blocked and preserved — the body is renewed, disease and aging halted.

---

### Sahasrara Chakra — Seventh Chakra (Crown)

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Sanskrit name | Sahasrara (thousand-petaled) |
| Location | Crown of the head |
| Element | Transcends elements |
| Yantra | Circle / full moon (purna chandra) or formless (nirakara) |
| Petals | 1,000; colours: variegated rainbow |
| Seed sounds | All Sanskrit vowels and consonants (AH through KSHA) |
| Presiding deity | Guru / Paramatma (the supreme Self; Mahashakti) |
| Shakti | Chaitanya (pure consciousness) |
| Ruling planet | Ketu |
| Loka | Satyam Loka (plane of truth/reality) |

**Liberation**: The yogi attaining Sahasrara achieves *asamaprajnata samadhi* — enters trance, with activity suspended and only consciousness remaining. Sat-chit-ananda is experienced directly. The yogi may continue to live in the body (*sahaja samadhi*) or may leave, according to remaining karma.

## Behavioral Typology and Life Stages

Johari provides a developmental psychology based on the chakras: each chakra corresponds to a life phase when its energy and concerns naturally predominate.

| Chakra | Life stage | Dominant drive |
|---|---|---|
| Muladhara | 0–7 | Security, survival, earthing |
| Svadhishthana | 8–14 | Fantasy, sexuality, family identity |
| Manipura | 15–28 (adult ego) | Power, recognition, ego assertion |
| Anahata | Mature adult | Love, compassion, faith, service |
| Vishuddha | 28–35+ | Knowledge, consciousness, self-mastery |
| Ajna | Advanced practice | Transcendence, trikaladarshi |
| Sahasrara | Liberation | Samadhi, moksha |

## Comparison with Myss and Brennan

| Dimension | Johari (traditional) | Myss (therapeutic) | Brennan (auric) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Goal of chakra work | Liberation (moksha) | Healing illness | Healing the HEF |
| Chakra anatomy | Deities, mantras, yantras, karma | Seven Sacred Truths, illness, biography | Seven auric layers, front/back aspects |
| Kundalini | Central to the system | Briefly mentioned (menopausal energy) | Not systematically addressed |
| Behavioral typology | Chakra-dominant consciousness → desire → rebirth plane | Chakra depletion → illness | Chakra dysfunction → character structure |
| Element | Full element-sense-organ-planet correspondence | Element mentioned briefly | Not used |

The Western energy-medicine tradition (via Theosophical sources, Leadbeater, and Campbell) preserved the basic seven-chakra structure but recontextualised it within a therapeutic rather than liberative framework. Johari's book restores the fullness of the original system.

## Critique

**Methodological**: The attribute correspondences described in the Tantric texts vary across different scriptural sources; Johari draws on multiple texts (Rudrayamala Tantra, Kankalmalini Tantra, Kularnava Tantra, Khat Chakra Nirupana) which do not always agree on petal colours, deity descriptions, or other details. The system is not perfectly internally consistent across sources.

**Empirical**: No scientific measurement has detected the deities, yantras, or bija-mantra resonances described. The behavioral typology (life stage correlations) is a traditional psychological framework, not an empirically validated developmental model comparable to Erikson or Piaget.

**Intercultural**: The transplantation of this system into Western energy medicine (Myss, Brennan, popular yoga) involves significant decontextualisation — removing the system from its Tantric soteriological context, its Sanskrit liturgical practice, and its requirement for initiatory transmission.
