# Three Ages of Power

**Summary**: Myss maps human consciousness development across three astrological ages — Aries (Tribal Power), Pisces (Individual Power), and Aquarius (Symbolic Power) — arguing that each age contributes a distinct form of energy that remains active in people today and directly shapes the dynamics of illness and healing.

**Sources**:
- [Myss, C. (1997). *Why people don't heal and how they can*. Harmony Books.](../sources/why-people-dont-heal.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

---

## Overview

In [*Why People Don't Heal*](../sources/why-people-dont-heal.md), [Caroline Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md) develops a historical framework for understanding the evolution of human power and consciousness. Drawing on the symbolism of astrology (not as prediction but as a mythology of collective development), she identifies three 2,000-year ages, each of which contributed a distinct form of power that remains layered in the human psyche. Healing requires integrating all three rather than defaulting to the earliest layer.

This framework extends and contextualises her chakra system: the three forms of power correspond broadly to different chakra groupings and to the overall direction of energetic development from tribal survival toward cosmic unity.

## The Three Ages

### Age of Aries (c. 2000 BC – 0 AD): Tribal Power

Aries is a fire sign associated with initiation, action, and collective creation. The dominant consciousness of this age was **Tribal**: identity derived entirely from membership in a family, clan, tribe, or nation. The self was the group; individual desire was subordinate to group survival and collective law.

Key developments of the Arien age:
- The codification of law (Hammurabi, Moses): establishing behavioral boundaries for group survival
- The formation of national identity (Israel, Greece, Rome)
- Sacrifice as a mode of relating to the divine (the story of Abraham and Isaac marks the *end* of literal sacrifice — a shift in consciousness)
- Power expressed through honour codes, loyalty, and group authority

The **shadow** of Tribal power: rigidity, xenophobia, conformity, the suppression of individual conscience. Diseases associated with unresolved Tribal stress cluster in the lower three chakras — the colon, lower back, immune system, legs, and reproductive organs.

### Age of Pisces (c. 0 – 2000 AD): Individual Power

Pisces is a water sign associated with emotion, introspection, and duality (symbolised by two fish swimming in opposite directions). The dominant consciousness of this age was **Individual**: the gradual emergence of personal identity separate from tribal determination.

Key developments of the Piscean age:
- The teachings of Jesus and Buddha: compassion, individual relationship with the Divine, forgiveness, choice
- The Renaissance: artists signed their work; individual genius was celebrated
- The Reformation, Enlightenment, and democratic revolutions: rights of the individual against collective authority
- The emergence of science, psychology, and personal narrative (diaries, autobiography)
- The concept of romantic love — choosing a partner rather than accepting tribal arrangement

Myss identifies two "Judas experiences" that mark Piscean transitions: moments when the tribe's authority is superseded by an inner calling, appearing to the tribe as betrayal but representing evolutionary movement. The crucifixion of Jesus is the archetypal example: a choice not to yield to tribal pressure, accepted with forgiveness rather than vengeance.

The **shadow** of Individual power: narcissism, polarisation (the Piscean split between body/spirit, science/religion, reason/intuition), and the loss of community. Diseases associated with unresolved Individual-level stress correspond to the middle chakras: heart, will, and identity.

Myss's choice of Pisces for this age has a direct technical precedent in [Jung](../thinkers/carl-jung.md)'s [*Aion*](../sources/aion.md), where the two fish of the Pisces constellation — swimming in opposite directions — become the organizing symbol for the entire Christian era's Christ/Antichrist polarity. See [Christ as a Symbol of the Self](christ-as-symbol-of-the-self.md): where Jung reads the Piscean duality as the light/dark split of a single archetype (the Self, doctrinally denied its shadow), Myss's much simpler reading treats the same age's duality as body/spirit, science/religion — a psychologically parallel but far less doctrinally elaborated polarity.

### Age of Aquarius (entering now): Symbolic Power

Aquarius is an air sign associated with mind, unity, and the flow of information. The emerging consciousness is **Symbolic**: the capacity to perceive events not as isolated personal dramas but as expressions of universal patterns — the realm of archetypes, energy, and interconnectedness.

Key markers of the Aquarian transition:
- The 1959 Tibetan diaspora bringing Buddhist teachings to the West
- Vatican II (1962) opening Catholic mysticism to lay practitioners
- The 1960s consciousness revolution, civil rights, holistic health movement
- The internet and global information flow (the "global village")
- The emergence of energy medicine, systems thinking, and environmental consciousness

Symbolic power corresponds to the upper chakras and to the perceiver level Myss calls [Symbolic Sight](symbolic-sight.md): the ability to read events, illness, and relationships as choreographies of archetypal meaning rather than purely personal or random occurrences.

## Three Powers, Not Three Stages

Myss is explicit that the three forms of power are **cumulative, not sequential**. People do not graduate from Tribal into Individual and leave Tribal behind. All three forms are active simultaneously; the challenge is to draw on all three in balance.

- **Tribal power** grounds: providing identity, belonging, and survival instincts.
- **Individual power** liberates: enabling choice, self-examination, and the exercise of personal will.
- **Symbolic power** transcends: offering meaning, perspective, and access to energies beyond personal control.

Illness typically occurs when one form of power is overused or when the transition between forms is resisted. A person locked in Tribal consciousness who experiences a Piscean calling (to change career, leave a marriage, follow an inner truth) will generate the energy conflict that Myss associates with the onset of illness.

## The Three Ages and the Chakra System

The three ages map broadly onto the chakra system:

| Chakras | Form of Power | Age |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| 1–3 | Tribal | Aries |
| 4–7 | Individual | Pisces |
| 7–8 | Symbolic | Aquarius |

The eighth chakra — the seat of the Sacred Contract in later Myss writing — belongs to the Aquarian level: the point at which personal biography merges with cosmic purpose.

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

In [*Sacred Contracts*](../sources/sacred-contracts.md), Myss extends this framework through the [Archetypal Wheel](archetypal-wheel.md), where the twelve houses correspond to aspects of Tribal, Individual, and Symbolic experience. The [Four Survival Archetypes](four-survival-archetypes.md) (Child, Victim, Prostitute, Saboteur) are specifically linked to Tribal-level power dynamics.

The Three Ages framework also parallels [Carl Jung](../thinkers/carl-jung.md)'s concept of individuation: the movement from collective/group identification toward integrated individual selfhood. Myss's Aquarian age corresponds loosely to what Jung might call the integrated Self — beyond individual ego, in relationship with the transpersonal.

A likely direct precedent, rather than a coincidental parallel: [Gary Zukav](../thinkers/gary-zukav.md)'s [*The Seat of the Soul*](../sources/seat-of-the-soul.md) (1989) — which credits Myss's own contributions in its acknowledgments — develops a two-term version of this same progression seven years before *Why People Don't Heal*, contrasting "external power" (control over circumstances and others, corresponding roughly to Tribal and much of Individual power) with "[authentic power](authentic-power.md)" (alignment of personality with soul, corresponding roughly to Symbolic power). Because Myss was in documented dialogue with Zukav's material before formulating her own three-stage model, the relationship is best read as probable direct influence rather than independent convergence, though no single passage in either book can be traced as the source of a specific claim in the other.

## Critique

**From mainstream scholarship**: Myss uses astrology as a metaphorical system, not a predictive science, which sidesteps the empirical objections to astrology. However, historians of consciousness (Ken Wilber, Jean Gebser) have developed similar age-stage models of cultural development with more rigorous scholarly foundations — though they too are contested.

**Internal tension**: The framework implies evolutionary progress (Aquarius is "higher" than Aries), which risks denigrating tribal and traditional cultures still organised around Arien values. Myss does not systematically address this tension, though she acknowledges that Tribal power has genuine strengths.

**Astrological dating**: The precise boundaries of astrological ages are disputed even within astrological traditions. Myss treats the dates schematically rather than arguing for specific boundaries.
