## Overview

In Chapter 1 of *[Light Emerging](../sources/light-emerging.md)*, [Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) introduces the concept of "frozen psychic time" to explain why insight and willpower are insufficient to resolve deep wounds. Traumatic experiences do not merely leave memories — they arrest the development of a segment of the psyche at the age the trauma occurred. These frozen segments cluster together by energetic similarity, forming "time conglomerates": thematically related knots of frozen energy and consciousness that continue to operate in the present as if no time has elapsed.

> "The energy and consciousness composing this time block coagulated around an image conclusion. This image conclusion is based on the experience — in Joe's case, the experience of abandonment. Based on the child logic at the time of the experience, it states: 'If you love someone, you will be abandoned.'"
> — Brennan (1993), Chapter 1

## The Mechanism

### Step 1: The Original Trauma

A painful experience occurs that the child's psyche cannot fully process. To protect itself from overwhelm, the psyche withdraws consciousness from the area of pain — by tensing muscles, repressing awareness, or simply "cutting off" from the experience.

### Step 2: Freezing in Time

The part of the psyche that experienced the trauma does not age or mature from that point. A child of two who experiences a catastrophic abandonment has a part of their psyche that remains, functionally, at age two. This part does not participate in subsequent development; it remains frozen at the moment of the original trauma.

> "The part of the psyche associated with the event froze at that moment and stopped its pain. This part of the psyche remains thawed. It does not mature."

### Step 3: The Image Conclusion

Alongside the frozen energy, the child's psyche forms an **image conclusion** — a belief derived from the event using child logic. Image conclusions are:
- Based on the limited information available to a child at the age of the trauma
- Experienced as absolute truth, not opinion
- Highly generalised from a specific event to a universal rule

Examples:
- "If you love someone, you will be abandoned"
- "I am not loveable unless I take care of others"
- "The world is dangerous and people cannot be trusted"
- "I am defective and must hide it"

These conclusions colour every subsequent experience that resembles the original wound. The adult behaves as if the image conclusion were currently true, regardless of the actual evidence in the present situation.

### Step 4: Time Conglomerate Formation

Related traumatic experiences — those that share the same image conclusion — connect energetically, forming a cluster. For example: the original abandonment, a subsequent abandonment by a teacher at age 10, a painful breakup at 22, and a betrayal by a friend at 30 may all belong to the same time conglomerate. They are energetically connected because they all activated the same image conclusion ("people who love you leave you").

> "Energy consciousness of different time blocks coagulates according to energy type, forming a time conglomerate. For example, energies of abandonment."

The time conglomerate contains all the frozen energy from all these events, not as separate memories but as a single undifferentiated cluster. This is why resolving one piece does not necessarily clear the whole pattern; the entire conglomerate must be thawed progressively.

### Step 5: The Replay

In adult life, any situation that resembles the original wound activates the time conglomerate. At the moment of activation, the adult is no longer experiencing reality from their current age; they are experiencing it from the age of the frozen part. Reactions are disproportionate to the actual situation because they contain the total accumulated charge of every event in the conglomerate.

> "This causes all kinds of exaggerated reactions to a given situation. You can see in such cases people never underestimate the power of image conclusions."

The frozen part also creates behaviour that tends to recreate the original trauma — because its entire frame of reference is the wound. The child who concluded "I will be abandoned by those I love" grows into an adult whose behaviour (excessive demands for proof of love, accusations of planning to leave, pushing people away) creates the abandonment they fear, thereby "confirming" the image conclusion.

## Multiple Lifetimes

Brennan extends the time conglomerate model beyond the current lifetime:

> "I have found through my work that life traumas that underlie present-day problems are often difficult to resolve because they are associated with past lives. The traumas of past lives are also frozen in time conglomerates."

Past-life time conglomerates are structurally identical to current-life ones but are encoded at a deeper layer of the auric field — on the ketheric template (seventh layer) as "life bands." They show up clinically as patterns that respond temporarily to auric-level work but keep returning, suggesting their root lies deeper than the current biography can account for.

This connects to Roger Woolger's past-life regression therapy, which Brennan cites: Woolger finds that clients can clear pain through past-life regression that does not respond to current-life therapy precisely because the frozen time conglomerate belongs to a past-life event.

## Visibility in the Energy Field

Time blocks and conglomerates are visible through [HSP](high-sense-perception.md) as specific types of [energy blocks](energy-blocks-defense-systems.md):
- Compactions and compressions in the second (emotional) and third (mental) auric layers
- Dark or dull patches in the field corresponding to the emotional content of the conglomerate
- In severe cases, large areas of the field that appear "dead" — not moving, not pulsating

The age of the frozen segment is sometimes perceptible through HSP; healers may see a child's size or shape in the auric field at the location corresponding to the wound.

## The Healing Process

Healing a time conglomerate involves three phases:

**1. Making contact with the wound**: Using techniques such as body posturing (Brennan) or regression (Woolger), the therapist guides the client to make conscious contact with the frozen segment. This means *going into* the pain rather than around it — reaching the actual wound rather than the layers of defense that have accumulated around it.

**2. Progressive thawing**: As energy is brought to the frozen block, the ice melts incrementally. The process is not linear — releasing one layer often reveals the next. Patients may find themselves experiencing pain from progressively earlier events as the conglomerate thaws.

**3. Integration and maturation**: Once the frozen energy is released, the segment of the psyche that was arrested at that age can resume its development. What was a one-year-old part becomes a ten-year-old part; then eventually it integrates with the adult. This integration is experienced as a consolidation of the self — more coherent, more present, more whole.

> "When a time block in the psyche is released, the increased energy released into the field in turn starts releasing other segments of the time conglomerate energy. Going back to Joe's story, when a time block is released he may experience pain from thirty years ago — then pain from twenty years ago..."

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

**Keleman's frozen excitation**: Keleman's [insults to form](insults-to-form.md) describe the same phenomenon at the somatic level: shocks and traumas freeze the body's pulsatory patterns, creating fixations that replay. The time conglomerate is the psychic/energetic correlate of what Keleman maps as somatic fixation.

**Myss's cellular energy bank**: Myss's [cellular energy bank](cellular-energy-bank.md) describes life force held in the past — in wounds, resentments, and unresolved experiences — as a drain on present energy. Time conglomerates are the structural explanation for why this energy is "locked up": it is literally frozen in time.

**Myss's woundology**: The time conglomerate provides the structural basis for woundology — the wound identity is maintained because the time conglomerate keeps the wound psychically current. The person is not using an old wound; from the perspective of the frozen psychic segment, the wound is still happening.

**Keleman's somatic distress patterns**: The four [somatic distress patterns](somatic-distress-patterns.md) (rigid, compact, swollen, collapsed) are, in part, the long-term somatic crystallisation of time conglomerates — specific patterns of freezing expressed at the level of body form.

## Critique

**Empirical status**: Time conglomerates are phenomenological descriptions from energy-field observation and clinical regression work. They are not verified by neuroscience or mainstream psychology, though the general concept of trauma as arrested development has substantial support in the trauma literature (van der Kolk, Herman, Levine).

**Past-life extension**: The extension of time conglomerates to past lifetimes is the most empirically controversial aspect. Woolger's past-life regression work and Brennan's observations are case-based and not subject to independent verification.

**Note**: The concept of frozen psychic time is consistent with trauma theories in mainstream psychology (PTSD, developmental arrest, emotional flashback) but is expressed in energy-field terms that mainstream psychology does not use. The energy-field description adds a layer of phenomenological richness but also a layer of untestability.
