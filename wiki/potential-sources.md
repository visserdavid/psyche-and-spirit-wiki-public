## Key Works Cited in Two Essays on Analytical Psychology (Jung, CW 7)

### By Freud and Adler (Jung's Theoretical Counterpoints)

- **Freud, S. (1900). *The interpretation of dreams*. Franz Deuticke.**
  Found in: Two Essays on Analytical Psychology, Essay 1 (Jung's starting point for dream analysis as "via regia" to the unconscious, before diverging from Freud's wish-fulfilment reading).
  Relevance: The founding text of psychoanalytic dream interpretation; essential primary-source counterpoint for understanding exactly where Jung's compensatory reading of dreams departs from Freud's wish-fulfilment theory.
  Status: Not in vault.

- **Freud, S. (1905). *Three essays on the theory of sexuality*. Franz Deuticke.**
  Found in: Two Essays on Analytical Psychology, Essay 1 (Freud's libido theory, which Jung both credits and argues is too narrowly sexual).
  Relevance: Freud's foundational statement of libido/Eros theory; the direct target of Jung's broader, non-sexual redefinition of libido as generalized psychic energy.
  Status: Not in vault.

- **Adler, A. (1917). *The neurotic constitution* (B. Glueck & J. E. Lind, Trans.). Moffat, Yard.**
  Found in: Two Essays on Analytical Psychology, Essay 1 (Jung's primary source for Adler's will-to-power/masculine-protest theory of neurosis, discussed in parallel with Freud's Eros theory).
  Relevance: Adler's foundational statement of individual psychology; essential companion for the eros-and-will-to-power concept page and the Alfred Adler thinker page.
  Status: Not in vault.

### On Fantasy, Symbolism, and Concretization

- **Wells, H. G. (1925). *Christina Alberta's Father*. Macmillan.**
  Found in: Two Essays on Analytical Psychology, Essay 2 (Jung's illustration of the animus-as-plurality: the heroine's internalized "Court of Conscience," a college of condemnatory judges).
  Relevance: Jung's chosen literary illustration for the animus's tendency to appear as an assembly rather than a single figure; relevant to the anima-animus concept page's discussion of anima-singular/animus-plural asymmetry.
  Status: Not in vault.

- **Silberer, H. (1917). *Problems of mysticism and its symbolism* (S. E. Jelliffe, Trans.). Moffat, Yard.**
  Found in: Two Essays on Analytical Psychology, Essay 2 (Jung's source for the distinction between reductive/causal and constructive/symbolic dream interpretation, and for alchemy as psychology's unwitting precursor).
  Relevance: An early psychoanalytic study of mystical and alchemical symbolism that anticipated Jung's own later alchemical work; directly relevant to the compensation and coniunctio concept pages.
  Status: Not in vault.
