# Chakras: Energy Centers of Transformation

**Summary**: Harish Johari's authoritative guide to the traditional Hindu-Tantric chakra system, covering Kundalini Yoga, the nadi and prana systems, detailed attributes of each chakra (deities, mantras, yantras, carriers, behavioral characteristics), the eight-limbed Ashtanga Yoga path, and the relationship between chakras, karma, and rebirth.

## Publication Details

**Original edition**: 1987, Destiny Books (ISBN 0-89281-677-5). Revised and expanded edition 2000 (ISBN 0-89281-760-7; 188 pages). The vault file is the 2000 revised edition.
**Author**: Harish Johari (1934–1999), Indian scholar, artist, and Tantric teacher. The revised edition was completed posthumously by his family and students; Johari died on 20 August 1999 in Haridwar.

## Overview

Where Western energy-medicine authors (Myss, Brennan) adopt and adapt the chakra system for therapeutic use, Johari writes from within the living Hindu-Tantric tradition. This book is less a healing manual than a practical guide to Kundalini Yoga — the system of physical, energetic, and meditative disciplines by which the dormant Kundalini Shakti is awakened and guided through the chakras to liberation.

The book has four main sections:
1. **Kundalini Yoga** — the foundational theory and methods: prana, nadis, the five koshas, Ashtanga Yoga (eight limbs), Kshata Karmas (six purification acts), bandhas, mudras, and Svara Yoga
2. **Essentials of Chakras** — a systematic survey of all seven chakras plus the Soma and Sahasrara, each with its traditional attributes: element, yantra, bija mantra, carrier animal, presiding deity, Shakti doorkeeper, sense organ, work organ, ruling planet, loka (plane), and behavioral characteristics
3. **Chakras and Rebirth** — the relationship between chakra dominance, karmic plane after death, and the kind of birth taken next
4. **Appendix** — extensive extracts from primary Hindu scriptures (Upanishads and Tantras) on each limb of Ashtanga Yoga

## Key Frameworks

### Kundalini and the Goal of Yoga

Kundalini Shakti is the divine feminine energy lying dormant as a coiled serpent at Muladhara Chakra (base of spine). The entire system of Yoga — asana, pranayama, mudra, mantra, meditation — is organised around awakening and guiding Kundalini from Muladhara through the six chakras to Sahasrara (crown), where Shakti unites with Shiva-consciousness, producing liberation (moksha). See [Kundalini](../concepts/kundalini.md).

### Prana, Nadis, and Svara Yoga

The physical body is animated by prana (life force), distributed through a network of 72,000 nadis (subtle energy channels). The three principal nadis — Ida (lunar, left), Pingala (solar, right), and Sushumna (central) — determine the flow of prana and the activation of chakras. Sushumna contains three inner channels: Vajrini, Chitrini, and Brahma Nadi (the passage of Kundalini). Svara Yoga, the science of nasal breath, shows how the alternating dominance of Ida and Pingala corresponds to which chakra/element is dominant at any moment. See [Prana and Nadis](../concepts/prana-and-nadis.md).

### The Five Koshas

The living person exists as five interpenetrating sheaths (koshas) from gross to subtle: Annamayi (matter/food), Pranamayi (vital air), Manomayi (mental), Vijnanamaya (wisdom/intellect), and Anandamayi (bliss). Kundalini energy operates through all five; the goal of Yoga is to pierce through each sheath to the Self (Atman) within. See [Koshas](../concepts/koshas.md).

### Ashtanga Yoga

The eight-limbed (Ashtanga) Yoga path described by Patanjali — yama, niyama, asana, pranayama, pratyahara, dharana, dhyana, samadhi — provides the systematic method for purifying body and mind, activating Sushumna, and guiding Kundalini to liberation. See [Ashtanga Yoga](../concepts/ashtanga-yoga.md).

### Traditional Chakra Attributes

Each chakra carries a complete traditional profile: an element (tattva), geometric yantra shape and colour, seed sound (bija mantra), presiding deity, Shakti doorkeeper, carrier animal (vahana), associated sense and work organ, ruling planet, corresponding cosmic plane (loka), and a set of behavioral/psychological characteristics. Johari also describes specific age-ranges during which each chakra dominates human development and motivation. See [Tantric Chakra Attributes](../concepts/tantric-chakra-attributes.md).

### Chakras and Karma

After death, the plane (loka) in which a person dwells is determined by which chakra dominated their consciousness during life. Desires unfulfilled at that chakra's level draw the soul back to earth to complete them. The goal of Yoga is to transcend all chakra-bound desires and reach Sahasrara, from which the yogi may exit the cycle of rebirth.

## Relationship to Other Sources in the Wiki

Johari's book provides the primary-source Hindu-Tantric grounding for the chakra system that Myss and Brennan use in therapeutic Western contexts. Key comparisons:

- **Myss** reads chakras as maps of biography and illness. Johari reads them as stages of consciousness and motivation in the Kundalini ascent. Both identify behavioral and psychological characteristics per chakra, but from opposite directions (healing vs. liberation).
- **Brennan** maps the chakra system onto the seven-layer HEF and provides Western-style psychological profiles. Johari maps it onto the Tantric cosmos of nadis, pranas, deities, mantras, and karma.
- The element-planet-deity-mantra correspondences in Johari are largely absent from the Western energy-medicine literature; they represent the tradition behind what Myss borrows.

## Thinkers and Traditions Cited

Johari draws exclusively on the Hindu-Tantric scriptural tradition rather than modern researchers:
- **Patanjali**: Yoga Sutras (Ashtanga Yoga framework)
- **Shankaracharya**: Viveka Chudamani (mind as maya); Saundarya Lahari (Kundalini in six chakras)
- Key Tantric texts: Rudrayamala Tantra, Lalita Sahasranama, Mahanirvana Tantra, Khat Chakra Nirupana, Kularnava Tantra, Gheranya Samhita, Hatha Yogapradipika, Shiva Samhita, Shiva Purana, Kankalmalini Tantra, Gyanarva Tantra
- Key Upanishads: Varahopanishad, Yogashikhopanishad, Shandilyopanishad, Mandalabrahmanopanishad, Shvetashvataropanishad, Darshanopanishad, Yoga Tattvopanishad, and others

Johari's other works cited: *Tools for Tantra* (Destiny Books, 1986), *Breath, Mind, and Consciousness* (Destiny Books, 1989), *Leela: The Game of Self Knowledge* (Destiny Books, 1993).
