# Character Analysis

**Summary**: Wilhelm Reich's 1933 clinical masterwork — expanded through a 1945 English translation and a 1949 third edition — arguing that chronic character traits and chronic muscular tension are the same defensive structure ("character armor"), and that psychic health is inseparable from full orgastic discharge of sexual energy ("orgastic potency"); the foundational text of the entire body-centered psychotherapy tradition (Lowen, Pierrakos, Keleman, Brennan).

**Sources**:
- [Reich, W. (1949). *Character analysis* (T. P. Wolfe, Trans.; 3rd, enlarged ed.). Orgone Institute Press. (Original work published 1933 as *Charakteranalyse*)](character-analysis.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-07-18

---

## Overview

*Character Analysis* began as a 1933 Vienna-published clinical monograph (*Charakteranalyse*) extending Freud's psychoanalytic technique: where Freud interpreted symptoms and their content, Reich showed that the *form* of a patient's behavior — tone of voice, posture, politeness, irony, silence — was itself analyzable as a "character resistance," and that consistently analyzing this form, rather than chasing content, was often the only way past patients whose defenses were too well organized to yield to direct interpretation.

Reich's American translator, Theodore P. Wolfe, brought out an expanded English edition in 1945 (Orgone Institute Press), and Reich further enlarged it for a 1949 third edition — the version summarized here (the vault file is the Vision Press, London reprint of the Wolfe translation). Between the first German edition and the third English edition, Reich's own thinking moved from clinical psychoanalysis toward his later, more controversial biophysical theory of "orgone energy," and the later editions graft this trajectory onto the original text via prefaces, footnotes, and two added chapters — including an extended case history of a schizophrenic patient treated with early orgone therapy.

## Key Takeaways

- **[Character armor](../concepts/character-armor.md)**: chronic character traits and chronic muscular tension are the same defensive structure, serving to bind anxiety and protect the ego from both outer danger and inner instinctual pressure.
- **[Character resistance analysis](../concepts/character-resistance-analysis.md)**: Reich's technical innovation — analyzing *how* a patient relates to the analyst (the form of communication) before interpreting *what* they say, since character itself organizes and conceals the content.
- **[Reich's character types](../concepts/reich-character-types.md)**: a clinical typology (hysterical, compulsive, phallic-narcissistic, masochistic) describing distinct patterns of libidinal fixation, armoring, and defense — the direct historical ancestor of Lowen's bioenergetic characters and Brennan's auric character structures.
- **[Orgastic potency](../concepts/orgastic-potency.md)**: Reich's sex-economic theory that the capacity for full orgastic discharge is the measure of psychic health, and its absence ("orgastic impotence") is the universal core of neurosis — a claim that alienated much of the psychoanalytic establishment.
- **[Masochism and the death instinct](../concepts/masochism-and-death-instinct.md)**: Reich's clinical re-analysis of masochism as a disturbed pleasure mechanism (fear of the "melting" sensation preceding orgasm), offered as an empirical refutation of Freud's speculative death instinct.
- **[The emotional plague](../concepts/emotional-plague.md)**: Reich's late concept of a socially transmitted, character-rooted "biopathy" — chronic envy, moralizing sadism, and persecution of the living — that he saw operating at mass scale in fascism and the Inquisition.
- The book's final chapters document Reich's turn toward **vegetotherapy and orgone therapy**: working directly on muscular tension to release blocked "orgone" energy, on the theory that psychic and somatic armor are one and the same phenomenon — the direct precursor of [Alexander Lowen's](../thinkers/alexander-lowen.md) bioenergetics and [John Pierrakos's](../thinkers/john-pierrakos.md) Core Energetics.

## Structure

The book falls into three parts. **Part One (Technique)** presents Reich's clinical innovation of character-resistance analysis through detailed case histories — most from his pre-1933 psychoanalytic practice in Vienna and Berlin. **Part Two (Theory of Character Formation)** develops the general theory: character as "armor," the genital character versus the neurotic character, sublimation versus reaction formation, and the four character types. **Part Three (Psychoanalysis and Orgone Biophysics)**, added for the English editions, documents Reich's transition from psychological to explicitly biophysical technique — muscular armor, the orgasm reflex, and a lengthy case history applying early orgone therapy to a schizophrenic patient.

## Reception and Critique

Within body-centered psychotherapy, *Character Analysis* (particularly its pre-1933 technical core) is still read as a serious, clinically rigorous contribution — the founding document of the idea that psychological defense is inseparable from bodily posture and tension. [Alexander Lowen](../thinkers/alexander-lowen.md), [John Pierrakos](../thinkers/john-pierrakos.md), and [Stanley Keleman](../thinkers/stanley-keleman.md) all built directly on it, and [Barbara Ann Brennan](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) explicitly credits Reich as the origin point of her [auric character structures](../concepts/character-structures-auric.md).

Reich's orgasm theory and, more sharply, his later orgone-energy claims were never accepted by mainstream psychoanalysis or by mainstream science; his 1933 expulsion from the International Psychoanalytic Association (officially over his political activity, though the book's translator argues the sex-economic theory was the real issue) and his subsequent U.S. Food and Drug Administration prosecution, book-burning, and death in federal prison in 1957 mark the most extreme case in this wiki of a thinker whose early clinical insight and later empirical claims diverge sharply in credibility. See the [Wilhelm Reich](../thinkers/wilhelm-reich.md) thinker page and [Character Armor](../concepts/character-armor.md) for a fuller discussion of where mainstream science draws that line.
