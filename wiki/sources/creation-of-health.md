# The Creation of Health

**Summary**: The original Myss–Shealy joint research publication, presenting dozens of dual-perspective case studies (medical diagnosis + intuitive energy analysis) to document how Power, Responsibility, Wisdom, and Love are the four domains from which illness originates.

**Sources**:
- [Myss, C., & Shealy, C. N. (1988). *The creation of health: The emotional, psychological, and spiritual responses that promote health and healing*. Stillpoint Publishing.](creation-of-health.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

---

## Bibliographic Note

First published 1988, Stillpoint International, Walpole, NH. Revised and retitled edition 1993, Stillpoint Publishing. Later reissued by Three Rivers Press/Harmony Books (1998, ISBN 9780609803233). Foreword by Bernard S. Siegel, M.D.

Authors: Caroline Myss (M.A., journalist and medical intuitive) and C. Norman Shealy, M.D., Ph.D. (Harvard-trained neurosurgeon, founder of the Shealy Institute for Comprehensive Health Care, Springfield, Missouri).

## Overview

*The Creation of Health* is the published record of a decade-long collaboration in which [Shealy](../thinkers/norman-shealy.md) provided patients' names and ages to [Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md), who conducted remote energy evaluations, and the two compared findings against clinical diagnoses and patient histories. The book makes two arguments simultaneously:

1. **Empirical**: that specific emotional and biographical stress patterns reliably precede and correlate with specific diseases, documented across hundreds of case studies.
2. **Theoretical**: that a new paradigm of medicine is emerging — one in which allopathic and holistic approaches are complementary, not competitive.

The dual-voice format is the book's defining structural feature. For each case, Shealy presents the medical diagnosis and treatment; Myss presents the energy analysis — identifying the biographical stresses, emotional patterns, and chakra-level disruptions that she perceives as underlying the illness. The convergence of the two voices case after case constitutes the book's central argument.

## Structure

### Part I: Understanding the Nature of Energy and Creating Health

**Chapter 1: Shifting Thinking About the Cause of Illness**

Myss argues that the real origin of illness lies not in external agents (viruses, bacteria) but in the person's interior life. She and Shealy identify **eight significant stress patterns** across their case work:

1. A consuming life stress running since childhood (rejection, inadequacy, grief)
2. Degree of control over one's belief system — whether attitudes are empowering or disempowering
3. Ability to give and receive love (the degree of warmth in a person's life)
4. Capacity for humour and the ability to distinguish major from minor concerns
5. Holding dominion over one's choices and life direction
6. Attending to the body's physical needs (nutrition, exercise, sleep)
7. The vacuum of suffering that accompanies loss of meaning
8. Tendency toward denial — the stress of not acknowledging what is not working

The chapter introduces the **four challenge areas**: [Power, Responsibility, Wisdom, and Love](../concepts/four-challenge-areas.md) — the domains in which stress generates disease. This is the precursor framework to the Seven Sacred Truths in *Anatomy of the Spirit*.

**Chapter 2: Medicine Draws on the Holistic Model**

A paradigm comparison: allopathic medicine locates disease causation in external agents; holistic medicine locates it in the person's inner state. Myss and Shealy argue these paradigms are not competitors but complements — "medicine is the mind, holistic medicine is the heart." See [Two Paradigms of Medicine](../concepts/two-paradigms-of-medicine.md).

**Chapter 3: Enter Intuitive Medicine**

A survey of intuitive diagnosticians who have influenced the field:
- [Edgar Cayce](../thinkers/edgar-cayce.md) — 15,000 trance health readings linking attitude and emotion to specific organ dysfunction
- **Robert Leichtman, M.D.** — physician-intuitive studied by Shealy alongside Myss for seven years
- **Edward Bach** — developer of Bach Flower Remedies; diagnosed emotional states through plant energies
- **Torkom Saraydarian** — philosopher and writer who analysed the energy foundations of health

Myss herself is presented as emerging from this tradition while being distinguished by her decade of clinical collaboration with Shealy, which provides systematic verification.

**Chapter 4: Intuitive Diagnosis and the Human Energy System**

The architecture of the [energy system](../concepts/energy-anatomy.md): energy flows through the [chakra system](../concepts/chakra-system.md), and disruptions are reflected in specific body regions corresponding to each chakra. Key propositions:

- The intuition channel (solar plexus / third chakra) is the body's primary survival-data processor, operating below conscious awareness.
- Issues of power, responsibility, wisdom, and love are distributed across the chakra centres.
- Environmental stress (violence, toxic relationships, institutional dysfunction) affects the energy system even when the person has done personal healing work.

**Chapter 5: How to Heal**

Distinguishes soul pain from physical pain. Illness as teacher: it is the body's communication that a pattern is no longer sustainable. Religion and spirituality — not as doctrinal positions but as sources of meaning and connection — are healing resources. Time and present-moment awareness are emphasised: healing happens in the present, not by revisiting the past.

### Part II: Acts and Attitudes That Generate Disease

The core of the book: fourteen chapters of case studies organized by disease category, each presenting both medical and energy analysis. See [Disease Energy Profiles](../concepts/disease-energy-profiles.md) for the full taxonomy.

**Chapter 6: Heart Disease, Stroke, and Cancer**
- Heart disease: fourth-chakra issues of love, forgiveness, and connection
- Stroke: transition, identity crisis, the energy of "stopping"
- Cancer: accumulated unexpressed rage, deep fear, and sustained self-negation

Illustrative case — **Frank** (Chapter 6): A forty-one-year-old ex-priest who had left the priesthood and married an ex-nun. He developed angina that prevented walking more than a hundred feet. Shealy treated with diet, biofeedback, and counselling; Myss identified that Frank had not released his priest identity — he continued functioning according to a form he no longer inhabited. The stress of this identity transition was held in his heart.

**Chapters 7–10**: Acute diseases (AIDS, herpes, shingles, accidents), chronic disease (epilepsy, MS, ALS, diabetes), chronic pain (migraine, fibromyalgia), mental and emotional disorders (Alzheimer's, addiction, panic attacks).

Illustrative case — **Eugene** (diabetes): A thirty-one-year-old who developed diabetes after losing his job. His mother came to visit and cooked his childhood favourites; he gorged on desserts. Myss's analysis: losing the job ignited fear of taking responsibility; his mother's visit triggered a regressive pull toward childhood dependency. The pancreas — which Myss reads as governing "the fuel allocation of the self" — responded to the resentment signal by disrupting insulin production.

Illustrative case — **Lori** (cancer): A woman who developed cervical cancer that spread to uterus and intestines. She refused surgery and pursued alternative therapies with apparent conviction. Myss identifies that Lori's alternative-medicine programme had become a sophisticated form of denial — she was not healing but avoiding confronting her fear of the cancer. The "dragon" Lori would not face was her own terror. She died two years later. This case is the book's most important self-critique: alternative medicine can serve denial as effectively as any other avoidance strategy.

### Part III: Stress as the Common Denominator

**Chapter 11: Low Self-Esteem Leads to Sexual Conflicts**

Low self-esteem is the foundation of most illness. Hypocrisies — the gap between declared values and lived behaviour — generate a particular quality of chronic stress. Second-chakra energy (sexuality, creativity, financial security) becomes disrupted when self-esteem is absent or conditional.

**Chapter 12: Psychosomatic Illness**

The stress cycle and how it produces physical illness. The placebo effect as evidence that the mind exerts genuine physiological authority. Why this does not make illness "imaginary" — it makes the mind a real biological actor.

### Part IV: Create Health

**Chapter 13: Stay Healthy**

Practical: nutrition, exercise, developing empowering attitudes, building alternatives to the patterns that generate illness.

**Chapter 14: Become an Elegant Spirit**

The concept of elegance as a quality of spirit — understanding one's situation clearly, choosing survival over drama, looking at life from the longest perspective available. This anticipates the *Sacred Contracts* framework's emphasis on aligning with the soul's purpose.

## Key Contributions

### The Dual-Voice Method

The most important formal innovation of the book. By placing Shealy's clinical findings and Myss's energy readings side by side, the book creates a cumulative case that the two paradigms are addressing the same underlying reality from different directions. Neither voice claims to be complete; each illuminates what the other cannot see.

### The Four Challenge Areas

The precursor to the Seven Sacred Truths: Power, Responsibility, Wisdom, and Love as the four domains in which human beings must grow and in which failure to grow generates illness. See [Four Challenge Areas](../concepts/four-challenge-areas.md).

### Disease-Specific Energy Profiles

The most detailed organ-level disease-emotion mapping in the Myss corpus. Each major disease category is given an energy analysis grounding it in specific chakra disruptions and biographical patterns. See [Disease Energy Profiles](../concepts/disease-energy-profiles.md).

### The Paradigm Comparison

A nuanced discussion of why allopathic and holistic medicine are not antagonists. Myss coins the phrase "medicine is the mind of health care, holistic medicine is the heart." She is particularly critical of the term "alternative medicine" as implying a lesser or subordinate system. See [Two Paradigms of Medicine](../concepts/two-paradigms-of-medicine.md).

### The Lori Case: Self-Critique

The inclusion of Lori's story — a patient who died having used alternative medicine primarily as denial — is the book's most intellectually honest moment. It demonstrates that holistic medicine is not inherently superior to allopathic medicine and that the inner work it requires is genuinely difficult.

## Relationship to Later Works

This book is the empirical foundation of [Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md)'s entire subsequent output:

- The four challenge areas (Power, Responsibility, Wisdom, Love) are reorganised into the Seven Sacred Truths in [*Anatomy of the Spirit* (1996)](anatomy-of-the-spirit.md)
- The disease-emotion mappings here become the case material for the chakra system chapters in *Anatomy of the Spirit*
- Medical intuition as documented here provides the credibility for Myss's later frameworks
- The distinction between healing and curing introduced here is developed fully in [*Why People Don't Heal* (1997)](why-people-dont-heal.md)

## Critique

**Selection bias**: The case studies are selected to illustrate the framework. Cases where the energy analysis was wrong, or where a patient's biography showed no stress pattern but illness developed anyway, are not included.

**Causality**: Even if emotional patterns reliably precede illness (which the case studies suggest), this does not establish that the emotional pattern *caused* the illness. Reverse causality (pre-symptomatic illness causing emotional distress) and common causes (genetic vulnerability producing both) are not ruled out.

**The Lori problem**: The Lori case can be read as showing the limits of the entire framework — not just of denial as a strategy, but of a framework in which the patient's inner state is treated as the primary causal variable. Some people with Lori's diagnosis and Lori's full emotional engagement with healing still do not survive.

**Note**: The disease-emotion correspondences documented in this book are not validated by controlled clinical research. Psychoneuroimmunology confirms that chronic stress affects physiological outcomes, but the specific mappings proposed here — pancreatic cancer from resentment, heart disease from blocked love — are not supported by epidemiological evidence.
