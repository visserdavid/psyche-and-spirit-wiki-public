# Global Mind Change

**Summary**: Harman (1988) — the foundational philosophical argument that Western civilisation is undergoing a Copernican-scale revolution in its root assumptions about reality; introduces the M-1/M-2/M-3 three-metaphysics taxonomy, diagnoses the pathologies of the M-1 worldview, and assembles evidence from PNI, parapsychology, transpersonal psychology, and world wisdom traditions to support M-3 (consciousness-as-primary) as the emerging paradigm.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The Copernican analogy**: The shift now under way — from matter as fundamental to consciousness as fundamental — is of the same order as the Copernican revolution. It will reshape science, medicine, education, economics, and the legitimacy of war.

2. **The M-1/M-2/M-3 taxonomy**: Three metaphysical positions map the intellectual landscape. M-1 (materialistic monism) has dominated Western science for four centuries. The evidence — from PNI to parapsychology to wisdom traditions — points toward M-3 (consciousness as primary, matter as its manifestation).

3. **Unconscious belief shapes perception**: Following Milton Rokeach's model, Harman argues that concentric layers of unexamined belief operate below awareness, systematically distorting perception to confirm existing beliefs. The dominant M-1 worldview is itself such a belief system — invisible precisely because it is universal.

4. **Mind and healing**: The three interconnected systems (nervous, endocrine, immune) communicate via neuropeptides. The mind — through its beliefs, meanings, and intentions — operates on all three. Illness arises at the level of meaning before manifesting physically. Placebo, psychosomatic disease, multiple personality disorder (MPD), and firewalking all demonstrate mind's primacy.

5. **Transpersonal psychology**: ESP, multiple personality, channelling, creativity, and exceptional capabilities are not marginal anomalies but data points that demand an M-3 or M-2 framework for explanation. Harman catalogues decades of suppressed evidence — from hypnosis to parapsychology — that the M-1 assumption has excluded.

6. **The global macroproblem**: The civilisation-level dysfunctions (arms race, poverty, ecological destruction, commodification of work) all share a common root: a worldview that prioritises material production and control over meaning, relationship, and wisdom. The macroproblem is a consciousness problem.

7. **Delegitimating war**: Historical parallels show that profound change (abolition of slavery, end of feudalism) comes not from governments or battles but from enough people changing their minds. The withdrawal of legitimacy is the most powerful force in human history.

## Structure

**Chapter 1 — Scientific Heresy and the Transformation of Society**

Opens with the Copernican revolution as a historical template. Copernicus did not intend revolution, but his heliocentric model required replacing an entire authority structure. The book argues that recognising consciousness as causal reality is today's equivalent heresy. Introduces the concept of unconscious belief: beliefs shape perception; the M-1 worldview is the unexamined belief system of Western science.

**Chapter 2 — Consciousness as Causal Reality**

Develops the M-1/M-2/M-3 taxonomy. Examines the ten unspoken assumptions of conventional science (senses as sole knowledge gateway; consciousness as epiphenomenon; no purpose in evolution; consciousness does not survive death; etc.). Argues that M-3 — consciousness as primary — is not new: it is the shared view of wisdom traditions across history and cultures. The "de-hypnotisation" metaphor: mystics, from Ouspensky to Vivekananda, have always held that ordinary waking consciousness is a trance from which one can awaken.

**Chapter 3 — Challenges to Positivism and Reductionism**

Examines three domains where M-1 shows strain:

- *Mind, health, illness, and healing*: PNI (three-system model: nervous, endocrine, immune), fight-or-flight, the placebo effect (Krebiozen case), regeneration, biofeedback, and the Simonton visualisation work all demonstrate mind's causal role in biology.

- *Attention and volition*: Kornhuber's readiness potentials show volition precedes neural activity. Hilgard's hidden observer experiments show multiple simultaneous levels of awareness. Biofeedback shows the body "knows" things the conscious mind does not.

- *Mind, instinct, and evolution*: Sheldrake's morphic resonance hypothesis (rat learning accumulating across isolated generations; morphogenetic fields); Bergson's *élan vital*; Teilhard de Chardin's teleological evolution; the challenge to neo-Darwinian assumptions.

**Chapter 4 — New Paradigm Psychology: Legitimating the Transpersonal**

The longest chapter. Examines:

- *Multiple personality disorder (MPD)*: personality shifts accompanied by measurable physiological changes (brainwave patterns, allergies, visual acuity, chemical composition of bodily fluids). The Inner Self Helper — described by psychiatrist Ralph Allison — corresponds to the *Atman* in Indian philosophy: the alter that exhibits no fear, guilt, or anger; has access to the full history; acts as "conduit for God's love."

- *Creativity and unconscious knowing*: The part of mind that "knows" how to warm a fingertip, stop bleeding, and heal tissue. Affirmation and inner imagery as tools for reaching this level.

- *Exceptional capabilities*: ESP, psychokinesis, remote viewing, channelling — catalogued as phenomena that resist M-1 explanation. Eysenck's summary: "a conspiracy involving thirty university departments worldwide... claims researchers have come to the conclusion that people can obtain knowledge by means that science cannot currently explain."

- *Scientific objectivity under M-1*: Historical examples of suppression (meteorites dismissed by Lavoisier's committee; waves of light rejected; hypnosis surgeries whose patients were accused of pretending not to feel pain). The built-in bias of M-1 assumptions against phenomena that require M-2 or M-3 for their explanation.

**Chapter 5 — Transforming the World Macroproblem**

The civilisation-level analysis. The macro-problem has roots in M-1 values: the reduction of humans to consumers, the equation of GNP with wellbeing, the "Alice in Wonderland" logic of running faster to stay in place. Alternative value emphases emerging globally: the primacy of inner development over material consumption; meaningful work; ecological relationship.

**Chapter 6 — Aspects of World System Change**

Practical analysis: the necessity of redefining work (not all value is monetised), redefining development (not all growth is GNP growth), finding global security (delegitimating war as the next great civilisational shift, following abolition and decolonisation). The key variable in all of these: the changing image of reality in enough people's minds.

## Key Arguments

### Consciousness and Mind-Body Connection

Harman presents PNI as one of the strongest lines of evidence for M-3 primacy within established science. The three interconnected systems (neural, endocrine, immune) communicate via neuropeptides that carry emotional signals. The Krebiozen case (placebo remission followed by relapse when the patient read that the drug was worthless) shows that belief is a primary biological variable. Multiple personality disorder, in which physiological measurements change with personality shifts, provides perhaps the most dramatic evidence: the same body expresses different biological states depending on which self is "in charge."

### Unconscious Belief as the Key Variable

Following Milton Rokeach's *The Open and Closed Mind* (1960), Harman proposes that belief systems consist of concentric regions — from peripheral (changeable by education) through intermediate (requiring deep psychotherapy) to core (assumptions about the nature of reality itself). People do not choose their beliefs; beliefs are absorbed by osmosis in infancy. The M-1 worldview is a core-level belief for modern Westerners — therefore nearly invisible and highly defended. Firewalking is offered as a dramatic instance: those who change their belief (coals will not harm) walk unharmed; those who retain it suffer burns. The mechanism is not explained; the fact is demonstrated.

### The Inner Self Helper as Atman

Harman's key cross-cultural synthesis: psychiatrist Ralph Allison found in MPD systems an alter personality unlike all others — with no fear, hatred, or guilt; access to the complete biographical record; compassion for all other alters; and reports of having existed before birth and continuing after death. [Harman](../thinkers/willis-harman.md) identified this as the clinical rediscovery of the Vedantic *Atman* — the unchanging core of consciousness beneath all biographical fragmentation. The same insight found by two entirely different paths (American psychiatric practice and Indian philosophy) is the kind of convergent evidence Harman considers most significant.

### Science's Self-Limiting Assumptions

Harman catalogues historical instances where accepted science rejected valid phenomena because they didn't fit the prevailing M-1 framework: the French Academy dismissing meteorites (1772); the wave theory of light rejected by Newton's disciples; Semmelweis and Pasteur harassed for germ theory; hypnotic surgery patients accused of pretending. The pattern is consistent: paradigm-violating evidence is suppressed until the weight of anomalies forces a framework shift. Parapsychology research (Eysenck's "conspiracy of thirty university departments") represents the current frontier of suppressed evidence.

## Critique

**The metaphysical argument is not empirical**: Harman is explicit that the choice between M-1, M-2, and M-3 is not a scientific question — it is a prior philosophical commitment that shapes what counts as evidence. This makes the book's central thesis unfalsifiable in the conventional scientific sense.

**Selective reading of anomalies**: Harman presents parapsychology evidence sympathetically without engaging the detailed methodological critiques (e.g., Hyman's analysis of remote viewing). The cited "conspiracy of thirty university departments" quote (Eysenck) is from 1974 and does not represent the current state of the field.

**Civilisational determinism**: The claim that the macroproblem has a single root (M-1 worldview) risks being unfalsifiable. Many of the cited problems (inequality, ecological destruction, arms races) have structural-economic explanations that do not require a consciousness shift for remedy.

**Note**: Mainstream science continues to operate within M-1 assumptions. The case for a paradigm shift is philosophical and historical, not yet evidential in the mainstream scientific sense.
