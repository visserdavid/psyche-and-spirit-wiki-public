**Summary**: Brennan's 1993 follow-up to *Hands of Light* — expanding the healing model upward with two new dimensions (haric level and core star), inward with the psychology of the mask/lower/higher self and time conglomerates, and outward with the relational dynamics of auric field interactions and negative contracts.

## Overview

*Light Emerging* is [Barbara Ann Brennan's](../thinkers/barbara-ann-brennan.md) second major work and the systematic companion to *[Hands of Light](hands-of-light.md)* (1987). Where *Hands of Light* maps the anatomy of the Human Energy Field (seven layers, five character structures, complete healing spectrum), *Light Emerging* addresses the question: *what is the healing process from the inside — from the patient's experience, the healer-physician collaboration, and the deepest spiritual dimensions of the field?*

The book introduces two entirely new levels of the human energy system — the [haric level](../concepts/hara-level.md) (intentionality) and the [core star](../concepts/core-star.md) (divine essence) — that underlie the seven-layer auric field described in *Hands of Light*. These additions transform Brennan's model from a seven-layer anatomy into a [four-dimensional framework](../concepts/four-dimensions-of-being.md).

The book is organised around a new paradigm of health grounded in holographic physics (Pribram, Bohm, Harman) and the creative process: illness is understood as blocked creative energy arising from disconnection from the core, and healing as the progressive re-illumination of that core through all dimensions of being.

## Structure

The book is structured in six parts plus introduction, appendices, and conclusion:

**Introduction**: The creative process and a new paradigm of healing.

**Part I: Overview of Healing in Time**
- Chapter 1: The gift of healing — HSP, the core being, the mask/lower/higher self, time conglomerates
- Chapter 2: Four dimensions of creative energies — the four-level model; haric level; core star; detailed description of all seven HEF layers; chakras
- Chapter 3: A view of healing as a holographic experience — M-1/M-2/M-3 metaphysics; Pribram's holographic brain; seven holographic premises of reality; critique of mechanistic medicine
- Chapter 4: Honouring and balancing the system — the balancing system; illness as ignored signals; imbalance → disease

**Part II: Techniques for Healing in Time**
- Chapter 5: When the healer is not the physician or therapist
- Chapter 6: When the healer is part of the physician team

**Part III: The Experience of Healing**
- Chapter 7: Seven stages of healing — from denial to creation; Elisabeth Kübler-Ross's death-and-dying stages extended
- Chapter 8: Seven levels of the healing process — needs corresponding to each auric level; reframing illness as a life lesson

**Part IV: Creating a Healing Plan**
- Chapter 9: Energies of the earth — foundation of life; physical environment, food, nature
- Chapter 10: Your physical body as spiritual habitat — exercise, sleep, body care
- Chapter 11: Healing through love and letting go of perfectionism — colour breathing meditations
- Chapter 12: Healing through self-awareness — judgment charts; fear work

**Part V: Healing Relationships**
- Chapter 13: Creating healthy relationships — negative contracts; how childhood beliefs create relational traps
- Chapter 14: Three types of auric field interactions — harmonic induction, bioplasmic streamers, cords of light
- Chapter 15: Observations of auric interactions in relationships — detailed mapping of all five character structures' defensive auric patterns (five reactions each)

**Part VI: Healing Spiritual Realities**
- Chapter 16: The process of guidance and life — working with spirit guides (Heyoan); Kashmiri Shaivism and world peace
- Chapter 17: Intentionality and the hara dimension — hara line anatomy, three points, dysfunction, healing techniques
- Chapter 18: The divine core — core star anatomy, dysfunction, healing, Core Star Meditation

## Key New Concepts

### The Four Dimensions of Being
*Light Emerging* extends the *Hands of Light* model from seven layers to [four nested dimensions](../concepts/four-dimensions-of-being.md):
1. Physical world
2. Auric/HEF level (the seven layers described in *Hands of Light*)
3. [Haric level](../concepts/hara-level.md) — the dimension of intentionality and life purpose
4. [Core star level](../concepts/core-star.md) — the dimension of divine essence and creative source

This vertical expansion adds structural precision to the question of *why* people become ill at the deepest level: chronic misalignment with life purpose (haric dysfunction) and disconnection from the divine core are the primary causal factors that auric-level work alone cannot address.

### Hara Level (Chapter 17)
The hara line runs through the centre of the body from a point above the head to the core of the earth. Three points on the line carry distinct functions:
- **Individuation Point (ID Point)**: inverted funnel above the head; individual's connection to God/divine source; the reason for incarnation
- **Soul Seat**: diffuse light in the chest area; carries the soul's longing — the felt sense of life purpose
- **Tan Tien**: gold membrane below the navel; the "one note" that holds the body in manifestation; connects to the core of the earth

Illness that does not respond to auric-level healing often has its root in haric dysfunction: a disconnected ID point (cynicism/atheism), a shrouded soul seat (loss of meaning), or a damaged tan tien (chronic back problems, identity fragmentation).

### Core Star (Chapter 18)
Located about 1.5 inches below the navel on the midline, the core star is the divine essence — the individuated expression of the divine within each person. It precedes and underlies the haric level and is the source from which creative energy originates.

Brennan's Core Star Meditation (channelled from Heyoan) moves sequentially through alignment of the hara points and then opens a "corridor" from the core star through all dimensions to the physical, flooding the cell, body, and field with divine creative energy.

### Mask Self / Lower Self / Higher Self
Introduced in Chapter 1, this tripartite psychology organises the psyche around three aspects:
- **Higher Self** (divine core, God-self): the source of wisdom, love, and courage
- **Lower Self** (separated self): the part that acts from the illusion of separation; the source of "negative pleasure" from releasing pent-up pain; honest about its destructiveness, unlike the mask
- **Mask Self**: the protective facade that denies the lower self and the wound; blames others; projects; avoids responsibility

Illness arises from the mask's chronic suppression of the wound (and the energy that the wound contains), creating the conditions for disease.

### Time Conglomerates (Chapter 1)
Traumatic experiences arrest the development of a part of the psyche at the age the trauma occurred. These frozen fragments form clusters — "time conglomerates" — that replay in adult life whenever circumstances resemble the original wound. Healing involves thawing these frozen portions progressively, allowing the arrested parts of the psyche to mature.

### Negative Contracts (Chapter 13)
Unconscious behavioural agreements formed in early life (especially parent-child dynamics) that persist into adult relationships. Each contract encodes a belief ("I must take care of others to receive love"), a price paid to maintain the belief, and the true cost — the areas of life that become limited as a result. The chart format developed in the book provides a clinical tool for mapping and dissolving these patterns.

### Three Types of Auric Field Interactions (Chapter 14)
In relationships, the fourth-level (relational) field interacts in three distinct ways:
1. **Harmonic induction**: pulsation rates of one field induce changes in another (like tuning forks; the mechanism by which being with a teacher raises frequency)
2. **Bioplasmic streamers**: coloured streams of fourth-level plasma flow between people, carrying the emotional content of interaction
3. **Cords of light**: connecting strands that form between chakras in close relationships, based on the modelling of childhood relational bonds

### Holographic Model of Health (Chapter 3)
Brennan adopts Willis Harman's distinction between M-1 (matter gives rise to mind), M-2 (matter and mind as parallel), and M-3 (mind/consciousness gives rise to matter) and argues that healing requires M-3. Drawing on Pribram's holographic brain model and Bohm's implicate order, she presents seven holographic premises showing that consciousness, not matter, is the fundamental reality — with direct implications for healing (each piece contains the whole; past/future are accessible; consciousness creates the experience of health or dis-ease).

## Critique

**The hara and core star**: These two levels are described entirely through HSP observation. Unlike the HEF (which has at least partial empirical support through bioenergetic field measurements), the hara line and core star have no independent measurement correlates. Brennan acknowledges this but argues that the absence of instrumentation is a limitation of technology, not of the phenomena.

**Holographic premises**: Brennan's use of holographic physics is inspirational rather than scientific. Pribram's brain-as-hologram model and Bohm's implicate order are influential but contested scientific proposals; Brennan extrapolates from them to healing claims that the physics does not directly support.

**Case studies**: The book includes compelling individual cases (Sarah's knee healing without surgery; cancer cases; fibroids resolved). These are anecdotal reports and do not constitute clinical evidence. The absence of controls, long-term follow-up, and independent verification limits their evidentiary value.

**Note**: Mainstream medicine does not recognise the hara line, core star, or auric field as anatomical realities. The book's paradigm explicitly requires moving from M-1 to M-3 metaphysics — a shift that mainstream science does not accept. The framework should be read as a phenomenological and clinical map, not as established physiology.
