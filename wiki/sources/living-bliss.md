## Overview

*Living Bliss* is Shealy's most comprehensive autobiographical and synthesising work. It introduces **[Conscientious Psychology](../concepts/conscientious-psychology.md)** — his culminating contribution to the field — and presents the **[Five Sacred Rings](../concepts/five-sacred-rings.md)**: five acupuncture-based treatment circuits, each paired with an essential oil blend, that Shealy claims can raise DHEA, oxytocin, and neurotensin, eliminate free radicals, and regenerate telomeres.

The book moves between autobiography (fifty years of medical innovation), clinical framework (the role of conscientiousness, DHEA, and oxytocin in health), empirical research summaries (the Longevity Project; oxytocin studies), and therapeutic protocols (the Sacred Rings, Biogenics self-regulation, lifestyle steps).

Caroline Myss writes the foreword, identifying conscientiousness as the operationalization of the "taking responsibility for your life" principle she has taught for three decades. William Tiller contributes a physics appendix explaining how the Sacred Rings may work within his model of multiple levels of reality.

## Structure and Key Chapters

**Chapter One: Presenting the Roots of Conscientious Psychology**
Shealy's entry into medicine, his background in neurosurgery, and the realization — from treating 30,000 patients — that 97% of people do not follow four basic health habits. He introduces the book's organizing concept: conscientiousness as the missing key.

**Chapter Two: The Makings of a Life**
Personal history: nurturing parents, life on the farm, marriage to Mary Charlotte ("Chardy"). The crisis: her diagnosis with myelodysplastic syndrome (2010) and death (2011) after a thirteen-month battle. Her death became the impetus for his deepest research into conscientiousness and oxytocin.

**Chapter Three: Research Linking Conscientiousness to Longevity**
Extended treatment of Friedman & Martin's *Longevity Project* (2011), the culmination of an 80-year study of 1,500 people, which found conscientiousness to be the strongest personality predictor of longevity. Key findings: conscientiousness measured in childhood predicts longevity into old age; divorce is a health hazard; exercise matters; career engagement predicts male longevity.

**Chapter Four: Clinical Innovations — Tackling Chronic Pain and Depression**
The technical history of Shealy's innovations: dorsal column stimulation (1967), TENS (Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation — Shealy holds the patent and considers himself "the father of TENS"), Cranial Electrical Stimulation (CES/Liss device), the Shealy RelaxMate, facet rhizotomy, and Autogenic Training/Biogenics. His pain clinic achieved 85% success treating patients who had failed all other treatments.

**Chapter Five: Tapping the Knowledge of the Ages**
Shealy's exploration of energy medicine, acupuncture, past life regression therapy (PLT), medical intuition, and the founding of the American Holistic Medical Association (AHMA, 1978). Key encounters: Henry Rucker (psychic diagnostician), Robert Leichtman (internist with 96% intuitive diagnostic accuracy), Caroline Myss (1984 meeting, leading to ABSMI co-founding). Shealy's claimed past life as Dr. John Elliotson (1795–1868, London physician who introduced mesmerism and narcotics for pain control in England).

**Chapter Six: Lifestyle Changes to Increase Health and Happiness**
The Ten Steps to bliss:
1. BMI 18–24
2. 5–7 servings fruits/vegetables daily
3. Exercise 30+ min, 5 days/week
4. No smoking
5. 7+ hours sleep
6. 20 minutes daily Autogenic Training/self-regulation
7. Positive attitude
8. Social network
9. Sex with a loving partner (raises oxytocin)
10. Quality supplements

Extended sections on diet (Shealy strongly rejects the cholesterol/saturated fat narrative; argues carbohydrates and trans fats are the real threat), exercise, sleep, supplements, and the five recommended medical tests (hs-CRP, homocysteine, free radicals, DHEA, cholesterol).

**Chapter Seven: Boosting Oxytocin and DHEA — the Bliss Therapies**
The core clinical chapter. Introduces the Five Sacred Rings in detail with specific acupuncture point circuits and corresponding essential oil blends. See [Five Sacred Rings](../concepts/five-sacred-rings.md). Includes patient case studies demonstrating Ring Air's effects on PTSD, autism, ADHD, and depression.

**Chapter Eight: Lessons Learned as Student, Teacher, and Mentor**
Autobiographical: formative teachers (Dr. Talmage Peele, Dr. Eugene Stead), clinical journey, founding of Holos University Graduate Seminary (HUGS), relocation from Wisconsin to Missouri, relationship with Caroline Myss as ongoing mutual student-teacher.

**Chapter Nine: The Future of Conscientious Psychology**
Endowment of the Mary Charlotte Bayles Shealy Chair in Conscientious Psychology at Missouri State University; the research program at Holos Institutes Health; telomere regeneration research; the copper pyramid and quartz room at the Holos building. Shealy's vision for the last decade of his life.

**Afterword**: Personal telomere measurement data (2007–2014) showing growth under the Five Bliss Oils regimen (17.36% increase over 7 years vs. expected 25% decrease).

**Appendix (William A. Tiller)**: Theoretical physics framework: Tiller's biobodysuit model (soul self — three layers; personality self — two layers; spirit/God self — core) and how the Sacred Rings may function through the informational layer of reality not accessible to conventional physics.

## Key Concepts

- **[Conscientious Psychology](../concepts/conscientious-psychology.md)**: conscientiousness as the personality trait most predictive of health and longevity; a framework for developing it
- **[Oxytocin and Health](../concepts/oxytocin-and-health.md)**: oxytocin deficiency as the root cause of depression, ADHD, autism, addiction, OCD, and borderline personality; Ring Air as oxytocin booster
- **[Five Sacred Rings](../concepts/five-sacred-rings.md)**: Ring Fire, Air, Water, Earth, Crystal — each a circuit of acupuncture points activatable by GHz stimulation, massage/tapping, listening to CDs, or applying essential oil blends
- **[DHEA and Vitality](../concepts/dhea-and-vitality.md)**: extended with four methods to raise DHEA: progesterone cream, Youth Formula supplement, magnesium lotion, Ring Fire
- Telomere regeneration: DNA frequencies (54–78 GHz) and Bliss Oils can regenerate telomeres (4.5% annual growth vs. 1% expected shrinkage)
- Autogenic Training/Biogenics: 20-minute daily self-regulation practice managing 80% of stress illnesses

## Relation to Other Sources in the Wiki

- **Myss's *Anatomy of the Spirit***: Conscientiousness operationalizes Myss's principle that "biography is biology" — it is the trait through which people take responsibility for their energy, history, and health.
- **Shealy's *Sacred Healing***: This book extends Sacred Healing's healer documentation and DHEA research into a comprehensive lifestyle framework; the Sacred Rings are the clinical evolution of his GigaTENS electrotherapy work.
- **Myss's *Sacred Contracts***: Shealy believes each person chooses their life purpose before birth. Conscientiousness is the trait that allows a person to fulfill that purpose.
- **Hillman's acorn theory**: Shealy's belief that "you chose to come here to learn specific lessons and fulfil your purpose" parallels Hillman's daimon concept — though Shealy frames it theologically (Divine) while Hillman frames it psychologically (imaginal).

## Critical Assessment

**Strengths**: The conscientious psychology framework is grounded in the strongest longitudinal dataset in personality research (the Longevity Project). The oxytocin section is well-referenced (bibliography lists 100+ studies). The therapeutic protocols are specific and actionable. Shealy's clinical history is remarkable and documented.

**Limitations**: The Five Sacred Rings research is conducted largely by Shealy himself, without independent replication or peer-reviewed publication. Telomere regeneration claims are based on personal measurements and a small pilot (30 individuals). The past life regression chapter, while personally significant to Shealy, cannot be evaluated scientifically. Cholesterol and dietary fat claims are contested within mainstream nutrition science.

**Note**: Mainstream medicine accepts that conscientiousness is health-protective (consistent with the Longevity Project). Oxytocin's role in social bonding and mental health is well-supported. The specific claims about the Sacred Rings, telomere regeneration via oils, and DHEA as measurable life force remain scientifically unvalidated.
