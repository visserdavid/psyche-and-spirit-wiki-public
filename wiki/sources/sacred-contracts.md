## Overview

*Sacred Contracts* is Myss's most systematically developed work. Where *Anatomy of the Spirit* (1996) established the link between biography and biology through the seven-chakra energy model, this book asks a deeper question: why do we have the biographies we do? Her answer is the Sacred Contract — a pre-birth agreement between the soul and the Divine that specifies the lessons, relationships, and purpose of a lifetime.

The book has two interlocking parts: a theological and mythological argument for the existence of soul-level agreements, and a practical self-discovery system based on identifying twelve personal archetypes and mapping them onto a twelve-house wheel.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Every soul has a Sacred Contract** — a set of agreements made before birth specifying what to learn, whom to meet, and what to contribute. Myss draws on Plato's myth of Er, Vedic traditions, and the covenant narratives of Abraham, Moses, Jesus, Muhammad, and the Buddha.

2. **Archetypes are the language of the Contract** — twelve archetypal patterns shape each person's psyche, relationships, and challenges. Four are universal (the survival archetypes); eight are personal companions chosen by the soul.

3. **The Archetypal Wheel / Chart of Origin** — a twelve-house map (adapted from astrology) where personal archetypes are placed to reveal the domains of life governed by each pattern.

4. **The chakra system as spiritual backbone** — Myss extends the chakra model from *Anatomy of the Spirit* to include an eighth chakra (above the crown), which she sees as the seat of the Sacred Contract itself.

5. **Five stages of Contract unfolding** — Contact, Heeding the Call, Renaming, Assignments, Surrender. Illustrated through the biographies of Abraham, Jesus, Muhammad, and the Buddha.

6. **Grace and charism** — Divine infusions of energy (charism, from the Greek for gift) that empower fulfillment of the Contract. Grace is the force that helps navigate the Contract's challenges.

7. **Shadow work is essential** — Each archetype has a shadow expression that blocks growth. Confronting shadows (especially the survival archetypes' shadows) is integral to living out the Contract.

8. **The Vasalisa story** — Myss draws on [Clarissa Pinkola Estés](../thinkers/clarissa-pinkola-estes.md)'s reading of the Vasalisa/Baba Yaga tale (see [*Women Who Run with the Wolves*](women-who-run-with-the-wolves.md)) to illustrate the Sacred Contract's stages of unfolding; Estés also gave the book a cover endorsement.

## Structure

- **Introduction**: The premise — each life has a hidden sacred purpose.
- **Chapters 1–3**: The Sacred Contract framework — what it is, precedents in world traditions, five stages of unfolding.
- **Chapter 4**: The four survival archetypes (Child, Victim, Prostitute, Saboteur) in depth.
- **Chapter 5**: Identifying personal archetypes — the interview process, selection of eight companions.
- **Chapter 6**: Chakras as the spiritual backbone — linking archetypes to energy centers.
- **Chapters 7–10**: The Archetypal Wheel — construction, interpretation, and practical use.
- **Chapter 11**: Healing challenges through the Archetypal Wheel — case studies.
- **Appendix — Gallery of Archetypes**: Descriptions of over 70 archetypes with examples from myth, film, literature, and religion.

## Relationship to Earlier Work

*Sacred Contracts* deepens and complicates *Anatomy of the Spirit*:

- The chakra system is retained and extended (adding the 8th chakra and archetypal layer).
- The "biography is biology" principle is reframed: biography is the expression of a pre-birth contract, not just accumulated emotional stress.
- Myss's earlier focus on *illness as a spiritual lesson* becomes *life itself as a sacred curriculum*, not only health crises.
- [Symbolic sight](../concepts/symbolic-sight.md) is expanded into the "Three Column Model" for seeing life simultaneously on material, psychological, and sacred planes.
