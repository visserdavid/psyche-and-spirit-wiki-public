# Sacred Healing

**Summary**: Shealy's 1999 survey of sacred healing — tracing the history of spiritual healing across world religions, profiling outstanding healers, and advancing bioelectromagnetic and holistic frameworks as the foundation of twenty-first-century medicine.

**Sources**:
- [Shealy, C. N. (1999). *Sacred healing: The curing power of energy and spirituality*. Element Books.](sacred-healing.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

---

## Overview

*Sacred Healing: The Curing Power of Energy and Spirituality* was published in 1999 by Element Books. It is authored by [C. Norman Shealy](../thinkers/norman-shealy.md), M.D., Ph.D., with a foreword by [Caroline Myss](../thinkers/caroline-myss.md). Shealy brings together three decades of clinical investigation into healers, energy medicine, electrotherapy, and spirituality to argue that healing originates from a Divine Source, that illness stems from imbalance in spirit, and that energy-based medicine will be central to twenty-first-century healthcare.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Healing comes from the Divine**: Shealy's central conclusion after twenty-five years investigating healers is that genuine healing — beyond what medicine and surgery can explain — originates from what he calls the Divine Source. The healer is a channel, not the origin.

2. **History of sacred healing**: Across every world religion and culture, healing and religion have been inseparable. Shealy surveys ancient Greece, Rome, early Christianity, Islam, and indigenous traditions to show that the modern medicalisation of health is historically anomalous. See [Spiritual Healing Traditions](../concepts/spiritual-healing-traditions.md).

3. **Outstanding healers**: Shealy documents remarkable healers he personally investigated: Olga Worrall, Harry Edwards, Ostad Hadi Parvarandeh, Philippine psychic surgeons, Edgar Cayce, and others. Over one hundred documented cases of healing — where patients with medically incurable conditions recovered — form the evidential core of the book. See [Prayer and Healing](../concepts/prayer-and-healing.md).

4. **Attitude as the foundation of health**: Shealy presents a detailed taxonomy of health-promoting versus health-depleting attitudes (forgiveness, tolerance, love, compassion vs. anger, resentment, fear, guilt). He argues that attitude is the single most important determinant of health and healing. See [Attitude and Health](../concepts/attitude-and-health.md).

5. **Bioelectromagnetism**: Shealy integrates the work of [Robert Becker](../thinkers/robert-becker.md) and others to argue that life is fundamentally electromagnetic. The body's energy field is a real, measurable phenomenon — not metaphorical — and illness is a disruption of this field. See [Bioelectromagnetism](../concepts/bioelectromagnetism.md).

6. **Electromagnetic Dysthymia (EMD)**: Shealy coins this term for a syndrome of chronic stress overload — produced by modern environmental, chemical, and electromagnetic stressors — that depletes DHEA, disrupts neurochemistry, and underlies most chronic disease. See [Electromagnetic Dysthymia](../concepts/electromagnetic-dysthymia.md).

7. **DHEA as life energy marker**: Shealy proposes DHEA (dehydroepiandrosterone) as a measurable biochemical correlate of life energy — a "chemical battery" that reflects overall vitality. Its decline tracks with illness and stress. See [DHEA and Vitality](../concepts/dhea-and-vitality.md).

8. **Electrotherapy**: Shealy documents a century of suppressed electrotherapy — TENS, Cranial Electrical Stimulation (CES/Liss device), electroacupuncture, and Microwave Resonance Therapy (GigaTENS at 52–78 GHz) — as practical tools for activating the body's bioelectric healing capacity. See [Electrotherapy](../concepts/electrotherapy.md).

9. **Prayer, placebo, and conventional medicine**: Shealy argues that medicine overestimates drug efficacy (85% of drugs lack documented evidence; 80% of conditions are self-limiting or stress-related) and underestimates healing, prayer, and attitude. The Byrd coronary care study is cited as evidence of prayer's measurable effects.

10. **Spirituality vs. religion**: Shealy distinguishes spirituality (direct communion with God/soul) from religion (dogma, institutional structure). Spirituality — as defined by forgiveness, love, compassion, faith, serenity — is the foundation of health and healing.

## Structure

The book has eleven chapters:
1. Introduction: What is Sacred Healing?
2. A Physician's Search for Sacred Healing (autobiography)
3. Religion and Sacred Healing in History
4. The Healer's Role in Medical History
5. Spirituality and Healing Reawakened
6. Outstanding Healers of Our Time
7. What is Sacred Healing?
8. Brain, Mind, and Spirit
9. Energy and Electromagnetism
10. Holy Water / Sacred Water
11. Electrotherapy and Healing

## Relation to Other Sources

Caroline Myss wrote the foreword, situating the book within the broader project they share. Where Myss's [*Anatomy of the Spirit*](anatomy-of-the-spirit.md) focuses on the internal energetic and archetypal dimensions of illness, Shealy's *Sacred Healing* is more empirical — cataloguing healers, clinical cases, and scientific frameworks. The two books are complementary: Myss provides the conceptual map; Shealy provides documentation and bioelectromagnetic grounding.

Shealy references his earlier collaboration with Myss in [*The Creation of Health*](creation-of-health.md) (1988; revised and retitled 1993).
