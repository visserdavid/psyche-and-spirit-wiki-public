# The Holographic Universe

**Summary**: Talbot (1991) synthesises David Bohm's implicate-order physics and Karl Pribram's holonomic brain model into a unified popular account of the holographic paradigm, arguing that the universe is a giant hologram and that this explains paranormal phenomena, mystical experience, multiple personality disorder, placebo effects, psychokinesis, NDEs, OBEs, and the human energy field.

## Overview

*The Holographic Universe* is the definitive popular synthesis of the [holographic paradigm](../concepts/holographic-healing-model.md). Written by Michael Talbot — a novelist who experienced paranormal phenomena throughout his life — it draws on two decades of evidence from physics, neuroscience, medicine, psychology, and parapsychology to argue that the universe at the deepest level is a hologram: a multidimensional interference pattern that projects the apparent three-dimensional world of objects and events.

The book's central figures are physicist David Bohm (University of London) and neurophysiologist Karl Pribram (Stanford University), who arrived independently at complementary holographic models — Bohm from dissatisfaction with quantum theory, Pribram from puzzles in brain science — and whose combined framework, Talbot argues, explains phenomena that no other model can accommodate.

## Part I: A Remarkable View of Reality

### Chapter 1: The Brain as Hologram (Pribram)

Karl Pribram's holographic brain model begins with a puzzle: where are memories stored? Neurophysiologist Karl Lashley trained rats in mazes, then removed portions of their cortex. No matter how much cortex he removed — up to 90 percent — the memories persisted (though with degraded quality). Memory, it seemed, was distributed throughout the brain rather than localised in specific cells.

In the mid-1960s Pribram read an article describing holography and was struck: holograms store information in a distributed way — any piece of a holographic plate contains the complete image (though with less resolution). The brain might work similarly. When he discovered that neurophysiologists Russell and Karen DeValois had found brain cells in the visual cortex respond not to patterns per se but to Fourier transforms (wave-form representations) of those patterns, the holographic model was confirmed: the brain uses Fourier mathematics — the same mathematics Gabor used to invent the hologram — to process information.

This explains not just distributed memory but:
- **Vast memory capacity**: 280,000,000,000,000,000,000 bits (von Neumann's estimate) storable by frequency-domain encoding
- **Associative memory**: a whiff of Proustian madeleine can retrieve an entire scene
- **Face recognition**: invariant recognition across viewing angles and years
- **Phantom limb pain**: the brain has encoded a hologram of the missing limb in its interference patterns
- **Photographic memory**: individuals with eidetic memory access larger regions of the distributed holographic storage

### Chapter 2: The Cosmos as Hologram (Bohm)

David Bohm developed his holographic cosmology from dissatisfaction with quantum theory's inability to explain quantum non-locality — the experimental finding (confirmed by Alain Aspect's 1982 experiment in Paris) that particles separated by arbitrary distances instantaneously correlate their behaviour.

**The implicate order**: Bohm proposed that underlying observable reality (the "explicate order") is a deeper level — the "implicate order" — in which everything is enfolded into everything else. The explicate world of separate objects is generated by continuous processes of "unfolding" from the implicate order and "enfolding" back into it. The entire universe is one undivided whole ("holomovement") in which apparent separateness is a product of limited observation.

**The ink-in-glycerine model**: Bohm illustrated the implicate order with a cylinder of viscous glycerine: drop ink in, turn the cylinder, and the drop spreads and disappears (enfolding into implicate order). Reverse the cylinder, and the drop reappears (unfolding into explicate order). Order is preserved in the glycerine even when invisible to the eye.

**Undivided wholeness**: Bohm argued that the tendency to fragment reality into separate things — particles, waves, mind, matter — is the root of contemporary problems, both scientific and civilisational. The universe is not made of separate things; it is a flowing holomovement in which apparent things are like whirlpools in a stream — distinguishable aspects of a single flow.

**Pribram + Bohm together**: The brain is a hologram perceiving a holographic universe. Reality as experienced is the brain's holographic interpretation of frequencies projected from the underlying implicate order. What we call the physical world is a domain of frequencies; reality as it appears is a frequency transform of the deeper order.

## Part II: Mind and Body

### Chapter 3: Holographic Model of Psychology

The holographic model provides new grounding for phenomena that challenge conventional psychology:

**Jung's collective unconscious**: In a holographic universe, consciousnesses are not isolated in skulls. Jung's discovery that his patients' dreams contained symbols from world mythologies they had no cultural contact with is explained: at the implicate level, all consciousness is interconnected. The collective unconscious is the implicate order experienced from within the mind. Psychologist Robert Anderson proposes that psychic abilities involve tapping the implicate order through resonance.

**Grof's transpersonal experiences**: Stanislav Grof's LSD research produced experiences that the conventional brain model cannot accommodate — detailed access to the history of the human race, experiences of other organisms and entities, and encounters with archetypal figures. Grof concluded that the brain cannot be the source of these experiences; they arise from the holographic implicate order of which the individual brain is a tuned receiver.

**Altered states**: Lucid dreaming (Fred Alan Wolf), synchronicity (Peat), and various altered-state phenomena become intelligible as temporary access to normally filtered implicate-order information.

### Chapter 4: The Singing Body Holographic

The body reflects holographic structure in multiple ways:

**Microsystem acupuncture**: The ear, hand, foot, and iris each contain a complete map of the entire body — the "holographic body" principle. Terry Oleson's controlled study (published in *Brain Research*) confirmed ear acupuncture diagnosis at 75.2% accuracy. Dale's naloxone studies show ear acupuncture analgesia is mediated by endorphins (same pathway as standard acupuncture).

**Valerie Hunt's bioelectric measurements**: UCLA biophysicist Valerie Hunt measured the bioelectric output of the human body with equipment capable of detecting frequencies up to 200,000 Hz (conventional ECG and EEG operate at 250 Hz max). She found consistent patterns corresponding to descriptions of the chakra system and human energy field. Healers Barbara Brennan and Rosalyn Bruyere participated in her studies; her readings showed specific high-frequency patterns when healers worked.

**Shafica Karagulla's clinical auric diagnosis**: Neurologist and psychiatrist Karagulla documented dozens of individuals with "higher sense perception" (HSP) who could accurately diagnose illness by reading the human energy field — independently confirming what Brennan reports from her own HSP perceptions.

**Dolores Krieger's Therapeutic Touch**: Developed with Dora Kunz; now widely practised by nurses. Studies demonstrate statistically significant increases in haemoglobin following TT sessions.

### Chapter 5: Pocketful of Miracles

The richest chapter for this wiki's themes: healing beyond conventional explanation.

**Placebo effect**: 35% of patients given inert placebos experience significant benefit. Placebos have been shown as effective as morphine in six studies. Conditions responsive to placebo include tumours, warts, angina pectoris, and multiple sclerosis symptoms. The Krebiozen case — Wright's tumours melted when he believed in the drug, returned when he read it was ineffective, and vanished again when his doctor told him the first reports were wrong — is Talbot's paradigm case of belief creating biology directly.

**Multiple Personality Disorder (MPD)**: Subpersonalities within a single individual can have different physiology: one personality is allergic to orange juice; the others are not. Eye colour, handedness, handwriting, and medication response differ across personalities. Burns heal differently. Francine Howland documented a case where a wasp sting's swelling appeared and disappeared as personalities switched. This demonstrates that the physical body is not a fixed material object but a holographic projection of the dominant personality's belief system.

**Psychokinesis**: PEAR lab experiments (Robert Jahn and Brenda Dunne) show that concentrated intention reliably affects random event generators and pinball machines in ways that cannot be attributed to chance. Effect is real but small; everyone tested shows it to some degree. Bohm's framework explains PK as consciousness-matter resonance through the implicate order rather than force transmission.

**Healing by intention**: Filipino and Hawaiian kahuna healers; fractured bones healed through sustained group prayer (Gardner in *British Medical Journal*); stigmata (Padre Pio, Therese Neumann) as psychosomatic PK; Jansenist convulsionaries' invulnerability to physical blows. Talbot argues all are expressions of mind acting on body through holographic mechanism.

**Miracles**: San Gennaro's liquefying blood; Lourdes cures (Vittorio Michelli's documented pelvic regeneration over four months); Sathya Sai Baba's materialisations witnessed by Erlendur Haraldsson. Talbot treats these as evidence of PK at different scales — the mind's holographic influence on matter.

### Chapter 6: Seeing Holographically

Extended sensory perception — seeing the energy field — interpreted holographically:

**Brennan's high sense perception (HSP)**: Barbara Brennan's seven-layer auric anatomy is presented as the observational output of holographic vision — direct perception of the frequency domain underlying the visible world. Talbot credits Brennan as the most detailed published account of the energy field.

**Eyeless sight**: Rosa Kuleshova and other documented cases of tactile "vision" through hands and feet — explained as HSP ability to access holographic frequency information through any surface area.

**The frequency domain**: Pribram's metaphor — without the brain's holographic processing, reality would appear as a blur of interfering frequencies. HSP individuals are perceiving more of the frequency domain before it is "projected" into the apparent physical world.

## Part III: Space and Time

### Chapter 7: Time and Mind

In a hologram, all events in an object's history are encoded in the interference patterns simultaneously. Talbot proposes the past is not lost but remains enfolded in the holographic implicate order, accessible to consciousness that can tune to the appropriate frequency.

**Retrocognition**: Stefan Ossowiecki and George McMullen's psychometric abilities — reading the history of objects — explained as accessing the encoded past hologram.

**Hauntings and apparitions**: Replays of intense past events encoded in the local holographic medium. The tendency for hauntings to occur at sites of violence suggests the intensity of the original event creates a stronger holographic record.

**Precognition**: Future events exist as potential holograms in the implicate order. Rhine card experiments, Helmut Schmidt's REG device, Puthoff and Targ's remote viewing at Stanford Research Institute, PEAR lab precognition data, Croiset's chair tests. The future is not fixed: Talbot introduces "hololeaps" — the act of precognition can shift the experiencer from one potential future hologram to another.

### Chapter 8: Travelling the Superhologram

OBEs and NDEs interpreted as consciousness accessing other levels of the holographic universe:

**OBEs**: Robert Monroe's out-of-body travels; Janet Lee Mitchell's experiments at ASPR. OBE perceivers can accurately describe distant locations they have not physically visited.

**NDEs**: Ring's sixteen-element core NDE model explained: the tunnel (entering a different frequency domain), the being of light (Ground Luminosity), the life review (accessing the holographic record of one's biography), the city of light (a higher-order holographic region). Life plans (Whitton's work) exist in the implicate order as pre-birth intentions — parallel to Myss's Sacred Contracts.

**After-death realm**: Swedenborg, near-death research, and mystical traditions converge on a realm of thought-responsive reality: environment created by expectation and belief — the most literal possible expression of consciousness creating reality.

### Chapter 9: Return to the Dreamtime

The holographic paradigm reconciles science and mysticism:

- **Aboriginal Dreamtime**: An alternate order of reality in which the implicate and explicate are interchangeable — identical in structure to Bohm's model
- **Sri Aurobindo's supramental**: The evolution of consciousness toward the "Supermind" — a level at which the implicate order becomes directly accessible — parallels Ring's Omega Hypothesis
- **Prigogine's dissipative structures**: Complex systems evolve toward higher order by processing energy — physical parallel to consciousness evolution
- **The universe's intelligence**: Bohm's proposal that electrons and rocks possess rudimentary intelligence — everything participates in the holomovement

## Critique

**Popular extrapolation**: Talbot was a novelist, not a physicist. His use of Bohm and Pribram is inspirational rather than rigorous. Neither Bohm nor Pribram endorsed all the applications Talbot makes of their theories.

**Parapsychology evidence**: Many of the studies Talbot cites (PEAR lab, remote viewing, Kirlian photography) remain contested or have not been replicated under stricter protocols. The evidentiary bar for paranormal phenomena is (appropriately) higher than for normal phenomena.

**Victim-blaming risk**: The claim that consciousness creates biology can, if misapplied, imply that illness is chosen or deserved. Talbot does not address this risk systematically.

**Non-falsifiability**: The holographic model can accommodate almost any evidence by appeal to as-yet-unmeasured holographic processes. A framework that explains everything explains nothing distinctively.

**Note**: Mainstream physics and neuroscience do not endorse the holographic paradigm as Talbot presents it. Bohm's implicate order is a minority position in physics; Pribram's holonomic brain model is one of several competing frameworks in neuroscience.
