# Why People Don't Heal and How They Can

**Summary**: Myss's third major work diagnosing the cultural and psychological obstacles to healing — centred on "woundology" — and offering a practical three-column framework of Tribal, Individual, and Symbolic power for igniting what she calls the healing fire.

## Context and purpose

This book grew out of Myss's observation that many people attending her workshops were not healing despite sustained effort. Rather than asking "how do people heal?" she shifted focus to "why don't they?" The book functions as both a cultural diagnosis and a practical guide, aimed at readers already familiar with the chakra system introduced in *Anatomy of the Spirit*.

## Key argument

The core problem, Myss argues, is [woundology](../concepts/woundology.md): the cultural pattern of using personal wounds as social currency — as sources of intimacy, identity, and leverage — rather than moving through them toward genuine healing. People become defined by their wounds; the wound gives them community, attention, and a form of power that health cannot offer. As long as the wound is profitable, healing is resisted.

Woundology is embedded in a broader set of [Five Myths of Healing](../concepts/five-myths-of-healing.md) that Myss systematically dismantles: that life is defined by wounds, that being healthy means isolation, that pain signals destruction, that illness results from negativity, and that true change is impossible.

## Three ages of power

A distinctive contribution of this book is the [Three Ages of Power](../concepts/three-ages-of-power.md) framework, mapping the evolution of human consciousness across astrological ages:

- **Age of Aries** (2000 BC–0 AD): Tribal Power — group identity, law, collective survival
- **Age of Pisces** (0–2000 AD): Individual Power — choice, self-development, mind, dualism
- **Age of Aquarius** (entering now): Symbolic Power — unity, energetic perception, holism

Each age contributed a layer of consciousness that remains active. Healing requires integrating all three, not just living out of Tribal reflex.

## The cellular energy bank

Myss introduces the [Cellular Energy Bank](../concepts/cellular-energy-bank.md) metaphor: each person has approximately one hundred units of life force per day. Holding onto wounds, grudges, and past events drains this energy debt into cell tissue — contributing to illness. [Spirit Retrieval](../concepts/spirit-retrieval.md) — calling energy back from investments in the past — is a foundational healing practice.

## Three Column Sight

The book's primary practical tool is the [Three Column Sight](../concepts/symbolic-sight.md) model (later extended in *Sacred Contracts*): interpreting any challenge through three lenses simultaneously — Tribal, Individual, and Symbolic — to identify where energy is being lost and how it can be reclaimed.

## Chakra healing practices

Chapters 7 and 8 offer detailed visualizations for each chakra and detailed meditations on each of the seven sacraments as healing tools. These extend the theoretical framework of *Anatomy of the Spirit* into a direct healing protocol, chapter by chapter per chakra.

## Relationship to other works

- Extends [*Anatomy of the Spirit*](anatomy-of-the-spirit.md): assumes familiarity with the chakra/sacrament/sefirot system and moves from theory into practical healing method.
- Anticipates [*Sacred Contracts*](sacred-contracts.md): the Three Column model appears here in embryonic form; the eighth chakra (grace bank account) and archetypal reasoning are introduced but not yet fully developed.
- The dedication is to [Rachel Naomi Remen, M.D.](../thinkers/rachel-naomi-remen.md) and Daniel Lowenstein, M.D., reflecting Myss's dialogue with integrative medicine.

## Structure

- **Part One: Why People Don't Heal** — Woundology, the five myths, chakras and the astrological ages, beginning the journey toward Individual and Symbolic power
- **Part Two: How They Can** — Finding the path, igniting the healing fire, visualizing the chakras, using chakras and sacraments for healing
