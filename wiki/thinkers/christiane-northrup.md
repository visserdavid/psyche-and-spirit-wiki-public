# Christiane Northrup

**Summary**: OB/GYN physician, co-founder of Women to Women health clinic, collaborator with Caroline Myss, and author of *Women's Bodies, Women's Wisdom* (1994) — the landmark text integrating conventional gynecology with energy medicine, feminist psychology, and mind-body science.

## Overview

Christiane Northrup, M.D. (b. 1949), is an obstetrician-gynecologist and author whose primary contribution to this wiki's themes is the clinical application of energy medicine to women's reproductive health. Her landmark work *Women's Bodies, Women's Wisdom* (Bantam Books, 1994) integrates [Caroline Myss](caroline-myss.md) and [Norman Shealy](norman-shealy.md)'s chakra-organ-illness mapping with conventional OB/GYN medicine, psychoneuroimmunology (Candace Pert, Joan Borysenko), feminist psychology (Anne Wilson Schaef), and mind-body science.

Northrup trained at Dartmouth Medical School and completed her OB/GYN residency at Maine Medical Center. In December 1985 she co-founded **Women to Women** clinic in Yarmouth, Maine — one of the first women's health practices explicitly integrating emotional, psychological, and spiritual dimensions into conventional gynecological care. The clinic represented a clinical embodiment of the principles in this book.

## Collaboration with Caroline Myss

Myss contacted Northrup in autumn 1990 after reading an article Northrup had written. Their collaboration brought Myss's energy diagnostic work into gynecological clinical practice: Myss conducted energy evaluations of Northrup's patients, providing chakra-level readings that Northrup compared against her clinical findings and used to guide holistic treatment planning.

Myss describes this collaboration in *Anatomy of the Spirit* as exemplifying the coming of age of medical intuition — the integration of energetic diagnosis into mainstream clinical medicine by a fully credentialed physician. Northrup's willingness to incorporate Myss's evaluations into her practice, and to present case studies illustrating their accuracy, provided clinical grounding for the energy anatomy framework.

Northrup's acknowledgements in *Women's Bodies, Women's Wisdom* describe Myss as "the brilliance and generosity of friendship that nourished and inspired" — and credits the energy anatomy framework underlying the entire book to the Myss-Shealy collaboration and *The Creation of Health* (1988).

## Central Contributions

### Applying Energy Anatomy to Women's Health

Northrup's primary contribution to the wiki's frameworks is the *specific* application of the chakra-organ mapping to women's reproductive system. While Myss and Shealy developed the general framework in *The Creation of Health*, Northrup showed in clinical detail:

- How **second-chakra issues** (relationships, creativity, money, shame, the rape and prostitute archetypes) manifest as fibroids, endometriosis, ovarian cysts, and pelvic pain
- How **fourth-chakra issues** (giving without receiving, grief, resentment, suppressed need for nurturing) contribute to breast cancer and heart disease in women
- How **first-chakra tribal inheritance** produces the foundational beliefs (worthlessness, body shame, permission to receive) that become the soil for reproductive disorders

Her clinical case studies — particularly the fibroid remissions following emotional clearing work — are the most detailed documented examples in the wiki of the energy anatomy principle operating in women's second-chakra organs. See [Female Energy System](../concepts/female-energy-system.md).

### The Bodymind Framework

Northrup's detailed engagement with Candace Pert's neuropeptide research extends the [psychoneuroimmunology](../concepts/psychoneuroimmunology.md) framework into gynecology: the uterus, ovaries, and immune cells produce and respond to the same neuropeptides as the brain. This means pelvic organs literally think and feel — making the energy anatomy framework not merely metaphorical but consistent with the known biochemistry of the reproductive system.

### The Addictive System

Northrup's adoption of Anne Wilson Schaef's "addictive system" framework provides the cultural-sociological layer of the biography-is-biology argument: women's health is damaged not only by individual emotional patterns but by the systematised suppression of women's inner guidance, body knowledge, and self-authority that patriarchal culture demands. See [Addictive System](../concepts/addictive-system.md).

### The Menstrual Cycle as Information System

Northrup reframes the menstrual cycle as a monthly intelligence system with two alternating modes — outward expansion (follicular/ovulatory) and inward knowing (luteal/premenstrual) — arguing that PMS is the body's escalated signal when the premenstrual phase's information is consistently suppressed. See [Menstrual Cycle as Wisdom](../concepts/menstrual-cycle-as-wisdom.md).

### Twelve Steps to Healing

Northrup's healing programme adapts AA's twelve-step structure to create health within and despite the addictive cultural system. See [Twelve Steps to Healing (Northrup)](../concepts/twelve-steps-to-healing-northrup.md).

## Position in the Wiki

Northrup represents the **women's health** application of the energy medicine tradition. She is the figure who takes the abstract principles developed by Myss, Shealy, and Brennan and shows what they look like when applied clinically to the specific organs, conditions, and life contexts of women's bodies.

Her position at the intersection of:
- Conventional OB/GYN (trained practitioner with hospital privileges)
- Energy medicine (Myss and Shealy's framework)
- Feminist psychology (Schaef's addictive system)
- Mind-body science (Pert, Borysenko)
- Holistic nutrition (Kushi, macrobiotics)

makes her work a synthesis point for the wiki's core themes in their most clinically specific form.

## Critique

**Later trajectory**: Northrup's post-2010 work has moved increasingly away from mainstream medicine toward positions that have been widely criticised as medical misinformation. These include:
- Promotion of unproven treatments for COVID-19
- Anti-vaccine positions that made her one of the most prominent medical voices spreading vaccine misinformation during the COVID-19 pandemic
- Claims about 5G technology and microchipping that are not supported by evidence

These positions have led to her removal from several institutional affiliations and sharp criticism from the medical community. Her standing in mainstream medicine has declined significantly as a result.

**Relevance caveat**: This does not directly affect the material cited from *Women's Bodies, Women's Wisdom* (1994) or from *Anatomy of the Spirit* (1996), where she appears as a respected clinical collaborator whose work was ground-breaking in integrating energy medicine with mainstream gynecology. But readers should be aware that her subsequent trajectory illustrates the risk — present in multiple figures in this wiki — of moving from boundary-pushing but scientifically engaged work into positions that abandon evidential standards.

**Claims in the 1994 book**: Even within *Women's Bodies, Women's Wisdom*, several claims — the fibroid remission case studies, the chakra-organ-illness mappings, the dietary recommendations for endometriosis — were not supported by controlled clinical trials in 1994 and are not accepted by mainstream gynecology today.
