# Daskalos (Stylianos Atteshlis)

**Summary**: Cypriot mystic healer (1912–1995), known to his students as "Daskalos" (Teacher), who led a lay esoteric-Christian circle ("Researchers of Truth") in Strovolos, near Nicosia, teaching a syncretic cosmology of reincarnation, thought-form "elementals," out-of-body travel, and hands-on healing, chronicled by sociologist Kyriacos Markides across a three-book series beginning with *The Magus of Strovolos*.

**Sources**:
- [Markides, K. C. (1985). *The magus of Strovolos: The extraordinary world of a spiritual healer*. Routledge & Kegan Paul.](../sources/magus-of-strovolos.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-07-10

## Biography

Spyros Sathi — known to his students as Daskalos — was born in Cyprus in 1912 to a family with English and Scottish connections through his father, a career naval officer. As a young man he developed a reputation across the island as a healer and diagnostician of the "netherworld," inspiring both fascination and fear among locals, some of whom (per Markides) were warned by parish priests to avoid him. He served in various capacities during Cyprus's twentieth-century upheavals — the anti-colonial EOKA struggle of the 1950s and the intercommunal violence of the 1960s and 1974 invasion — episodes in which Markides records him claiming to have used clairvoyance and personal intervention (rather than force) to save lives on both the Greek and Turkish Cypriot sides. He led the "Researchers of Truth" circle from his home in Strovolos, a Nicosia suburb, training disciples including Iacovos, Kostas, and Theophanis of Paphos, and made annual teaching visits to affiliated circles in Greece. He died in 1995.

## Teaching and Practice

Daskalos taught through a combination of private consultations (healing sessions conducted for visitors, often diagnosing by photograph or by touch) and structured lessons to his inner circle in the "Stoa," a converted space behind his house, framed by Christian Orthodox ritual elements (icons, incense, a ceremonial "Unpointed Sword") alongside explicitly Kabbalistic and Gnostic symbolism. He claimed his central teachings were transmitted through a discarnate "Master," Father Yohannan (identified with John the Evangelist), whom he stated could speak through him in trance.

His system rests on a three-body model of the person (material, psychic/etheric, and noetic bodies), a hierarchy of "worlds" (matter, images, ideas, causes), and the doctrine that willed thought and desire condense into semi-autonomous "elementals" that shape both personal habit and, in his account of possession cases, pathological external intrusion. See [Daskalos's Cosmology](../concepts/daskalos-cosmology.md), [Elementals (Daskalos)](../concepts/elementals-daskalos.md), and [Exomatosis and Exteriorization](../concepts/exomatosis.md) for the detailed framework.

## Healing Claims

Daskalos is presented by Markides as an unpaid healer (he refused payment or gifts for his services throughout the book) whose methods included hands-on spinal manipulation, diagnosis at a distance via photograph, and claimed "dematerialization" of tumors and diseased tissue. He insisted that healing outcomes are always subordinate to the patient's Karma — a healer cannot cure what "Holy Providence" (his term) does not permit — and that a healer who deliberately assumes a patient's karmic debt (rather than merely channeling healing energy) will suffer physically as a consequence, a claim he applied to his own 1983 leg injury.

## Reliability as a Source

As with [Malachi Martin](malachi-martin.md), evaluating Daskalos's claims runs into the limits of the source material: virtually everything recorded about his healing successes, materialization feats, and possession diagnoses comes from Markides's own participant-observer narrative, without independent instrumentation or medical-record verification comparable to, for example, [Norman Shealy](norman-shealy.md)'s EEG studies of healer Ostad Hadi Parvarandeh in [*Sacred Healing*](../sources/sacred-healing.md). Markides himself is candid throughout that he had "no way of testing the reality" of Daskalos's more extraordinary claims (atomic transmutation, extraterrestrial contact), a limitation this wiki notes without either endorsing or dismissing the underlying claims.

## Connections to Other Thinkers

**Norman Shealy and Barbara Ann Brennan**: Daskalos's three-body model (material/etheric/noetic) and his claim that healing works by manipulating the "etheric double" parallel [Shealy](norman-shealy.md)'s bioelectromagnetic framework and [Brennan](barbara-ann-brennan.md)'s seven-layer Human Energy Field — though Daskalos's cosmology is explicitly esoteric-religious rather than framed, as Shealy's is, as an extension of measurable bioelectric science. See [Subtle Body](../concepts/subtle-body.md) for the cross-tradition comparison table.

**Malachi Martin**: Daskalos's possession cases (Chapter 11) and his benevolent discarnate "helpers" stand in instructive contrast to [Martin](malachi-martin.md)'s possessing entities in [*Hostage to the Devil*](../sources/hostage-to-the-devil.md): both frameworks posit discarnate intelligences that can act on or through the living, but Daskalos treats most such entities (with the partial exception of the "demons" discussed in Chapter 11) as either benevolent guides or morally neutral, evolving elementals rather than the wholly malevolent, strategically deceptive agency Martin describes. See [Karma and Possession (Daskalos)](../concepts/karma-and-possession-daskalos.md).

**Roger Woolger**: Daskalos's account of Karma as an impersonal law requiring the "assumption" and discharge of debts across incarnations parallels [Woolger](roger-woolger.md)'s clinical concept of [past-life complexes](../concepts/past-life-complexes.md) (samskaras) — though Woolger's framework is therapeutic and psychologically bracketed, while Daskalos asserts the karmic mechanism as literal cosmological fact, including specific named "Masters of Karma" governing its administration.
