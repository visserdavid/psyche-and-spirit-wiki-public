# David Bohm

**Summary**: American-British quantum physicist (1917–1992) and philosopher of science; colleague of Einstein at Princeton; developer of the implicate order — a holographic cosmology proposing that the universe is an undivided whole in which apparent separateness is an abstraction from a deeper enfolded order; the philosophical foundation of Brennan's holographic healing model and Talbot's holographic universe.

## Biography

David Joseph Bohm was born in Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania, in 1917. He completed his doctorate under J. Robert Oppenheimer at Berkeley and moved to Princeton, where he worked as a junior colleague of Einstein in the early 1950s. Called before the McCarthy committee and refusing to testify, he lost his Princeton position and was unable to work in the United States thereafter — teaching at the University of São Paulo, the Technion in Israel, and finally Birkbeck College, University of London, where he remained until his death in 1992.

His physics career began with plasma physics (his work on plasmons was considered a landmark), moved to a causal reinterpretation of quantum theory (the "pilot wave" or "hidden variables" interpretation, later confirmed to require non-local connections), and culminated in the implicate order framework developed in the 1970s and 1980s.

In 1980 he published *[Wholeness and the Implicate Order](../sources/wholeness-and-the-implicate-order.md)* — the distillation of his philosophical physics. He worked closely with [J. Krishnamurti](j-krishnamurti.md) on dialogue and consciousness. His work with F. David Peat produced *Science, Order and Creativity* (1987).

## Fragmentation and Wholeness

Before developing the implicate order, Bohm opens *Wholeness and the Implicate Order* with a social and psychological diagnosis: that fragmentary thought — mistaking convenient divisions of thought (nations, professions, disciplines, the separate ego) for divisions in reality itself — is the generative mechanism behind ecological destruction, social conflict, and individual psychological confusion. This motivated his experimental "rheomode," a proposed verb-centred grammar meant to demonstrate that ordinary subject-verb-object syntax itself manufactures the illusion of separate, static things. See [Fragmentation and Wholeness](../concepts/fragmentation-and-wholeness.md) for the full argument.

## The Implicate and Explicate Order

Bohm's central distinction:

**Explicate order**: The observable world of separate objects in space and time. Particles have locations; events have causes; things appear to be separate from one another.

**Implicate order**: The underlying level of reality in which everything is enfolded into everything else. There is no fundamental separateness at this level — what appears as separate objects in the explicate order are aspects of one undivided holomovement. The implicate order is to the explicate order as the encoded interference patterns in a holographic plate are to the projected three-dimensional image.

**The ink-in-glycerine model**: Drop ink into a cylinder of viscous glycerine, turn the cylinder, and the drop spreads and appears to disappear (enfolding into the implicate). Reverse the cylinder, and the drop reconstitutes itself (unfolding into the explicate). The order is preserved, enfolded in the medium, even when not visible.

**Holomovement**: Bohm's preferred term for the flowing, dynamic whole of which both implicate and explicate orders are aspects. The universe is not a collection of things but a continuous movement — like a stream with eddies — in which apparent things are temporary patterns of unfolding.

## Soma-Significance

A key concept in Dossey's *Meaning & Medicine*: Bohm proposed that meaning is the bridge between soma (body/matter) and significance (mind/meaning). Information does not cause physical effects through force; it does so through meaning — the body responds to the significance of what is perceived. This framework grounds Dossey's argument that perceived meanings are primary biological variables.

## Quantum Non-locality and Bell's Theorem

Bohm's earlier work on the EPR (Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen) paradox led him to accept that quantum mechanics requires genuine non-local connections — instantaneous correlations between distant particles that cannot be explained by any signal travelling between them. The 1982 Aspect experiment (Alain Aspect, Paris) confirmed this experimentally. Bohm interpreted non-locality as evidence for the implicate order: separated particles are aspects of one enfolded whole, and their correlation is the explicate expression of their unbroken implicate connection.

## Consciousness and Matter

Bohm rejected the dualistic separation of mind and matter. Rather than claiming mind causes matter or matter causes mind, he proposed that both are "projections of a common ground" — the implicate order itself is neither mental nor physical, and what appears as mind or as matter are two orders of projection from it. He cites Leibniz's monads and Whitehead's "actual occasions" as historical anticipations of this position, and Jean Piaget's developmental psychology as empirical evidence that the child's sense of a world of separate objects existing in fixed space, time, and causal relationships is *constructed* through development rather than simply given. In Chapter 7 of *Wholeness and the Implicate Order*, Bohm cites [Karl Pribram's](karl-pribram.md) holographic model of brain memory storage directly as neuroscientific support for extending the implicate order to consciousness. This position — that consciousness is not produced by matter but shares a common ground with it — constitutes a physical argument for Harman's M-3 metaphysics.

Bohm and Jung were aware of each other's work; F. David Peat explored the Bohm-Jung connection in *Synchronicity: The Bridge Between Matter and Mind* (1987).

## Critique

**Minority position in physics**: Most physicists regard Bohm's implicate order as a metaphysical add-on to quantum mechanics — elegant but empirically indistinguishable from standard interpretations. The pilot-wave interpretation is mathematically equivalent to standard quantum mechanics and makes no additional testable predictions.

**Non-falsifiability**: If the implicate order is prior to spacetime and measurement, its postulation cannot be falsified by physical experiments — it is metaphysics wearing physics clothing.
