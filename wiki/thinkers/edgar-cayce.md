# Edgar Cayce

**Summary**: American psychic and healer (1877–1945), known as the "Sleeping Prophet," whose 15,000 trance readings — two-thirds related to health and illness — formed the foundation of the Association for Research and Enlightenment (A.R.E.) and influenced holistic medicine for generations.

**Sources**:
- [Shealy, C. N. (1999). *Sacred healing: The curing power of energy and spirituality*. Element Books.](../sources/sacred-healing.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-22

## Biography

Edgar Cayce (1877–1945) was born in Hopkinsville, Kentucky. A devout Christian who taught Sunday school throughout his life, he discovered in young adulthood that he could enter a self-induced hypnotic trance and, from that state, diagnose illness and prescribe treatments for patients he had never met and who were often in distant locations. He conducted approximately 15,000 trance "readings" over his lifetime.

Two-thirds of these readings were health-related. The remainder covered topics ranging from past lives and Atlantis to spiritual guidance and astrology. While the metaphysical dimensions of Cayce's readings remain deeply controversial, the health readings attracted the attention of physicians and became the basis of the Association for Research and Enlightenment (A.R.E.), founded in 1931 in Virginia Beach, Virginia, where his archives are preserved.

## The Readings and Their Health Principles

Cayce's health readings proposed:
- That illness was inseparable from attitude, emotion, and spiritual alignment.
- That healing required "attunement" — aligning body, mind, and spirit with Divine law.
- That no drug or treatment alone could heal without the patient's own spiritual engagement.

Representative Cayce readings cited by Shealy:

> "No one can hate his neighbor and not have stomach and liver trouble." *(Reading 4021-1)*

> "Hate, malice, and jealousy create poisons in the minds, souls, and bodies of people." *(Reading 3312-1)*

> "Know that the strength and healing of nature comes through changing the vibrations, attuning to the Divine in living tissue of the body's energies." *(Reading 167-1)*

These statements anticipate the [biography is biology](../concepts/biography-is-biology.md) framework that later thinkers like [Caroline Myss](caroline-myss.md) would develop in explicit energy medicine terms.

## Edgar Cayce and the A.R.E.

The A.R.E. clinic in Phoenix, Arizona, was founded by Drs. Gladys and William McGarry on Cayce's principles. One of Cayce's frequently prescribed treatments — castor oil packs (flannel soaked in castor oil applied to the abdomen with a heating pad) — remains in clinical use at the A.R.E. clinic and has been advocated by [Norman Shealy](norman-shealy.md) for immune function and anti-inflammatory support.

Shealy attended the A.R.E.'s "Week of Attunement" conference in August 1972, which he describes as a peak experience of connectedness with God and the universe — a turning point that launched his sustained investigation of healing.

## Attitude, Attunement, and the Divine

Cayce's philosophy shares with [attitude-based healing](../concepts/attitude-and-health.md) the view that specific emotional states produce specific physiological damage, and that healing requires a fundamental change in consciousness rather than symptom suppression. His Search for God Program — a study group curriculum developed from his readings — outlined what he considered the essentials of spirituality: cooperation, love, faith, virtue, fellowship, patience, balance, and the Christ consciousness.

## Critique

Cayce operated entirely outside scientific and medical structures. His trance readings cannot be reproduced, independently tested, or validated. His claims about Atlantis, reincarnation, and cosmic history have no evidential basis. Even his health readings — which contain genuine insights alongside extraordinary claims — have never been subjected to controlled evaluation.

The A.R.E.'s continued advocacy of some Cayce recommendations (castor oil, specific diets) without modern clinical trials leaves their efficacy unestablished. Mainstream medicine does not recognise trance diagnosis as a valid clinical method.

That said, Cayce's core insight — that attitude, emotion, and spiritual alignment profoundly affect physical health — is partially supported by modern psychoneuroimmunology, though the mechanism and scope differ substantially from his framework.
