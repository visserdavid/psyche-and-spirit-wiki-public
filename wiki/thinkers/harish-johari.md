# Harish Johari

**Summary**: Indian scholar, artist, and spiritual teacher (1934–1999) working in the Hindu-Tantric tradition; his book *Chakras: Energy Centers of Transformation* provides the most detailed traditional account of the chakra system in the wiki.

**Sources**:

## Life and Work

Harish Johari (1934–1999) was born and lived in Haridwar, one of the sacred cities of northern India on the banks of the Ganges. He was a practitioner and scholar of the Hindu-Tantric tradition — a painter, poet, musician, and teacher trained in Tantra, Yoga, Ayurveda, astrology, gemology, and culinary arts. He travelled between India and the West throughout his career, teaching students in the United States and Europe while remaining rooted in the traditional Indian context.

Johari died on 20 August 1999 in Haridwar. The revised and expanded edition of *Chakras: Energy Centers of Transformation* (2000) was completed posthumously by his wife Pratibha Johari, his family, and his students, published by Destiny Books (his American publisher).

## Published Works

Johari published extensively in English through Destiny Books (Rochester, Vermont), making traditional Hindu knowledge accessible to Western readers:

- *Tools for Tantra* (Destiny Books, 1986)
- *Chakras: Energy Centers of Transformation* (Destiny Books, 1987; revised ed. 2000)
- *Breath, Mind, and Consciousness* (Destiny Books, 1989)
- *Leela: The Game of Self Knowledge* (Destiny Books, 1993)

## Approach and Tradition

Johari's work stands apart from most Western writers on chakras in that he writes from within the tradition rather than adapting it. His framework rests on primary Sanskrit sources — the Upanishads, Puranas, and Tantric texts (Rudrayamala Tantra, Lalita Sahasranama, Khat Chakra Nirupana, Kularnava Tantra, Hatha Yogapradipika, Shiva Samhita, and many others) — and on living transmission from teachers. He explicitly aligns himself with the Shaiva-Shakta Tantric tradition: Shiva as supreme consciousness, Shakti as the divine energy that animates all life, Kundalini as Shakti in her dormant-then-awakening form in the human body.

His approach to the chakras is not therapeutic in the Western sense. Where [Caroline Myss](caroline-myss.md) reads the chakras as maps of how biographical stress creates illness, and [Barbara Ann Brennan](barbara-ann-brennan.md) maps them onto the Human Energy Field for clinical healing work, Johari presents the chakras as the primary landscape through which Kundalini energy must travel on its way to liberation (moksha). The behavioral and psychological characteristics of each chakra are understood as descriptions of what consciousness is like when it is still bound to that chakra's desires — not as a healing map but as a progress report on the path toward transcendence.

## Chakras: Energy Centers of Transformation

This is Johari's most widely read work and the primary source in the wiki. It covers:

1. **Kundalini Yoga**: the theoretical foundations of the Yoga system, including the five pranas, the nadi network (especially the Ida/Pingala/Sushumna triad), the five koshas, Svara Yoga (nasal breath science), the complete eight-limbed Ashtanga Yoga path, the three granthis (energetic knots), and the purification practices (Kshata Karmas, mudras, bandhas).

2. **Essentials of Chakras**: each of the seven principal chakras plus the Soma Chakra and Sahasrara, each presented with its full traditional attribute set: element (tattva), yantra shape and colour, bija mantra, carrier animal (vahana), presiding deity, Shakti doorkeeper, associated sense organ and work organ, ruling planet, corresponding loka (cosmic plane), and detailed behavioral characteristics and life-stage associations.

3. **Chakras and Rebirth**: the mechanism by which the dominant chakra at death determines the loka in which the soul dwells between incarnations and the kind of new birth it takes.

4. **Scriptural Appendix**: extensive extracts from Upanishads and Tantras on each limb of Ashtanga Yoga, providing the primary-source grounding for the system's claims.

## Position in the Wiki

Johari is the only traditional Hindu-Tantric voice in the wiki. His work provides:
- The element-deity-mantra-yantra attribute sets that the Western energy-medicine tradition (Myss, Brennan) largely omits
- The full context of Kundalini Yoga within which the chakra system was developed — a context that emphasises liberation rather than health
- The behavioral typology of chakra-dominant consciousness (security/earth, sensuality/water, power/fire, love/air, knowledge/akasha, insight/transcendent) that parallels — but differs structurally from — Myss's biography-is-biology framework

The intercultural tension is significant: Johari warns that playing with chakra energy without proper preparation can create problems. He stresses the necessity of a qualified teacher (guru) for Kundalini practices. This caution is largely absent from Western popular presentations of the chakra system.
