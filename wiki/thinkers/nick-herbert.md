# Nick Herbert

**Summary**: American physicist (Ph.D., Stanford, particle physics background) and popular-science writer whose *Quantum Reality* (1985) is one of the clearest lay expositions of the quantum measurement problem, Bell's theorem, and the interpretive crisis at the heart of physics — read carefully by, and cited in, much of this wiki's mind-body and consciousness literature.

**Sources**:
- [Herbert, N. (1985). *Quantum reality: Beyond the new physics*. Anchor Books/Doubleday.](../sources/quantum-reality.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-07-22

---

Not to be confused with [Herbert Benson](herbert-benson.md), the cardiologist behind *The Relaxation Response* — a coincidence of first names between two otherwise unrelated figures in this wiki.

Herbert worked as an industrial and academic physicist before turning to writing. His involvement with the Fundamental Fysiks Group at the Esalen Institute in the 1970s put him in direct contact with the physicists (Henry Stapp, John Clauser, Fritjof Capra's circle) who were, largely outside mainstream physics's official attention, taking the philosophical implications of quantum mechanics seriously — including its uncomfortable overlap with mysticism and consciousness studies. *Quantum Reality* is the product of that milieu: a systematic, unusually rigorous survey of the "eight quantum realities" (competing interpretations of what quantum mechanics says about the world) built around John Stewart Bell's 1964 theorem.

Herbert's distinguishing trait, relative to other physicist-popularizers cited in this wiki, is his discipline about the boundary between established physics and speculative extrapolation. He states plainly that Bell's theorem proves non-locality — a real, experimentally confirmed feature of reality — while treating claims that this licenses telepathy, macroscopic mind-over-matter, or consciousness-based healing as "rash speculation," not physics. See [Quantum Reality](../sources/quantum-reality.md) for the book's content and [Non-local Consciousness](../concepts/non-local-consciousness.md) for how Dossey and Talbot extend Herbert's physics into contested clinical/parapsychological territory.
