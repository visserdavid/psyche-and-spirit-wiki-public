# Norman Shealy

**Summary**: Harvard-trained neurosurgeon, founder of the American Holistic Medical Association, and pioneer of pain management, holistic health, and electrotherapy who collaborated with Caroline Myss for over a decade and spent thirty years documenting sacred healing.

## Biography

C. Norman Shealy, M.D., Ph.D., is a Harvard-educated neurosurgeon who became one of the founding figures of holistic medicine in the United States. He founded the Shealy Institute for Comprehensive Health Care in Springfield, Missouri, and was a founding president of the American Holistic Medical Association. He also served as Research and Clinical Professor of Psychology at the Forest Institute of Professional Psychology. He is the author of *Miracles Do Happen* and co-author (with Caroline Myss) of *The Creation of Health* (Stillpoint, 1988; revised 1993).

## Collaboration with Caroline Myss

Shealy met [Caroline Myss](caroline-myss.md) in spring 1984 at a conference. After she approached him claiming she could diagnose illness remotely, he began testing her with his patients. Her first evaluation — identifying esophageal cancer and the emotional antecedents (an addict who couldn't speak his truth, whose words "froze in his throat") — convinced him to pursue a sustained research collaboration.

Over ten years, Shealy provided Myss with patient names and ages, and she conducted intuitive evaluations without any clinical information. Her descriptions of physical illness and corresponding emotional/biographical patterns were checked against diagnoses and patient histories. The patterns Shealy and Myss identified across hundreds of cases — linking specific stress types to specific diseases — were published in [*The Creation of Health*](../sources/creation-of-health.md) (Stillpoint, 1988; revised 1993).

Shealy also developed healing programs based on Myss's readings. A notable case is Peter, a young man Myss identified as HIV-positive when two prior tests had been negative. Shealy designed a regimen of diet, exercise, castor oil packs, and psychotherapy; Peter tested HIV-negative six weeks later and remained so at the time of writing.

## Contributions to Energy Medicine

Shealy represents the institutional bridge between conventional medicine and [energy medicine](../concepts/energy-anatomy.md). His involvement gave Myss's work clinical credibility and provided a framework for understanding how energetic dysfunction could be identified and addressed before illness became fully manifest.

He also developed training programs in medical intuition, envisioning a future in which intuitives would work as members of health care teams alongside physicians. The farm at Springfield, Missouri, was proposed as a site for a "science of intuition" residency program.

## Key Concepts

Shealy's clinical perspective led Myss to articulate several principles:

- **Stages of illness development**: The body produces warning signals (fatigue, depression, lethargy) before illness manifests. These pre-illness states are detectable at the energy level even when clinical tests show nothing.
- **The dentist case**: A patient Shealy was treating developed pancreatic cancer after years of feeling trapped in his profession. His energy readings showed burdened responsibility and emotional exclusion. Despite advice, the man made no changes and died — illustrating that healing requires action, not information alone.
- **Power and treatment**: Shealy observed that treatments fail when patients have no power in their own healing process. His work with a depressed woman with neck pain led him to recognize that no therapy could succeed if the patient's power base remained entirely vested in others.

## Sacred Healing and the Investigation of Healers

In *Sacred Healing* (1999), Shealy documents thirty years of investigating spiritual healers — a quest that began in 1972 with his introduction to Olga Worrall and his attendance at the A.R.E. Week of Attunement. Key findings:

- He personally observed Philippine psychic surgeons (Tony Santiago, Tony Agpaou) and was unable to detect fraud despite close monitoring.
- He collected over one hundred medically documented cases of healing where conventional medicine offered no explanation.
- His most extensively documented healer was Ostad Hadi Parvarandeh, an Iranian diplomat who healed thousands without charge. Shealy's laboratory measurements of Parvarandeh's effects include EEG changes in patients at 40–100 feet distance, cardiac output changes, altered DHEA levels, and changes in the hydrogen-bonding structure of water.

Shealy concludes from this investigation that healing originates from a Divine Source — the healer is a channel, not the origin — and that illness stems from imbalance in spirit.

This conclusion parallels the Cypriot mystic [Daskalos](daskalos.md) (Stylianos Atteshlis), documented in [*The Magus of Strovolos*](../sources/magus-of-strovolos.md), who likewise refused payment for healing and framed his own abilities as channeling rather than personal power — though Shealy's investigation is grounded in instrumented measurement (EEG, DHEA levels), where Markides's account of Daskalos rests on participant-observer narrative alone.

## Bioelectromagnetism and Electrotherapy

Shealy's [(Shealy, 1999)](../sources/sacred-healing.md) work integrates the science of [bioelectromagnetism](../concepts/bioelectromagnetism.md) — particularly [Robert Becker](robert-becker.md)'s research — with clinical electrotherapy. Key developments:

- Shealy introduced the Dorsal Column Stimulator (implanted electrical device for cancer pain) in April 1967.
- He developed and championed the surface TENS (Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation) device — arguing skin stimulation is 1,000 times safer than implanted stimulation.
- He introduced Cranial Electrical Stimulation (CES/Liss device) as a treatment for depression, insomnia, and jet lag, achieving 85% relief of depression in his clinical programme.
- He brought Microwave Resonance Therapy (GigaTENS, 52–78 GHz) from Ukraine to the USA, claiming dramatic results for pain, addiction, and arthritis.

See [Electrotherapy](../concepts/electrotherapy.md) for the full framework.

## DHEA and Life Energy

Shealy proposes [DHEA](../concepts/dhea-and-vitality.md) (dehydroepiandrosterone) as a measurable biochemical marker of life energy — a "chemical battery" reflecting vitality reserves. Low DHEA is the hallmark of his concept of [Electromagnetic Dysthymia](../concepts/electromagnetic-dysthymia.md) (EMD) — a syndrome of cumulative stress overload underlying most modern chronic illness.

## Attitude and the Divine Source

Shealy [(Shealy, 1999)](../sources/sacred-healing.md) argues that attitude is the single most important health variable. He distinguishes spirituality (direct communion with God) from religion (institutional dogma) and argues the former — expressed through forgiveness, love, tolerance, serenity, and faith — is the foundation of health. See [Attitude and Health](../concepts/attitude-and-health.md).

## Living Bliss: Conscientious Psychology and the Five Sacred Rings

In *Living Bliss* (2014), Shealy synthesises fifty years of clinical work into his final theoretical framework. The book contains three interlocking contributions.

**Conscientious Psychology** is Shealy's culminating framework: after treating 30,000 patients, he concluded that the underlying failure in most cases was a deficit of conscientiousness — the personality trait of being organized, purposeful, and self-responsible. He grounds this in the *Longevity Project* (Friedman & Martin, 2011), the culmination of an 80-year study of 1,500 people, which found conscientiousness to be the strongest personality predictor of longevity. Shealy reports that 97% of patients did not consistently follow four basic health habits (weight, non-smoking, diet, exercise) — not for lack of knowledge, but for lack of the underlying trait. See [Conscientious Psychology](../concepts/conscientious-psychology.md).

**The Five Sacred Rings** are five circuits of acupuncture points Shealy developed between 1994 and 2012, each paired with an essential oil blend:
- **Ring Fire**: raises DHEA 60–250%; treats arthritis, depression, back pain
- **Ring Air**: raises neurotensin 600% → oxytocin; treats depression, autism, PTSD, ADHD
- **Ring Water**: normalises aldosterone; treats fluid retention and edema
- **Ring Earth**: stimulates calcitonin; treats pain (40–60× morphine potency), addiction, phobias
- **Ring Crystal**: reduces free radicals 85%; supports cell regeneration

Each ring can be activated via GHz stimulation (54–78 GHz), the corresponding Bliss Oil blend (30-second application), a CD recording, or massage/tapping. See [Five Sacred Rings](../concepts/five-sacred-rings.md).

**Oxytocin and mental health**: Shealy argues that oxytocin deficiency — established during a critical window from birth to age seven — is the biochemical root of depression, ADHD, autism, addiction, OCD, and borderline personality. The neurochemical foundation of conscientiousness is a well-functioning oxytocin system, produced by adequate early nurturing. See [Oxytocin and Health](../concepts/oxytocin-and-health.md).

**Telomere research**: Shealy's personal telomere measurements (2007–2014) combined with a 30-person pilot showed that the Five Bliss Oils, applied daily, produced a 17.36% telomere increase over seven years against an expected 25% decline — a swing of 42.36%. Independent replication has not been published.

**Institutional legacy**: Shealy endowed the Mary Charlotte Bayles Shealy Chair in Conscientious Psychology at Missouri State University, funded the research programme at Holos Institutes Health, and established Holos University Graduate Seminary (HUGS) as an accredited graduate programme in holistic medicine. He co-founded the American Holistic Medical Association (AHMA) in 1978 with thirty-six other physicians — the organisation that gave credibility to holistic medicine as a medical specialty.

**Caroline Myss** contributed the foreword to *Living Bliss*, identifying Shealy's "conscientiousness" as the practical operationalization of the personal responsibility principle she had taught for three decades.

## Critique

Shealy's collaboration with Myss is more documented than most in the field of energy medicine, but the evidence base remains primarily anecdotal — case studies rather than controlled trials. Critics in evidence-based medicine point out that dramatic recoveries like Peter's may reflect testing errors, spontaneous remission, or confounds rather than the efficacy of energy-based diagnosis and treatment. Shealy himself, despite his conventional credentials, is a committed advocate for views that mainstream medicine considers unproven.

His electrotherapy claims — particularly for GigaTENS and CES — are based on clinical observation and unpublished Ukrainian research, not on controlled Western trials. His DHEA framework is conceptually interesting but not validated. His healer documentation, while remarkable, does not meet the methodological standards of clinical evidence.
