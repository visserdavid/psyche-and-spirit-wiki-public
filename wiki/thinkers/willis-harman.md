# Willis Harman

**Summary**: American systems scientist, futurist, and social philosopher (1918–1997); professor of engineering sciences at Stanford University; president of the Institute of Noetic Sciences (IONS); author of *Global Mind Change* (1988); his three-metaphysics taxonomy (M-1/M-2/M-3) provides the philosophical foundation for Brennan's holographic healing model; his Copernican-revolution analogy for the coming consciousness paradigm shift parallels Ring's Omega Hypothesis; and he identified the Inner Self Helper in multiple personality disorder research as a clinical rediscovery of the Vedantic *Atman*.

## Biography

Willis Whitney Harman was born in 1918. He received his BS, MS, and PhD in electrical engineering from the University of Washington and the University of Florida, and joined Stanford University's engineering faculty in 1952. In the 1960s he directed Stanford Research Institute's (SRI) Educational Policy Research Center.

From 1975 to 1977, Harman served as a member of the President's Commission on World Hunger. He joined the Institute of Noetic Sciences (IONS) — founded by Apollo 14 astronaut Edgar Mitchell — and became its president in 1977, a position he held until his death on January 30, 1997. Under his leadership, IONS became the leading research organisation for the scientific study of consciousness.

## Three Eras of Metaphysics (M-1 / M-2 / M-3)

In *Global Mind Change: The Promise of the Last Years of the Twentieth Century* (Knowledge Systems, 1988), Harman proposed that the history of Western thought can be organised into three fundamental metaphysical positions concerning the relationship between mind and matter:

**M-1 (Materialistic Monism)**: Only matter is ultimately real; mind (consciousness, experience) is produced by matter and has no independent existence. This is the default assumption of conventional science and mainstream medicine.

**M-2 (Dualism)**: Both mind and matter are real but separate; they interact but are fundamentally distinct substances. This is the Cartesian position.

**M-3 (Transcendental Monism)**: Consciousness is the fundamental reality; matter is a manifestation within consciousness rather than consciousness being a manifestation of matter. This is the position of most mystical traditions and of Harman's own framework.

[Barbara Ann Brennan](barbara-ann-brennan.md) explicitly adopts Harman's M-3 metaphysics as the philosophical foundation of her [holographic healing model](../concepts/holographic-healing-model.md), arguing that healing requires working from the M-3 premise that consciousness is primary.

## The Inner Self Helper Observation

In *Fire in the Soul*, [Joan Borysenko](joan-borysenko.md) attributes to Harman the observation that the **Inner Self Helper** — the unique alter discovered by psychiatrist Ralph Allison within multiple personality disorder (MPD) systems — corresponds exactly to descriptions of the Self (*Atman*) in Indian philosophical texts.

The Inner Self Helper, unlike all other alters, exhibits no fear, guilt, hatred, or depression; has access to the complete history of the system; acts as a "conduit for God's love"; and is described by Allison as having existed before birth and continuing after death. Harman's observation was that this clinical discovery, arrived at through American psychiatric practice, maps directly onto what Vedantic philosophy has always taught about the *Atman* — the unchanging, undamaged core of consciousness beneath all the biographical fragmentation the personality can undergo.

## The Book: *Global Mind Change* (1988)

*Global Mind Change: The Promise of the Last Years of the Twentieth Century* (Knowledge Systems, 1988; second edition Berrett-Koehler, 1998) is Harman's central work. Its argument:

1. Western civilisation is undergoing a [paradigm shift of Copernican scale](../concepts/consciousness-paradigm-shift.md) — from matter as fundamental (M-1) to consciousness as fundamental (M-3)
2. The shift is evidenced by anomalies within M-1 science: PNI, placebo, firewalking, multiple personality, parapsychology, and wisdom traditions — all resist M-1 explanation
3. [Unconscious belief systems](../concepts/unconscious-belief-systems.md) make paradigm-violating evidence invisible to those whose worldview depends on the current paradigm — as historically documented with meteorites, wave theory, and hypnosis surgery
4. The civilisation-level consequences of completing the shift include transformed medicine, education, economics, and the eventual delegitimating of war

Key chapters:
- Chapter 2: The M-1/M-2/M-3 taxonomy; the "ten unspoken assumptions" of conventional science
- Chapter 3: Mind, health and healing (PNI, placebo, biofeedback, Simonton imagery); attention and volition (Kornhuber, Hilgard's hidden observer); Sheldrake's morphic resonance
- Chapter 4: Transpersonal psychology — MPD, the Inner Self Helper, creativity and unconscious knowing, exceptional capabilities, ESP, channelling
- Chapters 5–6: The global macroproblem and civilisational transformation

## Relationship to the Wiki's Frameworks

**[Brennan](barbara-ann-brennan.md)**: Harman's M-1/M-2/M-3 framework is the explicit theoretical foundation of Brennan's holographic healing model. The claim that healing operates "from the inside out" — that consciousness shapes matter rather than the reverse — requires M-3 metaphysics.

**[Dossey](larry-dossey.md)**: Dossey's Era III medicine (non-local healing; prayer at a distance) also rests on M-3 metaphysics. Harman and Dossey can be seen as addressing the same paradigm shift from complementary positions.

**[Ring](kenneth-ring.md)**: Ring's [Omega Hypothesis](../concepts/omega-hypothesis.md) and Harman's global mind change thesis converge: both argue that individual consciousness shifts aggregate into species-level transformation; both invoke Teilhard de Chardin's Omega Point and Sheldrake's morphic resonance as the mechanism.

**[Borysenko](joan-borysenko.md)**: The Inner Self Helper passage in *Fire in the Soul* draws on Harman's cross-cultural synthesis to argue that even the most extreme trauma (MPD from severe childhood abuse) cannot damage the Self — the core remains intact, which is the foundation of the [crisis-as-initiation](../concepts/crisis-as-initiation.md) argument.

## Critique

**M-3 is a metaphysical assertion**: The claim that consciousness is fundamental rather than matter is not falsifiable by conventional scientific methods — which operate from M-1 premises. Harman was aware of this and argued that the choice of metaphysical framework is itself a practical decision, not a purely logical one; M-3 enables kinds of inquiry that M-1 forecloses.

**IONS research quality**: While IONS has funded important work in the science of consciousness, it has also supported research on phenomena (telepathy, remote viewing) that remains highly controversial in mainstream science.
